# Brief Stress-Tester -- Marketing Brief

## 1. App Name Recommendation

**Keep "Brief Stress-Tester."** It does three things no alternative does: it tells you exactly what it does, it uses language lawyers already use ("stress-test" is a term of art in litigation prep), and it's instantly googlable. Clever names trade clarity for cleverness.

Alternatives considered and rejected:

| Name | Rationale | Why not |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Adversary | Strong, single-word, ominous | Too generic; conflicts with existing legal AI tools |
| BriefBreaker | Memorable, implies destruction-testing | Negative connotation -- sounds like it breaks your brief, not strengthens it |
| RedBrief | Echoes "red team" -- security people get it | Lawyers are not security people; the metaphor doesn't land |

**Verdict:** Ship as Brief Stress-Tester. Rename later only if user research says otherwise.

## 5. How It Works

### Step 1: Upload
Paste your brief and set the jurisdiction. That's it. No formatting, no templates, no 30-minute onboarding call.

### Step 2: Adversarial Rounds
Three AI agents attack, defend, and judge every claim in your brief across multiple rounds -- each round digging deeper than the last.

### Step 3: Fix What Matters
Get a severity-ranked list of vulnerabilities with specific, actionable fixes. Start with critical, work down.

---

## 6. Seven Features

### 1. See Your Argument's Attack Surface
**Argument Graph** -- A visual map of every claim, every piece of evidence, and every connection between them. Spot structural weaknesses you'd never catch reading linearly.

### 2. Deeper Than a Single Pass
**Multi-Round Debate** -- Each round builds on the last. The Attacker gets smarter. The Defender gets sharper. By round three, only real vulnerabilities survive.

### 3. Know What to Fix First
**Severity Scoring** -- Every finding ranked critical to low with confidence percentages. Stop wasting time on nitpicks when a fatal flaw is on page twelve.

### 4. Show Your Work
**Full Audit Trail** -- Every agent action logged, every argument traced. Hand the report to a partner and they can verify every finding in minutes.

### 5. Your Jurisdiction, Your Rules
**Jurisdiction-Aware Analysis** -- Set your jurisdiction once. The agents adapt their standards of review, burden analysis, and procedural requirements automatically.

### 6. Don't Just Find Problems
**Suggested Fixes** -- Each vulnerability comes with a concrete recommendation. Not "strengthen this argument" -- specific language, missing evidence, alternative framings.

### 7. No More Citation Bluffs
**Citation Verification** -- Flags unsupported assertions, misapplied precedents, and citation gaps before opposing counsel turns them into ammunition on the record.

---

## 7. Why Adversarial Testing

Every lawyer reviews their own brief. Almost none subject it to systematic adversarial pressure before filing. That is the equivalent of a boxer who trains alone and never spars. In federal courts, 36% of summary judgment motions are denied -- many on arguments that a ten-minute stress-test would have caught. The cost of finding a vulnerability in court is measured in months and six figures. The cost of finding it here is two minutes.
