# Research Insights — job market prediction

## Papers Reviewed

### [1] GPTs are GPTs: An Early Look at the Labor Market Impact Potential of Large Language Models (2023, 514 citations)
- **Authors:** Tyna Eloundou, Sam Manning, Pamela Mishkin, Daniel Rock
- **Relevance:** high
- **Domain:** AI/ML hiring
- **Key Finding:** LLMs could affect 80% of U.S. workforce tasks, with about 15% of tasks completed significantly faster; LLM-powered software amplifies impact to 47–56% of tasks.
- **Actionable Insight:** Job board aggregators should incorporate LLM‑skill alignment metrics to identify roles at risk or enhanced by AI, and highlight remote‑friendly roles that may be more susceptible to automation.
- **Source:** https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/5501d00310b06e00351295529498cc684187148d

### [2] An Enhanced Neural Network Approach to Person-Job Fit in Talent Recruitment (2020, 103 citations)
- **Authors:** Chuan Qin, Hengshu Zhu, Tong Xu, Chen Zhu, Chao Ma, Enhong Chen, Hui Xiong
- **Relevance:** high
- **Domain:** recruitment tech
- **Key Finding:** The TAPJFNN framework reduces dependence on manual labor and provides interpretable matching between job requirements and candidate experiences using hierarchical topic‑based attention.
- **Actionable Insight:** Implement similar neural matching models to improve candidate‑job matching accuracy, using attention mechanisms to explain why a candidate fits a role, thereby increasing user trust and conversion.
- **Source:** https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/6c17c2cd03dcea90ff33418e9d2a80ddad600922

### [3] Talent Demand-Supply Joint Prediction with Dynamic Heterogeneous Graph Enhanced Meta‑Learning (2022, 20 citations)
- **Authors:** Zhuoning Guo, Hao Liu, Le Zhang, Qi Zhang, Hengshu Zhu, Hui Xiong
- **Relevance:** high
- **Domain:** recruitment tech
- **Key Finding:** The DH‑GEM framework captures the interconnection between demand and supply sequences among companies and positions, enabling fine‑grained joint prediction and improving long‑tail forecasting.
- **Actionable Insight:** Use dynamic heterogeneous graphs to model job‑market fluctuations, enabling predictive analytics for job seekers and employers about future skill demands and regional imbalances.
- **Source:** https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/b130687bf2632da16973bf9755e81e061e0ca504

### [4] Predictive Algorithms in the Delivery of Public Employment Services (2021, 22 citations)
- **Authors:** John L. Körtner, G. Bonoli
- **Relevance:** medium
- **Domain:** recruitment tech / EU employment
- **Key Finding:** Predictive algorithms are used in public employment services for profiling, targeting, and matching; algorithmic fairness and caseworker interaction are critical considerations.
- **Actionable Insight:** Adopt similar predictive profiling for job seekers on the platform, but ensure fairness and transparency to avoid bias; consider hybrid human‑AI decision support to align with EU regulatory expectations.
- **Source:** https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/56206add8fc115e1c2e4529555a338b87f2b4e5c

### [5] A New Spatial Hedonic Equilibrium in the Emerging Work‑from‑Home Economy? (2021, 105 citations)
- **Authors:** J. Brueckner, Matthew E. Kahn, Gary C. Lin
- **Relevance:** medium
- **Domain:** remote work
- **Key Finding:** WFH leads to relocation to cheaper metro areas, flattening intracity house‑price gradients and reducing the premium for job proximity.
- **Actionable Insight:** Track geographic shifts in remote job postings and adjust regional job recommendations accordingly; anticipate increased remote job supply in lower‑cost regions and tailor aggregation strategies to capture emerging remote hubs.
- **Source:** https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/604328fb36aabf77b6e376890dbe02bd6a2a806a

5. **Track remote‑work adoption across EU regions** and adjust job‑aggregation strategies to capture emerging remote hubs, ensuring comprehensive coverage of distributed opportunities.
