# Deep Neural Networks for Real-Time Customer Retention Forecasting in Digital Banking Platforms

**Authors.** Asif Perwej, B. R. Supreeth, Pooja Chopra, Aabid Mushtaq Najar, G. Manikandan, Mohit Tiwari

**Venue / year.** 2025 Tenth International Conference on Science Technology Engineering and Mathematics (ICONSTEM) · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/7ab65512596bf1053728e3e8f48ac42040f85cc2)

**Abstract.**

The growing digitalization of banking services has complicated the situation with customer loyalty in highly competitive online services. Conventional predictive systems can be hard to adapt to the dynamism of customer behavior and are also not usually dynamically adaptive. To overcome these shortcomings, a deep neural network architecture that comprises gated recurrent units (GRU) and attention models isproposed to forecast customer retention in a real-time scenario. This model operates a streaming transactional and behavioral data processing pipeline based on Apache Spark and TensorFlow Serving, providing an opportunity to constantly update predictions. The experimental findings indicate 91% accuracy, 0.87 F1-score, AUC-ROC of 0.94, with a median inference latency of 120 milliseconds. Operational analysis also shows that customer retention has risen by 20.6% and false churn warnings have been cut by half. The given framework can provide predictive intelligence on proactive decision-making in the banking ecosystem that is scalable, interpretable, and low-latency.
