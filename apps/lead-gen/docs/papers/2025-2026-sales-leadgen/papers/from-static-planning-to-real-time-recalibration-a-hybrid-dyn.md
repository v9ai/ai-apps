# From Static Planning to Real-Time Recalibration: A Hybrid Dynamic Marketing Mix Framework for Marketing Intelligence Systems

**Authors.** Marwen Ayadi

**Venue / year.** 2026

**Links.** [DOI](https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6359978) · [source](https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6359978)

**Abstract.**

Purpose-This article develops a managerial decision-support framework that reconceptualizes the traditional 4Ps marketing mix as a Hybrid Dynamic Marketing Mix (HDMM) suited to data-intensive, analytics-enabled planning environments characterized by real-time feedback and omnichannel integration. Design/methodology/approach-The study adopts a structured conceptual development approach (Jaakkola, 2020), synthesizing marketing mix theory, marketing analytics research, digital transformation literature, and decision architecture scholarship to construct an execution-oriented model. The framework is formalized through theoretically derived propositions and an integrative conceptual model. Findings-The article introduces HDMM as a recursive planning and execution architecture characterized by embedded hybrid analytics, synchronized 4Ps recalibration, adaptive latency reduction, and continuous performance feedback integration. Four measurable constructs-adaptive latency, synchronization intensity, analytical embedding depth, and feedback velocity-translate the framework into empirically testable dimensions. Research limitations/implications-As a conceptual paper, the framework requires empirical validation across industries with varying levels of environmental volatility. Future research may examine longitudinal performance effects, governance boundaries, and trust implications associated with accelerated adaptive systems. Practical implications-HDMM provides managers with a systematic blueprint for redesigning marketing planning processes in analytics-intensive environments. Rather than expanding the 4Ps, the framework embeds real-time intelligence within existing mix elements, enabling synchronized decision-making and improved strategic responsiveness. 1 Originality/value-The study advances marketing intelligence and planning research by re-architecting the 4Ps from a static planning template into a hybrid adaptive execution system. It contributes a structurally grounded alternative to automation-centric interpretations of digital marketing transformation.
