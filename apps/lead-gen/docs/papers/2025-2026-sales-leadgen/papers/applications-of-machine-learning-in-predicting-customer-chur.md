# Applications of Machine Learning in Predicting Customer Churn in Telecommunications: A Review Analysis

**Authors.** Ajay Dasari, Venkata Kishore Chilakapati, Srikanth Reddy Keshireddy, Venkata Teja Nagumotu, Harsha Vardhan Reddy Kavuluri, Akhil Kumar Pathani

**Venue / year.** European Journal of Applied Science, Engineering and Technology · 2026

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/852e3e65c1cdaa49da18f069883d6590935f77c6)

**Abstract.**

The telecommunication industry is rapidly expanding, thereby raising the competition thereby rendering customer retention an issue of major concern to the service providers. Customer churn loss of subscribers directly influences revenue and market share, and predictive models that recognize at-risk customers are necessary. Machine learning (ML) churn prediction has been around for a while, and this article covers its background and present methods.   In order to classify big datasets, supervised learning techniques like as Decision Trees, Random Forests, Support Vector Machines, and Gradient Boosting are frequently employed. Deep learning models, particularly Convolutional Neural Networks, are utilized for pattern discovery in these datasets. Behavioral segmentation and analysis is aided by unsupervised learning, in particular, clustering. The service quality, customer satisfaction, pricing, and repurchase possibility are the primary churn factors. The research examines publicly available datasets, including PAKDD 2006, KDD Cup 2009, Cell2Cell, CrowdAnalytix, and IBM Telco Customer Churn, and this sets a standard to evaluate the model. Recent research shows that as compared to standard models, ensemble and deep learning methods consistently achieve better results in terms of accuracy, recall, and F1-score.  Prolonged difficulties include data mismatch, overfitting, and inadequate cross-dataset validation. This review highlights that ML-based analytics have the potential to be used to support proactive retention policies and make informed decisions in telecom operators.
