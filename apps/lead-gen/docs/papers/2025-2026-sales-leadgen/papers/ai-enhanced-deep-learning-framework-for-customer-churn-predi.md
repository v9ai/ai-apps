# AI-Enhanced Deep Learning Framework for Customer Churn Prediction In Indonesian Mobile Commerce Platforms

**Authors.** S. Rajagukguk, Faisal Binsar

**Venue / year.** 2025 5th International Conference of Science and Information Technology in Smart Administration (ICSINTESA) · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/1f3b4f1dcf16b053f329a5ad70237b380c89f977)

**Abstract.**

The rapid expansion of Indonesia’s mobile commerce (m-commerce) sector, valued at USD 79.84 billion in 2024, faces significant challenges in customer retention due to intense market competition and evolving consumer behaviors. This study presents an innovative deep neural network (DNN) framework integrated with advanced feature engineering techniques to predict customer churn in Indonesian m-commerce platforms. We collected comprehensive behavioral and demographic data from 5,127 m-commerce users across major platforms through structured surveys and anonymized API data. Our methodology employs a multi-layered DNN architecture with dropout regularization and batch normalization, achieving superior performance metrics compared to traditional machine learning approaches. The proposed model demonstrates exceptional predictive capability with an AUC-ROC score of 0.947, precision of 89.3%, recall of 91.7%, and F1-score of 90.5%. Furthermore, the model successfully identified key churn predictors including transaction frequency decline, customer service interaction patterns, and promotional engagement rates. Implementation of proactive retention strategies based on our predictions resulted in a 23.8% reduction in churn rates during the validation period. These findings provide actionable insights for m-commerce platforms to enhance customer retention strategies and optimize revenue generation in Indonesia’s competitive digital marketplace.
