# Predictive Analytics System for Customer Churn in Telecom Using Artificial Intelligence Approaches

**Authors.** Praveen Hegde, Misha Shah

**Venue / year.** International Workshop on Artificial Intelligence and Cognition · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/55acefeacb15255f016bd5a17ec2ee3ba0dbcf6e)

**Abstract.**

Customer churn prediction in telecom involves identifying consumers likely to discontinue their services with a telecom provider. The goal is to enable proactive retention strategies by predicting churn risks and addressing potential issues before customers leave. This research examines the Telco Customer Churn dataset containing 7,043 customer records, to analyze key predictive features through the lens of several ML models, such as LR: Logistic Regression, DT: Decision Tree, GB: Gradient Boosting, ANN: Artificial Neural Network, XGB: eXtreme Gradient Boosting. The dataset is split into training and testing subsets, followed by feature refinement. Feature refinement involved preprocessing steps such as handling missing values, label encoding of categorical variables, normalization of continuous features using Min-Max scaling, and addressing class imbalance with Synthetic Minority Over-sampling Technique (SMOTE). Among the models, XGBoost outperforms with an accuracy of 96.71 %, precision of 97.11 %, recall of $\mathbf{9 2. 6 2 \%}$, and an $\mathbf{F 1}$-score of $\mathbf{9 7. 0 3 \%}$. The model's remarkable ability to differentiate between churn and non-churn classes is further shown by its AUC of 0.9912. Comparative analysis highlights XGBoost's reliability and robustness in customer churn prediction, demonstrating its potential to enhance decision-making for customer retention strategies in the telecommunications industry.
