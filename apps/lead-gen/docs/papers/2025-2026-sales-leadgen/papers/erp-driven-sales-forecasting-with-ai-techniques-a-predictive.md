# ERP-Driven Sales Forecasting with AI Techniques: A Predictive Analytics Approach

**Authors.** Gopikrishna Maddali, Saurabh A. Pahune, Mahi Ratan Reddy Deva, Pushpalika Chatterjee

**Venue / year.** CSI International Symposium on Artificial Intelligence and Signal Processing · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/59163bf6ff5bcfc741da258129a644e9fc174107)

**Abstract.**

Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) systems are encircled by a robust sales forecast that will probably hold equal significance in corporate strategy formulation, inventory management, and resource maintenance. The author is mostly concerned with the use of different ML methods and predictive analytics in ERP systems in sales forecasting. The key subject of this work is to present a method of building a sound sales forecasting model with the use of ML. The authors utilize a dataset of real-life retail sales, which contains past data of transactions. An extensive data pre-processing pipeline is defined and includes the tasks of addressing the lack of data, feature normalization, and label encoding. The solution to sales prediction is a deep learning hybrid model, which applies both Gated Recurrent Units (GRU) and Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM). The efficacy of the model is assessed by applying standard ML techniques such as Support Vector Machine and Random Forest. The LSTMs with GRUs model with a R2 of 98.05, RMSE of 17.32 and an MAE of 13.27 outperforms all the other models with the experimental data. The findings demonstrate how a series of models founded on DL can enhance the degree of sales forecasting through the introduction of intricate time-related relationships. The implementation of the ML methods has significantly enhanced the sales prediction accuracy following the implementation of the ERP systems.
