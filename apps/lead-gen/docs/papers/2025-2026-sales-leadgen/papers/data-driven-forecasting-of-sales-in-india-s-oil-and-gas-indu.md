# Data Driven Forecasting of Sales in India's Oil and Gas Industry Using Machine Learning Techniques

**Authors.** Dipika Gupta, Punit Gupta

**Venue / year.** International Conference on Computational Intelligence and Communication Networks · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/eda8bac7e7157c71cf7d9df0a43ad41181e0f6a5)

**Abstract.**

Accurate forecasting of institutional sales is critical for strategic planning and operational efficiency in India's dynamic oil and gas industry. This paper provides a comprehensive review and comparative evaluation of machine learning mod-els-including Random Forest, XGBoost, Gradient Boosting, Artificial Neural Networks, and advanced hybrid techniquesapplied to the prediction of institutional sales within the sector. The research highlights the increasing adoption of ensemble and deep learning approaches, which consistently outperform traditional time-series methods in forecasting accuracy and business impact. The analysis draws on case studies and datasets reflecting Indian market conditions, while also referencing advancements from emerging and global markets. Key factors examined include model interpretability, integration of structured and unstructured data, and real-world business outcomes such as improved inventory control, enhanced customer engagement, and risk mitigation. The study demonstrates that hybrid and ensemble models are particularly effective in addressing the volatility and complexity inherent to institutional sales, offering actionable guidance for practitioners and decisionmakers striving to optimize sales performance in India's oil and gas industry.
