# Predicting Telecom Customer Churn with Combined Machine Learning Models

**Authors.** Sayantan Sinha, Saket Maheshwari, Rick Chatterjee, Puneet Sharma, Saksham Saxena, Harsh Rajoria

**Venue / year.** 2025 IEEE International Conference on Emerging Trends in Computing and Communication (ETCOM) · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/c609b972b6e1090d483daa33e24217bb2f7ee586)

**Abstract.**

One of the biggest challenges facing telecom companies in an effort to build customer retention and tailor services according to customers' needs is the prediction of customer turnover. This paper compares 21 customer-related attributes by which machine learning models used to predict telecom customer attrition are forecasted. The four models that we assess are Support Vector Machines (SVM), Random Forest, Logistic Regression, and a hybrid multimodel method using Artificial Neural Networks (ANN). These performance metrics involve accuracy, precision, recall, and the F1-score. From the outcome, it was found that a hybrid model of ANN could attain an accuracy score of 85%, but more conventional models such as Random Forest and Logistic Regression were generating good scores. This study will provide telecommunication firms with the vital information needed in the strategy of customer retention through an understanding of the importance of feature selection and possibilities for integrating multiple machine learning models in churn prediction.
