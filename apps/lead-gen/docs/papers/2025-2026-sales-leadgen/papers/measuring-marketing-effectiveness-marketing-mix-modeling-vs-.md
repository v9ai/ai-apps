# Measuring Marketing Effectiveness: Marketing Mix Modeling vs. Multi-Touch Attribution

**Authors.** GabrielBerger

**Venue / year.** Digital Repository (National Repository of Grey Literature) · 2026

**Links.** [source](http://www.nusl.cz/ntk/nusl-693558)

**Abstract.**

The thesis compares two approaches that companies use to measure the impact of marketing: attribution in Google Analytics 4 (MTA) and Marketing Mix Modeling (MMM). The aim of the thesis is to explain why these methods often produce different results and how they can influence marketing budget decision-making. The theoretical part explains the fundamentals of attribution models and describes how MMM works with data, time series, and external factors. The practical part uses weekly data from an e-commerce company and builds a Bayesian MMM to evaluate the contribution of individual marketing channels. The results show that MTA and MMM assign revenue to channels differently and can lead to different investment recommendations. In the conclusion, the thesis provides recommendations on how to use these methods sensibly in practice and what to consider when interpreting their results.
