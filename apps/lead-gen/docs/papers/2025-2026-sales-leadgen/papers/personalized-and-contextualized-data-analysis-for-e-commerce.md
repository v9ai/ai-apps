# Personalized and Contextualized Data Analysis for E-Commerce Customer Retention Improvement With Bi-LSTM Churn Prediction

**Authors.** Qing Wei

**Venue / year.** IEEE transactions on consumer electronics · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/6070da2d52019421961c3cd132f3605eb243f06c)

**Abstract.**

This study examines the issue of customer churn in the context of e-commerce businesses. Customer churn, or losing customers, poses a significant challenge for these businesses as they strive to retain a loyal customer base. This research paper delves into enhancing E-commerce customer retention through personalized and contextualized data analysis. This research paper introduces a novel method for predicting customer churn in the e-commerce industry. The proposed approach utilizes a Bidirectional Long Short-Term Memory (Bi-LSTM) neural network model. This study uses the Bi-LSTM architecture to efficiently capture the sequential patterns exhibited in user interactions, purchase history, and other pertinent time-series data. This study explores the application of feature engineering procedures to improve the performance and interpretability of a model. In this study, we assess the performance of the Bi-LSTM model on a real-world e-commerce dataset. Our findings indicate that the proposed model exhibits higher predictive accuracy when compared to conventional machine learning methods. This study emphasizes the importance of utilizing deep learning methods, specifically Bi-LSTM, to enhance customer retention strategies in e-commerce. This study examines the impact of accurate prediction and subsequent actions on customer retention and overall business performance. Taking an alternative perspective, the research highlights the attractiveness of these factors in driving higher customer retention rates. This study presents promising results in the field of predictions, achieving an accuracy rate of 99.80% on the IBM Telecom Dataset, 99.61% on the Bank Customer Dataset, and 99.22% on the Orange Dataset. These findings indicate high reliability and potential for accurate predictions in the given case.
