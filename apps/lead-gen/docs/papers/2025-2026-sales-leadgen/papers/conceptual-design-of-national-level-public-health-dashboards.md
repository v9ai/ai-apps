# Conceptual design of national-level public health dashboards for transparent and evidence-based decision-making

**Authors.** Stephanie Onyekachi Oparah, Funmi Eko Ezeh, Glory Iyanuoluwa Olatunji, Opeoluwa Oluwanifemi Ajayi

**Venue / year.** International Journal of Applied Research in Social Sciences · 2025

**Links.** [DOI](https://doi.org/10.51594/ijarss.v7i10.2084) · [source](https://doi.org/10.51594/ijarss.v7i10.2084)

**Abstract.**

National-level public health dashboards have emerged as essential tools for enhancing transparency, enabling real-time monitoring, and supporting evidence-based decision-making in public health systems. These dashboards integrate multiple data streams, including epidemiological surveillance, healthcare utilization, laboratory testing, environmental monitoring, and social determinants of health, to provide a comprehensive overview of population health status. By visualizing complex datasets in a user-friendly and interactive manner, dashboards facilitate timely interpretation of trends, identification of emerging health threats, and evaluation of intervention effectiveness. The conceptual design of these dashboards emphasizes standardization, interoperability, and accessibility, ensuring that data from diverse sources can be consolidated and analyzed efficiently. Key design considerations include the incorporation of real-time analytics, customizable reporting, and geospatial mapping to highlight regional variations and hotspots. Additionally, dashboards are structured to support multi-level decision-making, providing actionable insights for policymakers, healthcare managers, and public health professionals while maintaining clarity for broader stakeholder engagement. Transparency and data governance principles are central to the framework, ensuring that data integrity, privacy, and ethical use are maintained while enabling public trust and accountability. Dashboards also serve as strategic instruments for evidence-based public health responses, allowing for rapid assessment of intervention impacts, resource allocation, and priority setting. Integration with predictive modeling and risk assessment tools enhances the capacity to anticipate disease outbreaks, evaluate health system performance, and optimize response strategies. Furthermore, a user-centered design approach ensures that the dashboard interfaces are intuitive, accessible across devices, and adaptable to different levels of technical expertise. National-level public health dashboards represent a critical innovation for modern health governance, combining data integration, visualization, and analytic capabilities to support transparent, evidence-driven decision-making. By linking real-time health intelligence with actionable insights, these systems strengthen public health preparedness, policy formulation, and population health outcomes, offering a scalable model for improving national health system performance and resilience.
