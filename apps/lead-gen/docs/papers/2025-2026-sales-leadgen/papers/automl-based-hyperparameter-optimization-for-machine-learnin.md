# AutoML-Based Hyperparameter Optimization for Machine Learning Models in Software Defect Prediction

**Authors.** Harsih Rianto, Lita Sari Marita, Martua Hami Siregar, Mushliha, Mari Rahmawati, Ahmad Rais Ruli

**Venue / year.** 2025 IEEE International Conference on Advanced Information Scientific Development (ICAISD) · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/86a53fdec88a57865e78652590319f967704e075)

**Abstract.**

Software defect prediction (SDP) plays a critical role in improving the quality of software and reducing maintenance costs. However, the performance of predictive models is highly dependent on hyperparameter configuration, which is often tuned manually and may lead to suboptimal results. To address this challenge, this study applies AutoML-based hyperparameter optimization to five widely used classification algorithms: Logistic Regression (LR), Support Vector Machine (SVM), Random Forest (RF), Gradient Boosting (GB), and Neural Network (NN). Ten benchmark datasets from the NASA Metrics Data Program (MDP) were employed, and evaluation was conducted using 10-fold cross-validation with comprehensive metrics, including Accuracy, AUC, F1-score, and G-Mean. The experimental results demonstrate that AutoML significantly improves the model performance, particularly for metrics sensitive to class imbalance. The neural Network (NN) achieved the highest F1-score improvement from 0.53 to 0.63 and the highest accuracy of 0.99, while ensemble models ($\mathbf{R F}$ and GB) reached the best AUC values up to 0.98 and G-Mean up to 0.70 on larger datasets such as PC4 and PC5. Conversely, linear models (LR and SVM) showed limited or inconsistent improvements, suggesting that AutoML is more effective for complex algorithm development. Overall, these findings highlight the potential of AutoML to automate hyperparameter optimization and enhance the predictive accuracy of SDP tasks.
