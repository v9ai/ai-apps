# ACCOUNT-BASED MARKETING -WINNING BEFORE THE FIRST MEETING&amp;nbsp;

**Abstract.**

Account-Based Marketing Paper 
&lt;div&gt;
 &lt;br&gt;
&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;div&gt;
 In B2B markets, the conventional sales logic assumes that the buying process begins with a meeting. This paper challenges that assumption. Drawing on empirical evidence from the 2025 Edelman-LinkedIn B2B Thought Leadership Impact Report, Momentum ITSMA's Value of Thought Leadership series, and the account-based marketing (ABM) literature, we argue that thought leadership-when deployed strategically-functions as the first act of a structured sales conversation: one that the vendor begins, and the buyer continues alone. We examine thought leadership not as a brand-building exercise but as a demand generation instrument operating upstream of intent. We introduce the concept of the "invisible buyer"-decision-makers who influence purchase outcomes without ever appearing in a CRM or attending a vendor presentation-and argue that reaching this hidden constituency requires a fundamentally different content logic than traditional inbound or outbound marketing. The paper proposes a framework we term Thought Leadership as Pre-Qualification (TL-PQ), which describes how strategically crafted content can activate latent demand, build trust before contact, and reduce resistance at the first commercial interaction. We conclude with implications for Account-Based Marketing strategy in B2B industrial contexts, with particular reference to the Italian manufacturing sector.
