# Flight Delay Prediction using Machine Learning Techniques

**Authors.** Ratan Lal, Sai Sravani Veeranki, Chalam Naidu Yecharla, Shabna Shaik, Abhishek Appalashetty, Sunil Kumar Kondru

**Venue / year.** 2025 International Conference on Cognitive, Green and Ubiquitous Computing (IC-CGU) · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/ffce0da57f3e31f5904dc8f0f0cf6860b32b72c7)

**Abstract.**

Air traffic congestion has emerged as a significant global challenge, with delays due to weather conditions, busy runways, and low visibility. These delays not only impact operational efficiency but also lead to increased costs and safety risks for passengers and airlines. Accurate and timely prediction of these delays is crucial to effectively manage air traffic and minimizing disruptions. Therefore, we focus on developing a method based on Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) models to predict and control air traffic. First, we collect a large dataset from the Kaggle website. Then, we perform data cleaning to handle missing and duplicate values, and data preprocessing to convert categorical features into numerical features, as well as extract relevant features by feature selection methods. Next, we split the dataset into two subsets, namely, the training and testing subsets. We use the training subset to train different models, namely, Logistic Regression, Multi Logistic Regression, Convolutional Neural Network (CNN), Recurrent Neural Network (RNN), Random Forest Classifier, XGBoost, Light Gradient Boosting Machine (LightGBM), and catBoost. Subsequently, we evaluate them based on performance metrics. Then, we select the best model among all models based on their evaluation. From our experimental results, we have found that the CNN model achieves the largest recall of 98.2% and an F1-score of 88.0% compared to other models, except the RNN, while the RNN outperforms the CNN in all performance metrics except accuracy.
