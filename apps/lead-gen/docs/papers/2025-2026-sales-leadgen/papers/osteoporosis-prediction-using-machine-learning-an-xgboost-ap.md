# Osteoporosis Prediction Using Machine Learning: An XGBoost Approach for Early Detection

**Authors.** P. G, Kothai Ganesan, Sivakarthick B, V. K, N. S

**Venue / year.** International Conference on Intelligent Cloud Computing · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/32ddfa53fbe01763bc0e77736f4a6400056a88f6)

**Abstract.**

Osteoporosis is an ailment which affects the skeletal system and is chronic in nature. It decreases the bone mineral density, thus making an individual prone to fractures, and minimizes to the quality of life, especially in older age groups. Hence, early diagnosis becomes crucial for timely intervention to manage the disease efficiently. However, conventional diagnostic methods such as Dual-Energy X-ray Absorptiometry (DXA) and Quantitative Computed Tomography (QCT) are generally expensive, time-consuming, and mostly unavailable in resource-poor settings. In addition, the aforementioned diagnostic modules tend to have variable introspection, reliance on skilled radiologists and delays in diagnosis, which further limit their utility in mass screening. Thus, this research presents a machine learning-based osteoporosis prediction model which relies on Extreme Gradient Boosting. This work seeks to optimize prediction accuracy and efficiency. The XGBoost model is designed using standardized pre-processing and feature selection processes and hyper-parameter tuning, thus enhancing its predicting accuracy. It has been evaluated by the model on real-life datasets of the disease and has proven to have the ability to predict better compared to the others. It has undergone extensive experimentation and has been found out that the XGBoost model gives $\mathbf{9 2 \%}$ Accuracy, 98% Precision, 85% Recall, 91 % F1 score, and 67 % R-squared ($\mathbf{R}^{\mathbf{2}}$) values when compared to the conventional methods of diagnosis, making it a perfect solution for the diagnosis of osteoporosis. Thus, this outcome certifies the proposed study to be cost-effective and scalable approach without reduction in predictive accuracy for osteoporosis detection, facilitating the diagnosis without defining the need for radiation-based diagnosis. Therefore, the designed model could facilitate early detection and timely intervention that could lead to improved prognosis among patients and reduce healthcare burden as well.
