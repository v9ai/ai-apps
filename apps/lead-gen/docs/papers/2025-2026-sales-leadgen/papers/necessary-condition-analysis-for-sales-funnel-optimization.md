# Necessary condition analysis for sales funnel optimization

**Authors.** Richard Conde

**Venue / year.** 2025

**Links.** [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1057/s41270-025-00388-5) · [source](https://doi.org/10.1057/s41270-025-00388-5)

**Abstract.**

Abstract
          This study explores the application of Necessary Condition Analysis (NCA) to sales funnel optimization, offering a novel approach to understanding critical factors in the sales process. While traditional methods focus on sufficiency and average effects, NCA identifies indispensable conditions for success at each funnel stage. By analyzing necessary conditions, sales organizations can reveal bottlenecks and minimum requirements for converting prospects into customers. This research presents a comprehensive framework for implementing NCA across the seven sales’ funnel stages: prospecting, pre-approach, approach, presentation, overcoming objections, closing, and follow-up/retention. NCA’s unique perspective on causality, complementing predictive analytics, and AI-driven approaches, provides actionable insights and handles complex non-linear relationships. The study aims to contribute to sales analytics literature and equip sales professionals with a powerful tool for enhancing performance in challenging business environments, ultimately leading to more efficient resource allocation and improved sales strategies.
