# Ensemble Learning based Categorical Boosting for Customer Lifetime Value Prediction in Businesses

**Authors.** K. M. Monica, M. Habelalmateen, S. K S, A. Buckshumiyan, M. K

**Venue / year.** 2025 International Conference on Intelligent Systems and Computational Networks (ICISCN) · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/8569e739b7996c50bdaf981773d71f8b1f6bff65)

**Abstract.**

Nowadays, Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) prediction is becoming critical task for businesses to identify high-value customers and optimize the marketing strategies. However, the existing Deep Neural Networks (DNN) failed to predict CLV in business due to the dynamic and heterogeneous nature of customer behaviour. Hence, this research proposes an Ensemble Learning based Categorical Boosting (EL-CatBoost) to provide decision-making and strategic planning for businesses. Initially, the data is collected from online retail customer churn dataset and fed into preprocessing with K-Nearest Neighbor (KNN) imputation. The preprocessing technique effectively handles the missing values in the data by replacing the missing value with nearest similar one. Then, the Latent Semantic Analysis (LSA) is employed to extract semantic, sentiment, and contextual features from the preprocessed data. Finally, the proposed EL-CatBoost is employed to predict the CLV by capturing the complex relationships within the features. From the results, the proposed EL-CatBoost achieved better results in terms of terms of Rate of Investment (ROI) with 10-day prediction (ROI-10) of 0.1803 and ROI with 20-day prediction (ROI-20) of 0.3795 when compared with the existing Temporal Structural User Representation (TSUR) network.
