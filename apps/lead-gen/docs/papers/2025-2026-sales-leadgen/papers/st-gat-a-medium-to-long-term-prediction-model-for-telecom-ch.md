# ST-GAT: A Medium-To-Long-term Prediction Model for Telecom Churn Based on Spatial-Temporal Graph Attention Networks

**Authors.** Jun Wu, Ming-wen Wang, Zhi-yuan Yu, Qiang-jun Luo

**Venue / year.** Data Intelligence · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/c63765926aca1624bf275e767bf59a8aa44932c6)

**Abstract.**

Medium-to-long-term customer churn prediction plays a critical role in enterprise customer relationship management (CRM). Most existing predictive models focus on short-term forecasting (e.g., identifying customers at risk of churn in the next month), yet their practical efficacy is limited due to the delayed implementation effects of retention policies. To address this issue, this paper proposes a medium-to-long-term prediction model for telecom churn based on spatial temporal graph attention networks (ST-GAT). This model overcomes the limitations of traditional Graph Convolution Neural Networks’(GCNs) static adjacency matrices by dynamically adjusting spatial interaction weights among customers through a graph attention network, thereby accurately capturing the spatiotemporal evolution patterns of user behavior. To validate the model's effectiveness, empirical experiments were conducted on real-world telecom operator datasets for mid-term (2-month) and long-term (3-month) churn prediction tasks. Results demonstrate significant improvements in top-lift and profit maximization metrics compared to baseline models, confirming the model's superiority in addressing CRM challenges for telecom enterprises.
