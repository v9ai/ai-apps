# Analysis and Prediction of Chronic Kidney Disease Using Machine Learning Techniques

**Authors.** D. G., J. N., Govindaraju G N

**Venue / year.** 2025 Third International Conference on Networks, Multimedia and Information Technology (NMITCON) · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/a89a914b7c481abcd0378256d1e33d9e3eafde43)

**Abstract.**

In chronic kidney disease (CKD), the kidneys slowly stop working properly. Over time, they lose their ability to filter waste and fluids from the blood. This can lead to a buildup of harmful substances in the body where dialysis or surgery is the only options to save a patient's life. Early detection and intervention can reduce the likelihood of this happening. This study has looked at the potential of a number of machine learning methods to provide a timely diagnosis of chronic renal failure. This topic has been the subject of a substantial amount of research. However, by using predictive modeling, To strengthen the strategy to examine the correlation between data elements and the target class's characteristics in this approach. With the aid of different machine learning techniques and predictive analytics, different predictive models can be created. This study starts with 25 variables in addition to the class attribute, but by the end, it has narrowed the list to 30 % of those parameters as the most useful subset for CKD detection. The five models were evaluated through testing and training. The dataset can be separated into random testing and training subsets using sklearn.model_selection. Eighty percent of the data used for training the model and twenty percent for testing. The Gradient Boosting Classifiers demonstrated an accuracy of 0.937 and an AUC-ROC Score of 0.93, while the Extra Tree Classifiers demonstrated an accuracy of 0.975 and an AUCROC Score of 0.997. Following hyperparameter tuning RandomizedSearchCV method, the final results were assessed, and the Gradient Boosting Classifiers demonstrated the greatest accuracy of $\mathbf{0. 9 8 7}$ and AUC-ROC Score of $\mathbf{1. 0 0}$. This can save lives by assisting patients and healthcare professionals in predicting chronic kidney disease early.
