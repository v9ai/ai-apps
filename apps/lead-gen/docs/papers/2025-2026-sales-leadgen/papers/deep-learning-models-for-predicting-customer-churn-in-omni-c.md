# Deep Learning Models for Predicting Customer Churn in Omni-Channel Retail Environments

**Authors.** Talib Ahmed, Majid M. Medicine, Mohammed Fadhil Ibrahim, Wesam Ali Husien, Laila Abdul Karim Mohammed, Raed Majeed Abd Mohammed, Zainab Kadum Jabe, Saadaldeen Rashid Ahmed, Rafad Imad Kadhim

**Venue / year.** 2025 3rd International Conference on Business Analytics for Technology and Security (ICBATS) · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/a766ec6512171ef998d84d1b6bb25a54e45f9a9d)

**Abstract.**

As the retail environment is continuously changing, the typical problem of client churn is presenting itself. This is particularly true of omnichannel environments. In an omnichannel world, numerous data outputs from different channels make predictive algorithms rather complex. Therefore, there is an urgent need to boost churn prediction accuracy. This innovation can simply improve client retention efforts tremendously. To address this challenge, the authors build a new hybrid deep learning framework that blends CNN and LSTM networks. They carry out considerable data processing and feature engineering steps on a broad dataset encompassing both online and offline sources. Overall, the authors utilize deep learning, churn prediction, omnichannel retail, neural networks, customer analytics, machine learning, and predictive modeling. According to the findings of the conducted experiments, and employing TensorFlow and Keras, our model demonstrates a significantly enhanced performance over other baseline models. The results include an accuracy of 91.2%, a precision of 89.7%, a recall of 88.5%, an F1-score of 89.1 %, and a ROC-AUC of 0.93. In conclusion, the recommended framework is a better alternative to conventional ways, as it is a solution that can be scaled on a large level for real-world retail applications and will guarantee improved customer relationship management and revenue increase.
