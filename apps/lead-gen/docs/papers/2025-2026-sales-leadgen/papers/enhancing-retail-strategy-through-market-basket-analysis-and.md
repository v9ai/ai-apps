# Enhancing Retail Strategy Through Market Basket Analysis and Social Network Influence

**Authors.** M. Chaturvedi, Shweta Loonkar, Karishma Desai

**Venue / year.** 2025 International Conference on Applications of Machine Intelligence and Data Analytics (ICAMIDA) · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/c4a97bd83e78e07ba6e95f722c3a95380ea591eb)

**Abstract.**

with the retail industry going through dynamic and ever evolving transformation every minute to optimize their marketing strategies and gain better understanding of consumer behavior. Market Basket Analysis is a data mining technique used to find the associations between different products that are most likely to be purchased together, creating cross-selling and upselling opportunities. But these traditional methods of finding patterns are limited to just the transactional data of the customer and product. Social network influence is another technique, which can be combined in with the MBA algorithms to add an additional layer of data to make the results more personalized and specific to the customers. Social influences play a big role in shaping our purchasing choices, and hence leveraging social networking can help maximize retail efficiencies. By using social data from media platforms like Instagram, Facebook, X, etc. and gaining insights into the social trends, influences, social interactions and much more. The framework in this paper will leverage machine learning algorithm, Apriori algorithm, to explore and compare the results of using traditional Market Basket Analysis techniques and then integrating Social Network Influence to it, and evaluate the difference in the accuracy of the results. A combined framework would give product recommendations that will be up to the ongoing trends, and overall give results with a higher accuracy.
