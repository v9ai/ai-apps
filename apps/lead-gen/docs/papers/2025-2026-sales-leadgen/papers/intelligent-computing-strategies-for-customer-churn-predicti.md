# Intelligent computing strategies for customer churn prediction: Innovations and practical applications

**Authors.** Rekha R Nair, Tina Babu, Deepika Nayak

**Venue / year.** 2025

**Links.** [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1201/9781003515470-23) · [source](https://doi.org/10.1201/9781003515470-23)

**Abstract.**

The telecommunications industry is increasingly focused on customer retention due to high competition and market saturation. Customer churn prediction (CCP) has become essential for preventing customer loss, leveraging data analytics and machine learning to identify potential churners. This study explores various CCP models, including Support Vector Machines, Naive Bayes, Decision Trees, and Neural Networks, with an emphasis on data preparation techniques like feature selection and under-sampling for imbalanced data. Ensemble models and deep learning approaches, which combine multiple techniques, have shown significant improvements in predictive accuracy. The proposed analysis demonstrates that XGBoost, when optimized using gradient descent, achieved the highest precision (76.5%) and recall (68.0%) with an ROC AUC of 88.0%, outperforming other models. Logistic Regression also performed well with an optimized F1 score of 71.1% and an ROC AUC of 84.0%. These results highlight the importance of model optimization and the use of ensemble methods in enhancing the predictive power of CCP models, aiding telecom operators in implementing more effective customer retention strategies.
