# Harnessing AI and Machine Learning in E-Commerce to Advance Product Recommendation Systems and Sales Forecast Accuracy

**Authors.** Ganesh Waghmare, Jairaj Sasane, Nazir Ahmad Ahengar, Bipin Sule, Maryam Mansoor Mathkoor, Mustafa Mudhafar

**Venue / year.** 2025 IEEE 5th International Conference on ICT in Business Industry & Government (ICTBIG) · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/15e543d6ffaf2ed1ea50ae308508119203f63f2d)

**Abstract.**

This article is about a system that uses AI and ML to make buying online easier and faster. Smart recommendations, time-series sales forecasting, and adaptive pricing optimization are some of the things that work together to make the user experience seamless. You may use this architecture to build a full learning system that changes based on how people respond, what the market is like, and how much it costs. The earliest phases of the system combine attentionbased methods and deep neural embeddings to keep track of complex interactions between users and products. The outcome is solid advice for the person in question. We may combine this data with a hybrid temporal learning model that uses both recurrent and convolutional architectures to create accurate sales forecasts. These forecasts consider both short-term fluctuations and long-term buying patterns. Finally, a decision module that uses reinforcement learning finds a balance between meeting customer wants and making money by translating future results into adaptable pricing methods. The suggested structure works well, as indicated by its R2 value of 0.98, accuracy of 97.3%, and precision of 96.9%. Our adaptive pricing strategy makes the market move faster and cuts down on errors (mean absolute error: 0.089, root mean square error: 0.134). The technology is reliable and flexible enough to meet the demands of big e-commerce businesses. It can process data at a rate of 96.4% and run at a pace of 4.1 seconds. The framework quickly makes pricing, user engagement, and sales projections better by utilizing AI to tailor experiences, look at data, and make adjustments on the spot. The results suggest that intelligent, self-learning technology might change how businesses buy things online by making decisions easier, accelerating processes, and helping them keep up with the digital markets that are continually changing.
