# Social Network-Integrated Machine Learning for Customer Churn Prediction

**Authors.** J. R, A. Pravin, Ramiz. R. M, Ramprasath. R, K. A. Kumar

**Venue / year.** International Conference on Intelligent Cloud Computing · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/30606f0694727eb1f91ae9a99238aeff3e0bc04d)

**Abstract.**

Customer churn prediction is an area of concern for all types of businesses in their drive to develop retention strategies in a bid of financial sustainability over the medium to long term. Using social network and advanced machine learning techniques, this research focuses on the prediction of customer attrition. The system catches at-risk customers early, using patterns of interpersonal interactions, sentiment analysis, and behavioral patterns observed on the social media platforms. The loading of social networks, the feature engineering and the casting classification models, made with tree, logistic regression and neural network are included. The linking of social network data to the social engagement metrics and their ability to correlate to churn rates demonstrates the predictive power of social network data. The study focuses on the necessity to keep in mind the ethical questions, data security and the continuous model improvement for robust models. By leveraging this novel approach, an organization can protect itself from churn by deploying interventions targeted to those clients likely to exit, which augments both customer loyalty and an exit trajectory that is incompatible with long term growth. As a contribution to the emerging field of predictive analytics, this research provides useful business insight for companies that evaluate the ability to include social network data into customer relationship systems.
