# Graph Neural Networks for Customer Churn Analysis in Subscription-Based Business Models

**Authors.** Sagar Bhadange, Chanakya Kumar Jha, Ankit Bajaj, Ajit Sane, Manjiri Joshi, P. Jha, P. P. Gupta

**Venue / year.** 2025 3rd World Conference on Communication & Computing (WCONF) · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/db5d78f09c0304c7938fdf4c62499697d7c538e6)

**Abstract.**

Customer churn prediction is critical for subscription-based businesses to maintain revenue stability and optimize retention strategies. Traditional machine learning approaches treat customers as independent entities, ignoring the rich relational information inherent in customer networks. This paper proposes a novel Graph Neural Network (GNN) framework that captures complex customer interactions and social influences to predict churn probability. We model customers as nodes in a graph, with edges representing various relationships including referrals, shared services, and social connections. Our approach employs Graph Convolutional Networks (GCN) and Graph Attention Networks (GAT) to learn node embeddings that encapsulate both individual customer features and network structure. Experiments on three real-world datasets—Telco Customer Churn, IBM Watson Churn, and Kaggle Subscription Service—demonstrate that our GNN-based model achieves superior performance with accuracy improvements of 7.3%, 8.1%, and 6.8% over traditional methods including Random Forest, XGBoost, and Multi-Layer Perceptron. The model achieves F1-scores of 0.847, 0.862, and 0.835 on respective datasets, with AUC-ROC scores exceeding 0.91 across all benchmarks. Our findings suggest that incorporating network topology significantly enhances churn prediction capabilities, enabling more effective customer retention strategies.
