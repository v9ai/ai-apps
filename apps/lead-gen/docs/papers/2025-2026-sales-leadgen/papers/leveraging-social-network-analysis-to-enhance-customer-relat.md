# Leveraging Social Network Analysis to Enhance Customer Relationship Management in Retail Commerce

**Authors.** Snehlata Barde, S. Kishan, D. Swami, Sneha Soni, Himanshu Tiwari, Rahul Kumar

**Venue / year.** 2025 1st International Conference on Data Science and Intelligent Network Computing (ICDSINC) · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/d2fd9683902c6cc83b361e6961930410620d8d42)

**Abstract.**

In the present-day retail industry, which is very much cut-throat, it remains crucial to have the customer engagement and retention activities highly active and visible. The traditional Customer Relationship Management (CRM) systems primarily deal with transaction data; they often overlook the influence of social interactions on customers' behavior. The introduction of this research is the hybrid framework that integrates Social Network Analysis (SNA) with Machine Learning (ML) to enhance the effectiveness of CRM. The research was conducted on anonymized e-commerce data that was gathered over one year. The authors constructed customer networks, and from these, they extracted key network metrics, which were degree centrality, betweenness, PageRank, and modularity. These metrics were then handed over to ML models such as Logistic Regression, Random Forest, XGBoost, and Neural Networks for churn prediction. It is indicated by the findings that network-informed features inclusion raises predictive performance significantly, with XGBoost being the one attaining an ROC-AUC of 0.93. The SHAP feature importance analysis has identified the major churn drivers. The hybrid approach is compared to conventional RFM segmentation and it proves to be 18% (p < 0.05) more accurate in retaining customer prediction. The network visualizations are making the influencing customers and community structures visible. Besides, limitations and future research directions, such as real-time SNA-CRM integration, have been mentioned in the discussion.
