**Abstract.**

We introduce Behavioral Technical Analysis (BTA), a novel framework that applies financial market technical indicators-specifically the Relative Strength Index (RSI), Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD), and Bollinger Bands-to non-financial human behavioral time series data. We demonstrate that the same mathematical patterns that reveal momentum, exhaustion, and reversal in financial markets are equally present in behavioral data streams including music consumption, e-commerce activity, and social media engagement. We further show that these patterns encode two previously unexplored dimensions: (1) purchase intent timing-identifying the precise moment a consumer crosses the psychological threshold toward a buying decision, and (2) organizational decision structure-inferring whether a behavioral trace originates from a single decision-maker or a committee-driven entity. Back-testing against historical Spotify streaming data yields 80-85% directional accuracy. Cross-domain validation on 50 months of e-commerce transaction data (1,751 orders) demonstrates that BTA-identified T0 moments co-occur with 1.66x higher average order value and 1.77x higher revenue density compared to random baseline periods-consistent with the hypothesis that T0 selectively surfaces high-conviction purchasing behavior. This framework presents a significant commercial opportunity in real-time advertising targeting and a theoretical contribution to behavioral economics and computational marketing.
