# A Review of Customer Classification Techniques Based on Historical Purchase Data

**Authors.** Kartik Mittal, Priya, Om Maheshwar, Somya Srivastava

**Venue / year.** 2026 5th International Conference on Innovative Practices in Technology and Management (ICIPTM) · 2026

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/35188df5706021e5e7e381f8465df4ccac852863)

**Abstract.**

Customer segmentation is crucial for business intelligence as it helps to target customers in a personalized way and manage customer relationships effectively. This review covers the main methods of customer classification that utilize purchase history data and mentions the progression from RFM analysis to machine learning techniques that have become popular recently. Unsupervised models (e.g., K-Means and DBSCAN) discover inherent customer segments, while supervised models (e.g., Random Forests and SVMs) are used for churn prediction and customer lifetime value estimation. The latest techniques, such as Recurrent Neural Networks, are able to follow the temporal aspects of customer behaviour. The research evaluates the performance and interpretability of different models, acknowledges issues such as data sparsity and data drift, and points to the integration of deep learning and explainable AI as a direction for the future.
