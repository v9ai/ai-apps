# AI-Driven Timeline Drift Index for Schedule Management in Defence R&D Projects

**Authors.** Anushika Rana, S. Goswami

**Venue / year.** 2025 Second International Conference on Pioneering Developments in Computer Science & Digital Technologies (IC2SDT) · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/257e1cf60f2dcc77829a1f9afe75fad5da255dd1)

**Abstract.**

In high-stakes R&D environments, particularly within the defence sector, any delays in the project could significantly hamper mission-critical goals and lead to poor management of resources. The research introduces a two-module system that combines schedule volatility analysis and predictive modelling to enhance future timeline management of projects. The first module is introduced as the Timeline Drift Index (TDI) as the composite measure of the frequency of revisions of Probable Date of Completion (PDC), the total delay days and average delay per revision. The TDI scores of the projects can be clustered in low, medium, and high volatility using the K-Means clustering, and results are revealed under the label of laboratory affiliation, budget and project category related patterns. The second module makes use of machine learning models, including Linear Regression, Support Vector Regression (SVR), Random Forest and Gradient Boosting in order to model the timeline assumptions of final project completion using the engineered features, TDI and cost variance. An experiment on data of 1,000 anonymized defence R&D projects demonstrates that the Support Vector Regression (SVR) model performed best, reaching an accuracy measured by R2 of 0.99, and the Random Forest and Gradient Boosting models also bode well with scores over 0.90. The two-module process allows recognizing the risk of high-risk projects in advance and allows making better decisions regarding resource planning and delay mitigation.
