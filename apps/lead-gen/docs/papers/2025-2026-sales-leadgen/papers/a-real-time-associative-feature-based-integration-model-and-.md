# A Real-time Associative Feature-based Integration Model and Platform for Small and Medium-sized Enterprises in the Manufacturing Industry

**Authors.** Sun, Yiding

**Venue / year.** University of Alberta Library · 2025

**Links.** [DOI](https://doi.org/10.7939/83456) · [source](https://doi.org/10.7939/83456)

**Abstract.**

Small and Medium-sized Enterprises (SMEs) are essential drivers of global economic growth, yet they continually face significant challenges in digital transformation. These challenges include deficiencies in data transformation and synchronization, high complexity and cost barriers associated with existing integration solutions, difficulties in retrieving heterogeneous and unstructured enterprise data, and the complexities inherent in multi-variant structural design and visualization. To address these issues, this work first introduces a novel integration methodology specifically tailored for SMEs, focusing on Customer Relationship Management (CRM) and Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) systems. Utilizing associative feature technology, a new generic information carrier—Real-time Data Link Board (RDLB)—is proposed. The RDLB dynamically maps valuable data elements, enabling standardized, modular data exchange and synchronization across CRM, ERP, and related enterprise systems. This integration methodology ensures consistent data modeling and seamless information synchronization throughout the application lifecycle, underpinned by object-oriented software engineering principles. The effectiveness of this method is validated through a practical case study involving the integration of selling factor inheritance between CRM and ERP systems. Building upon this integration foundation, the work develops a Real-time Associative Feature-based Integration Platform (RAIP), extending beyond CRM and ERP to encompass additional enterprise software applications, cross-departmental collaboration, and core business process customization. RAIP leverages real-time associative feature modeling for data transformation, significantly enhancing cross-system interoperability and operation adaptability for SMEs. To further augment the platform's capabilities, RAIP is extended to RAIP-AI through the integration of Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) techniques and Large Language Models (LLMs). This advancement facilitates intelligent semantic retrieval, natural language querying, and contextual analysis across diverse enterprise data sources. The practical utility of RAIP/RAIP-AI is demonstrated through detailed case studies involving the associative integration of No Charge Request and Return Authorization Forms (RAFs), as well as an enterprise-level semantic question-answering scenario. Finally, this dissertation introduces an integrated void-lattice infill structure design framework based on the RAIP, addressing the growing demands for complex product design and mass customization within additive manufacturing contexts. A multiscale topology optimization approach is developed, employing an ordered SIMP-like stress interpolation combined with a modified Tsai-Hill failure criterion. The framework incorporates high-fidelity verification via de-homogenization and Finite Element Analysis (FEA), ensuring geometrically diverse, mechanically robust, and manufacturable design outcomes. RAIP seamlessly integrates the design, optimization, and real-time data management processes among CRM, ERP, and FEA systems, thereby achieving closed-loop digital New Product Development (NPD). Additionally, a multi-objective optimization strategy utilizing Variational Autoencoders (VAE) and the NSGA-II algorithm ensures diversity and rank of the optimized design candidates. Validation through a benchmark three-point support beam structure confirms the framework’s efficacy, demonstrating significant improvements in computational efficiency, structural performance, and manufacturability. Overall, this work presents a unified, scalable, reusable, and practically implementable integration framework, which significantly enhances information consistency, system interoperability, change propagation, and operational efficiency. By addressing critical SME-specific challenges, this work substantially contributes to the advancement of digital transformation, intelligent system integration, and customized NPD within manufacturing SMEs.
