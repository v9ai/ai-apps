# AI-Driven Environmental, Social, and Governance Score Predictions: A Machine Learning Approach Using SDG Indicators

**Authors.** Anmol Jain, Divyansh Agarwal, Dhairya Jain, Manoj Kumar, Nishant Gupta

**Venue / year.** 2025 International Conference on Sustainability, Innovation & Technology (ICSIT) · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/9f93449293c25c6efd01f81cd773489079c1d92e)

**Abstract.**

Sustainability has become a crucial priority for global development, specifically Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) scores that serve as criteria for judging ethical and sustainable behavior. Machine learning models are applied to predict ESG scores with respect to Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) indicators which lead us to a primary discussion of some challenges faced in ESG analytics, namely data sparsity, indicator interdependencies, and non-linear relationship. Through the Gradient Boosting methodology, PCA, and advanced feature engineering, we showcase a robust predictive framework that outperforms traditional statistical methods with respect to performance. This is a novel approach combining multi-source SDG data with advanced preprocessing techniques to deal with missing values and multicollinearity issues. The proposed model is significantly more accurate when compared to baseline methods and offers unique insights regarding feature importance within the environment, society, and governance domains. The present study highlights the importance of ESG metrics in sustainable development, the model performance is evaluated using various metrics, and the actionable implications for decision-makers are considered. Such interpretability is enhanced for practitioners, affirmed by the visual representation of results using feature importance plot, predicted vs actual score graph, and statistical comparison of models.
