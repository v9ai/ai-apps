# Learning Piecewise-Stationary Multi-Armed Bandit with One-Sided Feedback

**Authors.** Yingze Hou, Xiuli Chao, Jingui Xie

**Venue / year.** 2026

**Links.** [DOI](https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6417139) · [source](https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6417139)

**Abstract.**

We study piecewise-stationary multi-armed bandit problems with one-sided feedback, in which selecting a larger arm reveals information about smaller ones. Such informational coupling arises in various operational settings and can significantly accelerate learning. We consider environments where reward distributions may change abruptly over time while the one-sided feedback structure remains stable. We develop a learning framework that integrates elimination-based learning with an active change-detection mechanism, leveraging the one-sided feedback to update multiple actions from a single observation. We establish a regret bound of $\tilde{O}(\sqrt{MT})$ over horizon T with M abrupt changes, which is independent of the number of actions K. Our results show that one-sided feedback can yield K-independent learning efficiency in piecewise-stationary environments, without relying on specific analytical properties of the reward function.
