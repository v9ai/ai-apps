# Predicting Customer Churn in Financial Services Using Ensemble Machine Learning Models

**Authors.** Anirudh Parupalli

**Venue / year.** 2025 Global Conference on Information Technology and Communication Networks (GITCON) · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/e0eb061840d138344d86b7f315513ac7ac3e4dd8)

**Abstract.**

The early detection of client leave is critical for the survival of banks, making customer churn forecasting a long-standing problem in the banking industry. Predicting client attrition is challenging due to the data imbalance between the churn and no-churn classification groups. This study presents an ML-based approach to utilizing the Bank Customer Churn dataset to develop an accurate churn prediction model. Several classification models, such as Artificial Neural Networks (ANN), Random Forest (RF), and k-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) were used, and their Hyperparameters were hyperparameter-optimized. An ensemble classifier using weighted soft voting was proposed by combining classifiers to achieve a performance of 99.8% accuracy and 99.49% F1-score. Models were evaluated using parameters like F1-score, accuracy, recall, and ROC-AUC. Experimental evidence demonstrates that the ensemble model significantly outperforms individual models and serves as a stable tool in proactive customer retention. Confusion matrices and ROC curves were among the visualizations that further confirmed each model's efficacy. Findings from this study suggest that ensemble learning may be a powerful data-driven tool for churn prediction, enabling financial institutions to identify high-risk customers and implement targeted retention strategies to reduce losses.
