# Intelligent Forecasting of Online Retail Sales Using Ensemble Machine Learning Models

**Authors.** K. Reddy, Sreebhashyam Harimanasa, Majji. Reddy, G. Sai, T. Prasanthi

**Venue / year.** Proceedings of the 1st International Conference on Intelligent Methods and Advanced Computer Scientific Innovations · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/7ddb362c3c6a2fe4aa4d89fdf3286bc111bb7bfd)

**Abstract.**

: Accurate sales forecasting plays a pivotal role in optimizing decision-making processes in e-commerce environments, where dynamic trends and consumer behavior patterns create significant forecasting challenges. This research investigates the performance of various time series models on an e-commerce sales dataset to determine which approaches offer the highest predictive accuracy. The study evaluates classical machine learning models such as XGBoost and LightGBM, deep learning techniques including LSTM and GRU, and a statistical rule-based model (Prophet). Furthermore, it proposes an ensemble model that integrates XGBoost, LSTM, and Prophet to leverage their complementary strengths. Evaluation metrics R² score, Mean Absolute Error (MAE), Mean Squared Error (MSE), and Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE) were used to assess forecasting accuracy. The ensemble model achieved superior performance, with an R² score of 0.93, MAE of 0.15, MSE of 0.05, and RMSE of 0.22, outperforming all other individual models. These results underscore the effectiveness of hybrid approaches in capturing nonlinear patterns, seasonal trends, and structural complexities within sales data. The findings contribute valuable insights for deploying advanced forecasting models in real-world e-commerce platforms.
