# Modeling Jordanian audience engagement and news credibility in the digital media era: a PLS-SEM approach

**Authors.** Yousef Awad Ahmad Al-Mshashaqbeh

**Venue / year.** 2025

**Links.** [DOI](https://doi.org/10.3389/fcomm.2025.1675693) · [source](https://doi.org/10.3389/fcomm.2025.1675693)

**Abstract.**

The rise of digital news has reshaped the landscape of news consumption, especially in emerging media environments such as Jordan, where audiences increasingly depend on online and social platforms for information. This study examines how Trust in Digital News (TND) and Social Media Use (SMU) influence Audience Engagement (AE) and Perceived News Credibility (PNC) among Jordanian users. A two-step methodological approach was adopted: first, a bibliometric mapping was conducted to identify research trends and gaps in digital media studies; second, Partial Least Squares Structural Equation Modeling (PLS-SEM) was applied to survey data collected from 1,200 respondents.
