# Hybrid Learning Models for Remaining Useful Life Prediction of Lithium-Ion Batteries in Electric Vehicles

**Authors.** G. Sahdev, S. Agarwal, P. Bhati

**Venue / year.** IOP Conference Series: Materials Science and Engineering · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/648deaa7382f53a341fdc900304455fa9c760378)

**Abstract.**

The fast adoption of electric vehicles (EVs) requires useful battery management for determining the Remaining Useful Life (RUL) of lithium-ion batteries (LiBs). RUL estimation is necessary for highlighting capacity and power fade generated by repeated charging cycles, environmental conditions and operational factors that affect performance, safety and cost of electric vehicles. LiBs degrade with time and lead to downgrade of efficiency and can have possible failures. Accurate RUL measurement is important for proactive maintenance and operational planning. Battery degradation is affected by different types of factors that require strong methodologies to give precise estimation. Data-driven techniques are helpful for RUL prediction as they can make use of real-world battery performance data to improve the accuracy. Prior studies have investigated different types of methodologies that include machine learning, deep learning, statistical techniques and hybrid models to predict battery degradation patterns effectively. The limitations of these approaches have an impact on optimizing battery health and reducing costs of maintenance. The aim of this research work is to look for a robust hybrid learning approach for RUL prediction. Four hybrid models are implemented in this research, namely Gated Recurrent Unit and Support Vector Machine (GRU-SVM), GRU and Extreme Gradient Boosting (GRU-XGBoost), Long Short-Term Memory and SVM (LSTM-SVM), and LSTM-XGBoost. This study highlights finding such a data-driven system that helps in optimizing battery health, improving reliability and reducing maintenance costs. The evaluation metrics used for indicating the performance of proposed hybrid models are Mean Absolute Error (MAE), Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE), Mean Squared Error (MSE) and R2 score to ensure precise and reliable predictions. The results show that the LSTM-XGBoost hybrid model outperforms all other models with the highest R2 score of 0.9797. The RUL prediction results for LSTM-XGBoost also showed the lowest error values with the MAE, RMSE and MSE as 4.40, 6.33 and 40.19, respectively. This hybrid model provided the most accurate and reliable results for RUL prediction of LiBs among the proposed models.
