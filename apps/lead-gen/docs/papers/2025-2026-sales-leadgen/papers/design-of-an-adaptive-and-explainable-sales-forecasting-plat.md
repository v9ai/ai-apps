# Design of an Adaptive and Explainable Sales Forecasting Platform Using Auto-ARIMA

**Authors.** S. Sangeetha, R. Valency, CS. Nanthetha Sri

**Venue / year.** 2026 Second International Conference on Multi-Agent Systems for Collaborative Intelligence (ICMSCI) · 2026

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/6dde36df57d5b7541a14e702eb65b102d2320930)

**Abstract.**

Accurate and explainable sales forecasting is central to modern retail practice. It directly affects inventory decisions, procurement planning, and long-term financial performance. Deep learning models such as LSTMs and Transformers can deliver strong predictive accuracy, but their “black-box” nature and heavy computational requirements often limit their use in contexts such as regulation or high-stakes settings where transparency and auditability are essential. In this work, we propose and evaluate an adaptive sales forecasting platform based on the Auto-ARIMA methodology. The system comprises an automated statistical model selection, Seasonal Trend decomposition using Loess (STL), and probabilistic residual-based anomaly detection within a unified, microservicesbased architecture. This enables the full pipeline from robust data ingestion and preprocessing through dynamic retraining, natural language explanations, and interactive dashboards. Experiments on representative retail datasets, such as subsets of the M5 competition data, demonstrate that our platform reaches a Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE) of 11.2%, competitive when compared to other, more computationally expensive LSTM-based competing approaches while being easier to deploy, monitor, and audit. These results suggest that carefully engineered statistical platforms can balance predictive accuracy with human-centred transparency, offering a practical blueprint for the next generation of explainable decision support systems in retail.
