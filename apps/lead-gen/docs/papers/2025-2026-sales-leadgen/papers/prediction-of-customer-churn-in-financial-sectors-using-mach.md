# Prediction of Customer Churn in Financial Sectors using Machine Learning Algorithms

**Authors.** M. Aruna, P. Kumari, Shaiku Shahida Saheb

**Venue / year.** CSI International Symposium on Artificial Intelligence and Signal Processing · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/77c30738351c928de87d3f0370889103aabf36c3)

**Abstract.**

Machine Learning algorithms became effective models for estimating customers' financial performances, notably in the area of customer churn patterns for financial institutions, especially in the banking industry. To comprehend how customers have churning from their financial institutions, it is also important to explore the pragmatic driving forces of customer relationship management. In a competing environment, commercial banks are undertaking empirical evidence-based analysis of their customers' churn behavior, with a view to increasing retention and the profitability function. The present study suggests that the potential for using ML models such as Logistic Regression (LR), Support Vector Machine (SVM), k-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), and Random Forest, as well as Neural Networks, would significantly add to the predictive modelling capacity in the financial sector. Moreover, these models would likewise predict churn based on profitability with respect to delinquent collections and customer engagement. In addition, it will remain important to maintain customer data privacy while conversely developing ML models as predictive models that will, to the best of their abilities, be trustworthy. The use of ML algorithms has been proven to understand customer churn can provide effective and efficient improvements to customer satisfaction levels and subsequent customer loyalty that aiming to retain customers in the marketplace.
