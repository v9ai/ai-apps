# Hybrid Machine Learning Model for Crop Yield Prediction

**Authors.** G. Kumar, K. Nishanth, K. S. Kumar, Allamaprabhu Swamy, M. Jabbar

**Venue / year.** 2025 Fourth International Conference on Smart Technologies and Systems for Next Generation Computing (ICSTSN) · 2025

**Links.** [source](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/0fdc8a1bba85015eed6549b89acbb1be1037b410)

**Abstract.**

Accurate crop yield prediction is an essential aspect of modern agriculture practices, which are increasingly subjected to climate variability that adds uncertainty. A stacked ensemble model made up of Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, XGBoost, Support Vector Regression, and ElasticNet is proposed in this research study to enhance the accuracy of forecasts. The model, using a dataset that simulates actual farming conditions comprising weather, soil nutrients, and pesticide application, incorporates the advantages of different algorithms through a stacking technique. Using this methodology, the meta-learner is able to counterbalance the model-specific biases and thus, become more responsive to the changing growing conditions. The ensemble reached an $\mathbf{R}^{\mathbf{2}}$ score of 0.932, having the lowest RMSE (0.46) and MAE (0.32) among all the models tested, which is the proof of excellent predictive capability and consistency. This paper not only has technical benefits but also an important societal impact: providing farmers with yield predictions that are reliable could lead to better management of resources, better planning and decision-making, even in the long term. The proposed model is a significant advance towards more datadriven and climate-resilient agriculture.
