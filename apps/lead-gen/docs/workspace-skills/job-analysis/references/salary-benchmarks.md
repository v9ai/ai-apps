# Salary Benchmarks

Reference data for evaluating compensation in remote tech roles.

> **Note**: These are approximate ranges as of 2026 for remote workers worldwide. Actual salaries vary by country, experience, company size, and market conditions.

## Red Flags

### Suspiciously Low

If salary is >30% below these ranges, investigate:

- Is it a legitimate junior role with growth path?
- Is it part-time or contract?
- Is the company in a low-cost market?
- Could it be a scam or low-quality posting?

### Unrealistic Combinations

- Junior role offering senior-level salary (too good to be true?)
- Senior role below mid-level salary (undervaluing?)
- Excessive requirements for the compensation level

## Company Size Patterns

### Startups (Pre-Series A)

- Often lower base salary
- May offer equity compensation
- Higher risk, potential upside

### Scale-ups (Series A-C)

- Competitive salaries
- Some equity still available
- Growing benefits

### Established Companies

- Market-rate or above salaries
- Comprehensive benefits
- Less equity upside

### Big Tech (FAANG equivalent)

- Often 20-40% above market
- Significant equity compensation
- Premium benefits

## Currency Notes

Most remote jobs posted in:

- **EUR (€)**: Most common
- **USD ($)**: US companies, usually higher nominal but check conversion
- **GBP (£)**: UK companies
- **CHF**: Swiss companies, highest salaries

Always verify:

- Annual vs monthly (some countries list monthly)
- Gross vs net (always assume gross unless stated)
- Total compensation vs base (equity, bonuses separate?)

## Tips for Agents

1. **Context matters**: Don't judge salary in isolation
2. **Check currency**: €80k ≠ $80k ≠ £80k
3. **Consider location**: Spain salary ≠ Switzerland salary
4. **Equity counts**: Especially for startups
5. **Flag extremes**: Very high or very low needs explanation
