# Conversation Examples

Real-world examples of effective preference gathering conversations.

## Example 1: Confident User

**Agent:** What kind of remote role are you looking for?

**User:** I'm a senior backend engineer, looking for fully remote positions in Europe. I specialize in Node.js and Python.

**Agent:** Great! Let me capture that. You're looking for senior backend roles, fully remote from Europe, with Node.js and Python. A few follow-ups:

1. Any specific countries or regions you prefer?
2. Do you have a minimum salary in mind?
3. Any particular company size or stage you're interested in?

**User:** Germany or Netherlands preferred. I'd like at least €100k. And I prefer scale-ups over early startups.

**Agent:** Perfect! Let me confirm what I've gathered:

- **Role**: Senior Backend Engineer
- **Location**: Remote (Germany, Netherlands preferred)
- **Tech**: Node.js, Python
- **Salary**: Minimum €100k
- **Company**: Scale-ups preferred

Does that sound right?

**User:** Yes, exactly!

**Agent:** Excellent! Your preferences are saved. I'll use these to find matching jobs. You can update them anytime. Ready to see some opportunities?

## Example 2: Uncertain User

**Agent:** What kind of remote role are you looking for?

**User:** I'm not really sure. I just know I want to work remotely.

**Agent:** That's totally fine! Let's start with what you _don't_ want. Are there things from previous jobs you'd like to avoid?

**User:** Well, I don't want to work weekends anymore, and I hated project management. I prefer coding.

**Agent:** Got it - coding-focused role, no project management, normal work hours. What kind of coding have you done before?

**User:** Mostly JavaScript and React. Some backend stuff with Node.

**Agent:** Nice! So you're a full-stack developer with React and Node.js experience. Are you looking to stay full-stack, or focus more on frontend or backend?

**User:** Probably frontend, I like building UIs.

**Agent:** Great! Now, experience level - would you say junior, mid-level, or senior?

**User:** Mid-level, I think. I have about 3 years experience.

**Agent:** Perfect. Let me summarize what we have so far:

- **Role**: Mid-level Frontend Engineer (full-stack background)
- **Tech**: React, JavaScript, Node.js
- **Remote**: Fully remote
- **Work-life balance**: No weekend work

Does that sound right?

**User:** Yes! That's exactly what I'm looking for.

**Agent:** Awesome! Two more quick questions:

1. Any location requirements? (Specific countries, regions, or anywhere?)
2. Any minimum salary you need?

**User:** Remote worldwide would be great, I'm in Spain. Salary... maybe €50k?

**Agent:** Perfect! I've captured everything:

- **Role**: Mid-level Frontend Engineer
- **Location**: Remote (based in Spain)
- **Tech**: React, JavaScript, Node.js
- **Salary**: ~€50k minimum
- **Work-life balance**: Normal hours, no weekends

Your preferences are saved! Ready to explore some jobs?

## Example 3: Contradictions to Resolve

**Agent:** What kind of remote role are you looking for?

**User:** I want a junior developer role. I expect around €120k and I want to lead a team.

**Agent:** Thanks for sharing! I want to make sure I understand correctly. You mentioned:

- Junior developer role
- €120k salary
- Leading a team

These usually point to different experience levels. Could you help me understand:

- How many years of professional experience do you have?
- Have you led teams before?

**User:** Oh, I have 8 years experience and I've led a team of 3. I guess I should say senior, not junior.

**Agent:** That makes much more sense! So you're looking for a senior or lead developer role where you can manage a team, with an €120k target. Is that right?

**User:** Exactly!

**Agent:** Great! Let's continue from there. What's your tech stack?

## Example 4: Gradual Refinement

**Agent:** What kind of remote role are you looking for?

**User:** Something in tech, remote.

**Agent:** Sure! "Tech" covers a lot - are you thinking engineering, product, design, data, or something else?

**User:** Engineering, I guess.

**Agent:** Okay! What kind of engineering - frontend, backend, full-stack, DevOps, mobile?

**User:** I do both frontend and backend, so full-stack.

**Agent:** Perfect! What technologies do you work with?

**User:** JavaScript mostly. React on frontend, Node on backend.

**Agent:** Nice stack! Are you looking for roles that specifically use React and Node, or are you open to other technologies?

**User:** I'd prefer React and Node, but I'm open to learning new things.

**Agent:** Great attitude! So React/Node preferred but open to others. Now, what level - junior, mid, senior?

**User:** I have 2 years experience, so I think mid-level?

**Agent:** With 2 years, you're typically at the mid-level mark, perfect. Let me summarize:

- **Role**: Mid-level Full-Stack Engineer
- **Tech**: React, Node.js (preferred), open to learning
- **Experience**: 2 years
- **Remote**: Yes

Any location requirements?

**User:** I'm in Portugal, so remote worldwide would be ideal.

**Agent:** Excellent! And salary expectations?

**User:** Not really sure what's fair for my level.

**Agent:** For a mid-level full-stack engineer in Portugal, typical range is €40k-65k. Does that help? What feels right for you?

**User:** I'd like at least €50k.

**Agent:** Perfect! Here's your complete profile:

- **Role**: Mid-level Full-Stack Engineer
- **Location**: Remote (based in Portugal)
- **Tech**: React, Node.js (preferred), open to learning
- **Salary**: €50k+ minimum
- **Experience**: 2 years

All set! Want to see some matching jobs?

## Key Techniques Demonstrated

### 1. Start Broad, Then Narrow

Don't ask about specific frameworks before knowing the role type.

### 2. Use Their Uncertainty

"I'm not sure" → Ask what they _don't_ want instead

### 3. Provide Context for Decisions

Help with salary ranges, experience levels, etc.

### 4. Catch Contradictions Gently

"I want to make sure I understand..." not "That doesn't make sense"

### 5. Confirm Incrementally

Don't wait until the end - confirm as you go

### 6. Build on Previous Answers

Each question should flow naturally from the last

### 7. Offer Examples

"Like frontend, backend, or full-stack?" helps users categorize themselves

### 8. Reflect Their Language

If they say "UI building" → use "Frontend" but acknowledge their terms
