Forget trying to make every dev team “elite.” The real goal of platform engineering is to make the slowest teams less slow.

The CNCF Platform Engineering Metrics whitepaper reframes success: it’s not about the average. It’s about **reducing variance**. A healthy platform moves the bulk of teams toward a reliable, predictable baseline, shrinking the gap between your fastest and slowest services. This isn’t about holding anyone back—it’s about reducing systemic risk and creating true organizational predictability.

Here’s the data-driven shift you need to make:

- Stop reporting just average DORA metrics. Start tracking the **standard deviation** of your Lead Time for Changes across teams.
- Measure progress by how the **bottom quartile** of teams improves, not just how the top performers shine.
- Prioritize roadmap items that reduce friction for your **lowest-adopting teams**. Their pain points are your platform's true north.

A platform’s value is in compressing the performance distribution, not chasing a uniform elite threshold that ignores context.
