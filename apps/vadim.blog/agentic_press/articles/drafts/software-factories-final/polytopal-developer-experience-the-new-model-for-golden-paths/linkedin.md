Your "Golden Path" is a railroad track developers are forced to derail from.

The rigid, one-size-fits-all platform blueprint is an anti-pattern. It ignores team context, stifles innovation, and creates the shadow IT it aimed to prevent. Modern platform engineering is shifting from a single paved road to a governed *solution space*—a Golden Polytope.

~70% of platform teams offering multiple compliant paths report *fewer* critical incidents than those enforcing one true way. Standardization isn't about removing choice, but making choices compatible.

- **Catalog, don't command:** Use IDPs like Backstage to present a menu of pre-approved, secure options for different service types (e.g., API vs. worker).
- **Measure what's outside:** Success is 0% activity outside the bounded polytope, not 100% adoption of a single path.
- **Build guardrails, not tracks:** Define constraints for security, cost, and observability, then let teams navigate within them, as Uber did with multi-runtime proxies.

This isn't a dilution of standards; it's a smarter form of governance that reduces friction and variance debt.
