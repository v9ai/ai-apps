# SEO Strategy: WebAssembly Server-Side Computing

## Target Keywords
| Keyword | Volume | Difficulty | Intent | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| webassembly server side | Medium | Medium | Informational | P1 |
| wasm vs containers | Medium | Low | Informational | P1 |
| wasm cold start performance | Low | Low | Informational | P2 |
| fermyon spin review | Low | Low | Commercial | P2 |
| wasi preview 2 | Low | Low | Informational | P3 |

## Search Intent
Developers searching for these terms want to understand whether Wasm is production-ready for server-side use and how it compares to their current container setup. Primary intent is informational with a decision-making component.

## Competitive Landscape
| Competing Article | Angle | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| "Wasm is the future" blog posts | Hype-driven, no data | Missing production benchmarks |
| Docker documentation | Feature-focused | No comparison with alternatives |
| Cloud provider tutorials | Vendor-specific | No cross-platform perspective |

## Recommended Structure
- **Format**: Analysis / data-driven comparison
- **Word count**: 1200-1800
- **Title tag**: "WebAssembly vs Containers: Cold Start Data Changes the Equation"
- **Meta description**: "Fermyon Spin processes cold starts in under 1ms vs 200-500ms for containers. Here's what production Wasm data reveals about the future of server-side computing."
- **H1**: WebAssembly's Server-Side Moment: What the Cold Start Data Actually Shows
- **H2s**:
  1. The Cold Start Gap Nobody's Talking About
  2. How Wasm Differs from Containers Architecturally
  3. Production Numbers from Fastly and Fermyon
  4. WASI Preview 2: The Standardization Milestone
  5. What's Still Holding Wasm Back
  6. When to Choose Wasm Over Containers

## Content Gaps
Existing coverage is either hype-driven without data or vendor-specific tutorials. No article combines production benchmarks from multiple providers with an honest assessment of limitations.
