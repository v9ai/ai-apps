Mandatory office policies aren't just outdated—they're a strategic failure for AI startups. The data shows they cripple your access to the only resource that matters: elite, global talent.

The romantic myth of the "hallway breakthrough" is survivorship bias. Research in *Nature Human Behaviour* confirms remote teams produce breakthrough innovation at the same rate as colocated ones. For AI, the real circulatory system is digital: arXiv, GitHub, and open-source forums.

The core advantage isn't just flexibility; it's a fundamental redesign of how innovation scales.

Here’s what the evidence supports for AI companies:
→ **Adopt a remote-first default.** Design all processes for a distributed team from day one. The office is an option, not a mandate.
→ **Invest in digital context, not real estate.** Build a rich, searchable wiki and decision log. This creates durable institutional knowledge that outlasts any hallway conversation.
→ **Protect deep work.** Async practices and documented decisions prevent the constant context-switching that kills focused model development.
→ **Hire from the global talent pool.** Compete on mission, not relocation packages. The best ML engineer for your role likely doesn’t live in your city.

Building the future of work while mandating a 20th-century office model is myopic. True innovation scales with intentional design, not mandated presence.
