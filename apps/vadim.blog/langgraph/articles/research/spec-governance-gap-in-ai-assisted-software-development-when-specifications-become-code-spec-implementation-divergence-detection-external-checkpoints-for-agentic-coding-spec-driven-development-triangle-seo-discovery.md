## Search Intent
The primary searchers are software engineering leads, technical architects, and senior developers who are actively integrating AI coding assistants (like GitHub Copilot, Cursor, or Claude Code) into their team's workflow. Their intent is informational and decision-oriented: they are searching for solutions to a newly emerging problem. They have observed that when AI agents generate or modify code directly from natural language prompts or specifications, there is a critical "governance gap"—a lack of reliable, automated processes to ensure the final code correctly and completely implements the original spec. They want to learn about frameworks, methodologies, or tools (like "external checkpoints") to detect spec-implementation divergence and govern "agentic coding" processes. The outcome they desire is a practical understanding of how to establish quality control in an AI-augmented development lifecycle. The best content format is a comprehensive, expert-led guide or long-form article that blends conceptual models (like the "spec-development triangle") with actionable strategies.

## Content Differentiation
The typical treatment of "AI-assisted development" focuses on productivity gains, prompt tips, or tool comparisons. The critical gap is the lack of serious discussion on the systemic *quality and governance risks* introduced when specifications are directly executed by non-deterministic agents. This article must fill that gap by shifting the perspective from "how to code faster with AI" to "how to build a reliable, auditable software production line with AI agents." This requires real expertise in software quality assurance, formal methods (or lightweight alternatives), and systems thinking to propose the "spec-development triangle" (balancing spec clarity, agent capability, and verification rigor) and practical solutions for divergence detection that go beyond manual review.
