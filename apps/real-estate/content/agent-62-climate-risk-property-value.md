# Landscape Survey: Climate Risk Impact on Property Values

## **1. Flood Risk → Property Value Discount Modeling**

### **Empirical Evidence & Methodologies**
- **Hedonic Pricing Models**: Traditional approach using property characteristics and flood zone indicators
- **Difference-in-Differences (DiD)**: Comparing price trends in flood-prone vs non-flood areas
- **Spatial Econometrics**: Accounting for spatial autocorrelation in flood risk effects
- **Event Studies**: Price impacts before/after flood events and FEMA map updates

### **Key Findings from Literature**
- **Flood Zone Discounts**: Typically 5-15% for properties in 100-year floodplains
- **Information Effects**: Price impacts increase after flood events and map revisions
- **Insurance Effects**: Mandatory flood insurance requirements amplify discounts
- **Heterogeneity**: Effects vary by property type, location, and market conditions

### **Advanced Modeling Approaches**
- **Machine Learning Extensions**: Random forests, gradient boosting for non-linear effects
- **Bayesian Methods**: Incorporating uncertainty in flood risk projections
- **Dynamic Models**: Time-varying flood risk impacts
- **Integrated Assessment Models**: Combining physical and economic models

## **3. Sea Level Rise — Long-term Value Impact and Coastal Property Repricing**

- **Behavioral Biases**: Myopia, optimism bias, and salience effects
- **Option Value Theory**: Real options approach to coastal development

### **Empirical Evidence**
- **Tidal Flooding Effects**: Nuisance flooding impacts on property values
- **Elevation Premiums**: Price differences by elevation in coastal areas
- **Insurance Market Signals**: NFIP rate changes and private market responses
- **Municipal Bond Effects**: Credit rating impacts for coastal communities

### **Forward-Looking Models**
- **Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)**: Incorporating future flood risk
- **Stochastic Models**: Monte Carlo simulations of SLR scenarios
- **Spatial-Temporal Models**: Dynamic price diffusion patterns
- **Agent-Based Models**: Simulating market participant behavior
