## 1. Multi-Agent Educational Environments: Architectures and Use Cases

### 1.1 Evolution of Multi-Agent Architectures (2018-2026)

**Diana-Margarita Córdova-Esparza (2025)** [AI-Powered Educational Agents: Opportunities, Innovations, and Ethical Challenges](https://doi.org/10.3390/info16060469) provides a systematic review of 82 peer-reviewed and industry studies from January 2023 to February 2025, revealing that modern multi-agent educational systems have evolved from simple rule-based systems to sophisticated LLM-powered architectures with specialized agent roles.

**Tianfu Wang (2025)** [LLM-powered Multi-agent Framework for Goal-oriented Learning in Intelligent Tutoring System](https://doi.org/10.35542/osf.io/gt95h_v1) demonstrates GenMentor, an advanced framework that includes multiple specialized agents for goal-to-skill mapping, learning path optimization, and content tailoring, representing the state-of-the-art in multi-agent ITS.

### 1.2 Specialized Agent Roles and Coordination

**Yanghee Kim & Amy L. Baylor (2015)** [Research-Based Design of Pedagogical Agent Roles: a Review, Progress, and Recommendations](https://doi.org/10.1007/s40593-015-0055-y) established foundational principles that continue to inform modern multi-agent systems, with contemporary implementations including:
- **Tutor Agents**: Subject matter experts providing instruction
- **Companion Agents**: Emotional support and motivation
- **Evaluator Agents**: Assessment and feedback provision
- **Orchestrator Agents**: Coordination and resource management

### 1.3 Emerging Use Cases (2024-2026)

**Petr Špecián (2024)** [Digital Homunculi and Institutional Design: Breaking Through the Experimentation Bottleneck](https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.00826) introduces GenAI-powered agents role-playing humans in diverse institutional settings, enabling complex educational simulations previously limited by human subject constraints.

**Rok Gabrovšek & David Rihtaršič (2025)** [Custom Generative Artificial Intelligence Tutors in Action: An Experimental Evaluation of Prompt Strategies in STEM Education](https://doi.org/10.3390/su17219508) demonstrates configurable AI tutoring tools in electrical engineering laboratories, showing how multi-agent systems can provide personalized support in hands-on learning environments.

### 1.4 Technical Implementation Frameworks

**Jing Nan (2025)** [Z-SPACE: A MULTI-AGENT TOOL ORCHESTRATION FRAMEWORK FOR ENTERPRISE-GRADE LLM AUTOMATION](https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.5896270) presents enterprise-grade orchestration frameworks that enable scalable deployment of multi-agent educational systems across institutional contexts.
