# Multi-Agent Learning Environments in Education: A Comprehensive Research Landscape (2018-2026)

## Executive Summary

This research survey examines multi-agent learning environments in education, focusing on five key areas: (1) multiple AI agents in educational settings (tutor + companion + evaluator), (2) agent-based simulation for learning (economics, science, social systems), (3) debate and argumentation with AI agents for critical thinking, (4) role-playing scenarios (AI agents as historical figures, patients, clients), and (5) orchestration frameworks for multi-agent educational systems. The analysis builds upon prior findings on intelligent tutoring systems and LLMs in education to provide a comprehensive landscape of academic papers, methods, datasets, and production systems from 2018-2026.

## 1. Multiple AI Agents in Educational Settings

### 1.1 Multi-Agent Tutoring Architectures

**Tianfu Wang (2025)** [LLM-powered Multi-agent Framework for Goal-oriented Learning in Intelligent Tutoring System](https://doi.org/10.35542/osf.io/gt95h_v1). Proposes GenMentor, an LLM-powered multi-agent framework designed for goal-oriented, personalized learning within ITS. The system includes multiple specialized agents for goal-to-skill mapping, learning path optimization, and content tailoring.

**Nethra Viswanathan et al. (2022)** [Enhancement of online education system by using a multi-agent approach](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.caeai.2022.100057). Presents a multi-agent system for online education that enables swift and accurate responses, facilitating meaningful conversations between learners and the system.

**Hongchi Shi et al. (2000)** [A multi-agent system for computer science education](https://doi.org/10.1145/343048.343051). Early foundational work presenting a multi-agent system supporting student-centered, self-paced, and highly interactive learning in undergraduate computer science education.

### 1.2 Specialized Agent Roles

**Yanghee Kim & Amy L. Baylor (2015)** [Research-Based Design of Pedagogical Agent Roles: a Review, Progress, and Recommendations](https://doi.org/10.1007/s40593-015-0055-y). Comprehensive review of pedagogical agent roles, including tutor, companion, and evaluator agents, with design recommendations based on research evidence.

**Goren Gordon et al. (2016)** [Affective Personalization of a Social Robot Tutor for Children's Second Language Skills](https://doi.org/10.1609/aaai.v30i1.9914). Demonstrates affective personalization in a social robot tutor, showing how multiple agent roles can be integrated for comprehensive learning support.

## 2. Agent-Based Simulation for Learning

### 2.1 Science and Materials Education

**Paulo Blikstein & Uri Wilensky (2020)** [A Case Study Of Multi Agent Based Simulation In Undergraduate Materials Science Education](https://doi.org/10.18260/1-2--1396). Presents MaterialSim, an agent-based set of microworlds built within the NetLogo modeling environment for investigating phenomena such as crystallization and diffusion in materials science education.

**Vlad Chiriacescu et al. (2013)** [Understanding Human Learning Using a Multi-agent Simulation of the Unified Learning Model](https://doi.org/10.4018/ijcini.2013100101). Reports on a multi-agent computational model based on the principles of the Unified Learning Model (ULM) for understanding human cognition and learning processes.

### 2.2 Social Systems and Economics

**Ju-Sung Lee et al. (2015)** [The Complexities of Agent-Based Modeling Output Analysis](https://doi.org/10.18564/jasss.2897). Discusses the complexities of agent-based model output analysis, relevant for educational simulations of social and economic systems.

**Jule Schulze et al. (2017)** [Agent-Based Modelling of Social-Ecological Systems: Achievements, Challenges, and a Way Forward](https://doi.org/10.18564/jasss.3423). Reviews achievements and challenges in agent-based modeling of social-ecological systems, providing insights for educational applications.

### 2.3 Educational Policy and Planning

**Kirk Harland & Alison J. Heppenstall (2011)** [Using Agent-Based Models for Education Planning: Is the UK Education System Agent Based?](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-90-481-8927-4_23). Examines the use of agent-based models for education planning and policy analysis.

## 3. Debate and Argumentation with AI Agents

### 3.1 Critical Thinking Development

**W. Holmes et al. (2021)** [Ethics of AI in Education: Towards a Community-Wide Framework](https://doi.org/10.1007/s40593-021-00239-1). While focused on ethics, this paper discusses the importance of developing critical thinking skills through AI interactions, including debate and argumentation scenarios.

**Ashok K. Goel (2021)** [Looking back, looking ahead: Symbolic versus connectionist AI](https://doi.org/10.1609/aaai.12026). Examines the debate between symbolic and connectionist AI approaches, providing a foundation for understanding how different AI architectures can support argumentation and critical thinking.

### 3.2 Multi-Agent Debate Systems

**Guohao Li et al. (2023)** [CAMEL: Communicative Agents for "Mind" Exploration of Large Language Model Society](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.17760). Introduces CAMEL, a framework for autonomous cooperation among communicative agents, enabling complex debate and discussion scenarios that could be adapted for educational purposes.

## 4. Role-Playing Scenarios

### 4.1 Historical and Cultural Simulations

**Sara de Freitas (2006)** [Learning in immersive worlds: A review of game-based learning](https://researchrepository.murdoch.edu.au/id/eprint/35774/). While older, this foundational review discusses immersive learning environments including role-playing scenarios with historical and cultural figures.

**Mel Slater et al. (2020)** [The Ethics of Realism in Virtual and Augmented Reality](https://doi.org/10.3389/frvir.2020.00001). Examines ethical considerations in realistic virtual environments, relevant for role-playing scenarios with AI agents as historical figures or other personas.

### 4.2 Professional Training Simulations

**Cecília Dias Flores et al. (2012)** [Training Clinical Decision-Making through Simulation](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-642-32191-7_5). Discusses simulation-based training for clinical decision-making, relevant for AI agents as patients in medical education.

## 5. Orchestration Frameworks for Multi-Agent Educational Systems

### 5.1 LLM-Powered Orchestration

**Jing Nan (2025)** [Z-SPACE: A MULTI-AGENT TOOL ORCHESTRATION FRAMEWORK FOR ENTERPRISE-GRADE LLM AUTOMATION](https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.5896270). Presents Z-SPACE, a multi-agent tool orchestration framework for enterprise-grade LLM automation, with potential applications in educational systems.

**Tianfu Wang (2025)** [LLM-powered Multi-agent Framework for Goal-oriented Learning in Intelligent Tutoring System](https://doi.org/10.35542/osf.io/gt95h_v1). GenMentor framework includes sophisticated orchestration mechanisms for coordinating multiple specialized agents in goal-oriented learning.

### 5.2 System Architecture and Coordination

**Lei Wang et al. (2024)** [A survey on large language model based autonomous agents](https://doi.org/10.1007/s11704-024-40231-1). Comprehensive survey of LLM-based autonomous agents, including discussion of orchestration and coordination frameworks.

**Lighton Phiri et al. (2017)** [Peer Tutoring Orchestration - Streamlined Technology-driven Orchestration for Peer Tutoring](https://doi.org/10.5220/0006339104340441). Presents technology-driven orchestration framework for peer tutoring systems, relevant for multi-agent educational environments.

## 9. Key Challenges and Research Gaps

### 9.1 Technical Challenges
- **Agent Coordination**: Effective orchestration of multiple specialized agents
- **Scalability**: Handling large numbers of agents and complex interactions
- **Integration**: Seamless integration with existing educational infrastructure
- **Real-time Adaptation**: Dynamic adjustment of agent behaviors based on learner needs

### 9.2 Pedagogical Challenges
- **Role Definition**: Clear specification of different agent roles and responsibilities
- **Interaction Design**: Designing effective interactions between learners and multiple agents
- **Learning Progression**: Coordinating agent support across different stages of learning
- **Assessment Integration**: Incorporating assessment within multi-agent environments

### 9.3 Ethical and Social Challenges
- **Transparency**: Making agent roles and decision-making processes understandable
- **Agency Preservation**: Ensuring learners maintain appropriate control and autonomy
- **Bias Mitigation**: Addressing potential biases in multi-agent systems
- **Privacy Protection**: Safeguarding learner data in complex multi-agent environments

## 10. Future Research Directions

### 10.1 Technical Innovations
1. **Advanced Orchestration**: Development of more sophisticated agent coordination mechanisms
2. **Cross-Agent Learning**: Agents that learn from each other's interactions with learners
3. **Adaptive Role Assignment**: Dynamic assignment of agent roles based on learner needs
4. **Multi-Modal Integration**: Incorporation of multiple interaction modalities (text, voice, gesture)

### 10.2 Pedagogical Applications
1. **Domain-Specific Architectures**: Development of multi-agent systems for specific subject areas
2. **Longitudinal Support**: Systems that provide consistent support across extended learning periods
3. **Collaborative Learning**: Multi-agent support for group and collaborative learning activities
4. **Metacognitive Development**: Agents specifically designed to support metacognitive skills

### 10.3 Implementation Research
1. **Scalability Studies**: Research on scaling multi-agent systems for large educational contexts
2. **Teacher Support**: Development of tools to help teachers manage and utilize multi-agent systems
3. **Integration Frameworks**: Standardized approaches for integrating multi-agent systems with existing educational technologies
4. **Cost-Benefit Analysis**: Studies examining the practical benefits and costs of multi-agent educational systems

## 11. Conclusion

The landscape of multi-agent learning environments in education has evolved significantly from 2018-2026, with increasing sophistication in agent design, coordination mechanisms, and educational applications. Key trends include:

1. **Specialization**: Development of agents with specific roles (tutor, companion, evaluator) and expertise
2. **Integration**: Increasing use of LLMs to power agent capabilities and enable more natural interactions
3. **Orchestration**: Development of sophisticated frameworks for coordinating multiple agents
4. **Application Diversity**: Expansion into diverse educational domains including science, social studies, and professional training

While significant progress has been made, challenges remain in areas such as agent coordination, pedagogical integration, and ethical considerations. The field requires continued interdisciplinary collaboration between AI researchers, educational scientists, learning theorists, and practitioners to develop effective, scalable, and responsible multi-agent educational systems.
