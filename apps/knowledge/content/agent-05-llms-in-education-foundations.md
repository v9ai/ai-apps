# Foundational Applications of LLMs in Education: A Comprehensive Research Landscape (2018-2026)

## Executive Summary

This research survey examines the foundational applications of Large Language Models (LLMs) in education across five key domains: (1) LLM-based tutoring systems, (2) prompt engineering for teaching, (3) conversational pedagogy, (4) content generation, and (5) risks and limitations. The analysis covers academic papers, methods, datasets, and production systems from 2018-2026, providing a comprehensive landscape of AI/ML applications for learning and studying.

## 1. LLM-Based Tutoring Systems

### 1.1 Foundational Research

**Bond et al. (2024)** [A meta systematic review of artificial intelligence in higher education: a call for increased ethics, collaboration, and rigour](https://doi.org/10.1186/s41239-023-00436-z). This highly cited meta-review (420 citations) analyzes AI applications in higher education, highlighting the evolution from traditional intelligent tutoring systems to LLM-powered approaches.

**Pesovski et al. (2024)** [Generative AI for Customizable Learning Experiences](https://doi.org/10.3390/su16073034). This influential paper (185 citations) presents an affordable approach to personalized learning using generative AI, demonstrating how LLMs enable truly customizable educational experiences.

**Ogunleye et al. (2024)** [A Systematic Review of Generative AI for Teaching and Learning Practice](https://doi.org/10.3390/educsci14060636). This systematic review (117 citations) provides comprehensive insights into GenAI applications in higher education, including tutoring systems.

### 1.2 Conversational Educational Agents

**Xi et al. (2026)** [Investigating the effects of an LLM-based Socratic conversational agent on students' academic performance and reflective thinking in higher education](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.compedu.2025.105494). **Córdova-Esparza (2025)** [AI-Powered Educational Agents: Opportunities, Innovations, and Ethical Challenges](https://doi.org/10.3390/info16060469). This systematic review of 82 studies analyzes technical and pedagogical frameworks for AI-powered educational agents.

**Modran et al. (2024)** [LLM Intelligent Agent Tutoring in Higher Education Courses using a RAG Approach](https://doi.org/10.20944/preprints202407.0519.v1). Proposes an innovative chatbot tutoring system integrating Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) for customized guidance.
