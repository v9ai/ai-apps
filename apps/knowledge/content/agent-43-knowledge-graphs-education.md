# Comprehensive Landscape of Knowledge Graphs in Education (2018-2026)

## Executive Summary

This research survey provides a comprehensive analysis of knowledge graph applications in education, covering educational knowledge graph construction, knowledge graph embeddings for learner modeling, automatic prerequisite extraction, knowledge graph-enhanced recommendation systems, and open educational knowledge graphs. The analysis reveals significant advancements in leveraging structured knowledge representations for personalized learning, adaptive systems, and intelligent educational technologies.

## 1. Educational Knowledge Graph Construction

### 1.1 Core Components and Architecture
Educational knowledge graphs (EduKGs) represent structured knowledge about educational domains, including:
- **Concepts**: Domain-specific knowledge entities
- **Prerequisite relationships**: Hierarchical dependencies between concepts
- **Skills and competencies**: Learning objectives and abilities
- **Learning resources**: Content mapped to concepts
- **Assessment items**: Questions and exercises linked to skills

### 1.2 Construction Methods
**Li et al. (2022)** [MEduKG: A Deep-Learning-Based Approach for Multi-Modal Educational Knowledge Graph Construction](https://doi.org/10.3390/info13020091) presents a deep learning-based approach for constructing multi-modal educational knowledge graphs that incorporate teacher speeches, slides, and their relationships, addressing the limitation of text-only approaches.

**Ain et al. (2023)** [Automatic Construction of Educational Knowledge Graphs: A Word Embedding-Based Approach](https://doi.org/10.3390/info14100526) improves concept extraction and weighting mechanisms by leveraging word embeddings and deep learning techniques, enhancing the accuracy and performance of EduKG construction.

### 1.3 Domain-Specific Construction
**Aliyu et al. (2020)** [Development of Knowledge Graph for University Courses Management](https://doi.org/10.5815/ijeme.2020.02.01) demonstrates knowledge graph applications for university course management, addressing manual course allocation challenges through automated approaches.

## 2. Knowledge Graph Embeddings for Learner Modeling

### 2.1 Embedding Techniques for Educational Data
Knowledge graph embeddings transform educational entities and relationships into continuous vector spaces, enabling:
- **Similarity computation**: Measuring concept proximity
- **Link prediction**: Inferring missing prerequisite relationships
- **Personalized recommendations**: Matching learners with appropriate content

### 2.2 Advanced Embedding Approaches
**Sun et al. (2023)** [Weighted Heterogeneous Graph-Based Three-View Contrastive Learning for Knowledge Tracing in Personalized e-Learning Systems](https://doi.org/10.1109/tce.2023.3293953) introduces weighted heterogeneous graph-based contrastive learning for knowledge tracing, addressing interaction sparsity in educational resources.

**Li & Wang (2023)** [Knowledge Graph-Enhanced Intelligent Tutoring System Based on Exercise Representativeness and Informativeness](https://doi.org/10.1155/2023/2578286) develops knowledge graph-enhanced tutoring systems that consider exercise representativeness and informativeness, moving beyond single-relationship knowledge graphs.

### 2.3 Hybrid Modeling Approaches
**Chen et al. (2022)** [DCKT: A Novel Dual-Centric Learning Model for Knowledge Tracing](https://doi.org/10.3390/su142316307) explores a dual-centric learning paradigm that considers both knowledge and learner characteristics, addressing the coupling influence often ignored in traditional knowledge tracing methods.

## 3. Prerequisite Learning and Automatic Extraction

### 3.1 Prerequisite Relation Learning
**Pan et al. (2017)** [Prerequisite Relation Learning for Concepts in MOOCs](https://doi.org/10.18653/v1/p17-1133) investigates automatic inference of prerequisite relations between knowledge concepts in MOOCs, leveraging representation learning methods to uncover potential prerequisite relationships.

**Liang et al. (2015)** [Measuring Prerequisite Relations Among Concepts](https://doi.org/10.18653/v1/d15-1193) proposes reference distance (RefD) as a link-based metric for measuring prerequisite relations among concepts, effectively modeling how differently two concepts refer to each other.

### 3.2 Extraction from Educational Texts
Automatic prerequisite extraction techniques include:
- **Text mining approaches**: Pattern-based extraction from educational content
- **Co-occurrence analysis**: Statistical methods for identifying concept relationships
- **Deep learning models**: Neural approaches for relation extraction
- **Cross-document analysis**: Leveraging multiple educational resources

## 4. Knowledge Graph-Enhanced Recommendation and Path Planning

### 4.1 Personalized Learning Path Recommendations
**Ngo et al. (2024)** [Personalized Learning Path Recommendations: Fusing Knowledge Graph Embedding, Sequence Mining, and Collaborative Filtering](https://doi.org/10.1109/bigdata62323.2024.10825001) presents a comprehensive approach combining knowledge graph embeddings, sequence mining, and collaborative filtering for personalized learning path recommendations in MOOCs.

**Abu-Rasheed et al. (2025)** [LLM-Assisted Knowledge Graph Completion for Curriculum and Domain Modelling in Personalized Higher Education Recommendations](https://doi.org/10.1109/educon62633.2025.11016377) introduces an innovative approach using large language models for knowledge graph completion in higher education curriculum modeling, enabling personalized learning-path recommendations.

### 4.2 Exercise and Resource Recommendation
**Dong et al. (2023)** [Advanced Mathematics Exercise Recommendation Based on Automatic Knowledge Extraction and Multilayer Knowledge Graph](https://doi.org/10.1109/tlt.2023.3333669) proposes a framework for extracting complex mathematical semantics and providing personalized exercise recommendations using tree-based position encoding and multilayer knowledge graphs.

**Zhang et al. (2024)** [Prediction of Students' Grade by Combining Educational Knowledge Graph and Collaborative Filtering](https://doi.org/10.1109/access.2024.3390675) combines educational knowledge graphs with collaborative filtering for grade prediction, gathering course semantic information as auxiliary information.

### 4.3 Intelligent Question Answering
**Fang et al. (2023)** [An Intelligent Question-Answering Model over Educational Knowledge Graph for Sustainable Urban Living](https://doi.org/10.3390/su15021139) develops question-answering methods over educational knowledge graphs, providing students with immediate coaching and increasing learning interest and productivity.

## 5. Open Educational Knowledge Graphs

### 5.1 Wikidata and DBpedia for Education
**Rizun (2019)** [Knowledge Graph Application in Education: a Literature Review](https://doi.org/10.18778/0208-6018.342.01) examines knowledge graph applications in education, highlighting the potential of open knowledge bases like Wikidata and DBpedia for educational applications.

**Möller et al. (2022)** [Survey on English Entity Linking on Wikidata: Datasets and approaches](https://doi.org/10.3233/sw-212865) provides insights into Wikidata entity linking approaches, relevant for educational applications requiring integration with open knowledge bases.

Open educational knowledge graphs enable:
- **Resource linking**: Connecting educational content with open knowledge bases
- **Cross-platform interoperability**: Enabling data exchange between educational systems
- **Standardization**: Promoting common vocabularies and ontologies
- **Community contributions**: Leveraging crowd-sourced educational knowledge
