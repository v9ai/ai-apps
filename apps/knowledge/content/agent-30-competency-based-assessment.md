# Comprehensive Landscape of Competency-Based Assessment with AI (2018-2026)

## Executive Summary

This research survey provides a comprehensive analysis of AI-enhanced competency-based assessment across five key domains: (1) competency frameworks and ontologies for skills mapping, (2) evidence-centered design with AI-collected evidence, (3) performance-based assessment through simulations and project evaluation, (4) micro-credentials and digital badges with AI verification, and (5) portfolio assessment with automated rubric application. The analysis reveals significant advancements in AI-driven assessment systems, with particular emphasis on personalized learning pathways, automated evidence collection, and blockchain-verified credentials.

## 1. Competency Frameworks and Ontologies for Skills Mapping

### 1.1 Ontology Development for Skills Representation
**Shalaleh Rismani and AJung Moon (2023)** [What does it mean to be a responsible AI practitioner: An ontology of roles and skills](https://doi.org/10.1145/3600211.3604702) presents a comprehensive ontology for AI ethics competencies, mapping specific skills to roles in responsible AI development. This work demonstrates how ontologies can formalize competency relationships and enable automated skill gap analysis.

**Sondoss Elsawah et al. (2023)** [A Competency Framework for Participatory Modeling](https://doi.org/10.1007/s10726-023-09818-0) develops a structured competency framework for participatory modeling, identifying core competencies across technical, social, and process dimensions. The framework enables systematic assessment of complex interdisciplinary skills.

### 1.2 Industry-Aligned Competency Mapping
**CC Task Force (2020)** [Computing Curricula 2020](https://doi.org/10.1145/3467967) provides a comprehensive competency framework for computing education, mapping skills to industry requirements and emerging technologies. This work demonstrates how competency frameworks can bridge education-industry gaps.

**ACM Data Science Task Force (2021)** [Computing competencies for undergraduate data science curricula](https://doi.org/10.1145/3453538) establishes competency standards for data science education, providing a structured approach to skills mapping and assessment alignment.

### 1.3 AI-Enhanced Competency Analysis
**Clear et al. (2025)** [AI Integration in the IT Professional Workplace: A Scoping Review and Interview Study with Implications for Education and Professional Competencies](https://doi.org/10.1145/3689187.3709607) examines how AI integration transforms professional competencies in IT, identifying emerging skill requirements and assessment needs.

## 2. Evidence-Centered Design (ECD) with AI-Collected Evidence

### 2.1 Digital-First Assessment Ecosystems
**Thomas E. Langenfeld et al. ### 2.2 Learning Progression Analytics
**Marcus Kubsch et al. **Jiangang Hao et al. The landscape of competency-based assessment with AI has evolved significantly from 2018-2026, with substantial progress in automated evidence collection, performance evaluation, and credential verification.
