## **3. Top 5 Family Strategies to Start THIS WEEK**

### **Strategy 1: Daily "Special Time" (10-15 minutes)**
**What to do**: Uninterrupted, child-directed play with Bogdan
**Why it works**: Builds positive connection, reduces attention-seeking through negative behavior
**Evidence Confidence**: **HIGH** (PCIT research shows strong effects)

### **Strategy 2: Emotion Coaching**
**What to do**: 
1. Notice when Bogdan seems frustrated/angry
2. Label the emotion: "I see you're feeling frustrated"
3. Validate: "It's okay to feel that way"
4. Problem-solve: "What could help you feel better?"
**Why it works**: Teaches emotional regulation, reduces reactive aggression
**Evidence Confidence**: **MODERATE** (Emotion socialization research)

### **Strategy 3: Clear Family Rules About Respect**
**What to do**: 
- Create 3-5 simple rules (e.g., "We speak kindly about others")

**What to do**:

- Read together daily: "What would you do if you saw someone being left out?"
- Role-play positive responses
**Why it works**: Builds perspective-taking skills
**Evidence Confidence**: **MODERATE** (Social skills training evidence)

### **Strategy 5: School-Home Communication System**
**What to do**:

## **4. Red Flags Signaling Need for Professional Referral**

### **Immediate Referral (Within 24-48 hours)**
- **Physical aggression** toward peers or adults
- **Threats of harm** to self or others
- **School refusal** due to anxiety or fear
- **Significant deterioration** in academic performance

### **Urgent Referral (Within 1 week)**
- **Behavior escalates** despite initial interventions
- **Social isolation** increases (no positive peer interactions)
- **Emotional distress** signs: frequent crying, sleep changes, appetite changes
- **Family conflict** intensifies around the issue

### **Specialist Referral Considerations**
1. **Child Psychologist**: For comprehensive assessment if behaviors persist 4+ weeks
2. **Family Therapist**: If family dynamics contribute to or maintain the behavior
3. **School Psychologist**: For school-based intervention planning
4. **Psychiatric Evaluation**: If comorbid conditions suspected (ADHD, anxiety, depression)
