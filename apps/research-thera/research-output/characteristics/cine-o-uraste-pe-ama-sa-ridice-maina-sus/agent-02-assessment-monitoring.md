# Validated Assessment Tools and Progress Monitoring for Bullying and Peer Relationship Issues

## **1. Standardized Instruments with Psychometric Properties**

### **A. Strengths and Difficulties Questionnaire (SDQ)**
- **Description**: 25-item behavioral screening questionnaire for children aged 4-17
- **Relevant Subscales**: 
  - Peer Problems (5 items): Assesses peer relationships, social isolation, bullying
  - Emotional Symptoms: Anxiety, depression
  - Conduct Problems: Aggressive behavior
- **Psychometric Properties**:
  - **Reliability**: Good internal consistency (α = 0.73-0.82 for Peer Problems)
  - **Validity**: Strong convergent validity with other measures of psychopathology
  - **Normative Data**: Available for multiple countries and age groups
- **Versions**: Parent, teacher, and self-report (ages 11+)
- **Use**: Screening and monitoring changes over time

### **B. Forms of Bullying Scale (FBS)**
- **Description**: Specifically measures bullying victimization (FBS-V) and perpetration (FBS-P)
- **Dimensions**: Physical, verbal, social/relational, cyber bullying
- **Psychometric Properties**:
  - **Reliability**: Good internal consistency (α = 0.78-0.89)
  - **Validity**: Strong construct validity, distinguishes between victimization types
- **Age Range**: Adolescents (middle to high school)

### **C. Olweus Bullying Questionnaire**
- **Description**: Comprehensive assessment of bullying involvement
- **Components**: Victimization, perpetration, bystander behavior
- **Psychometric Properties**: Well-established reliability and validity across cultures
- **Use**: School-wide assessment and intervention planning

## **2. Brief Screening Tools for Repeated Caregiver Measurement**

### **A. SDQ Impact Supplement**
- **Description**: Brief follow-up assessing functional impairment
- **Items**: 5 questions about distress and social/educational impairment
- **Frequency**: Can be administered weekly or bi-weekly
- **Advantage**: Quick (2-3 minutes), sensitive to change

### **B. Peer Victimization Scale - Brief Version**
- **Description**: 6-8 item screening for relational and overt victimization
- **Frequency**: Suitable for weekly monitoring
- **Focus**: Social exclusion, rumor spreading, verbal aggression

### **C. School Connectedness Scale - Short Form**
- **Description**: Measures sense of belonging and safety at school
- **Items**: 4-6 items assessing peer relationships and teacher support
- **Use**: Monitor changes in school climate perceptions

## **3. Behavioral Coding Systems and Observation Protocols**

### **A. Classroom Peer Interaction Observation System**
- **Components**:
  1. **Social Engagement**: Positive vs. negative interactions
  2. **Inclusion/Exclusion**: Group participation dynamics
  3. **Verbal Behavior**: Supportive vs. aggressive comments
  4. **Non-verbal Behavior**: Gestures, facial expressions, proximity
- **Reliability**: Requires training but achieves good inter-rater reliability (κ > 0.70)
- **Use**: Structured classroom observations (15-30 minute intervals)

### **B. Playground Observation of Peer Relations (POPR)**
- **Description**: Naturalistic observation during unstructured time
- **Focus**: Social initiation, response to peers, conflict resolution
- **Coding Categories**: 
  - Prosocial behavior
  - Aggressive behavior  
  - Social withdrawal
  - Leadership/dominance

### **C. Social Network Mapping**
- **Method**: Peer nomination procedures
- **Measures**: 
  - Social centrality
  - Reciprocated friendships
  - Rejection nominations
  - Perceived popularity
- **Validity**: Strong predictive validity for social adjustment

## **4. Digital Assessment Approaches and App-Based Monitoring**

### **A. Ecological Momentary Assessment (EMA)**
- **Method**: Smartphone-based repeated sampling of experiences
- **Frequency**: Multiple times daily for 1-2 weeks
- **Content**: 
  - Social interactions (positive/negative)
  - Emotional states
  - Bullying incidents
  - Coping strategies
- **Advantages**: Real-time data, reduces recall bias

### **B. School Climate Apps**
- **Examples**: 
  - **SchoolPulse**: Weekly mood and school experience check-ins
  - **PeerSafe**: Anonymous reporting of bullying incidents
  - **Mindful Schools**: Daily emotional check-ins with coping tools
- **Features**: 
  - Data visualization for students/parents
  - Alert systems for concerning patterns
  - Connection to school support services

### **C. Digital Diary Methods**
- **Format**: Audio, video, or text entries about daily experiences
- **Analysis**: Qualitative coding or sentiment analysis
- **Benefits**: Captures nuanced social experiences

## **5. Measurement-Based Care Frameworks for Tracking Treatment Response**

### **A. Routine Outcome Monitoring (ROM) Framework**
- **Components**:
  1. **Baseline Assessment**: Comprehensive evaluation using multiple tools
  2. **Progress Monitoring**: Weekly/bi-weekly brief measures
  3. **Feedback System**: Data review with child, family, and school
  4. **Treatment Adjustment**: Modify interventions based on data
- **Tools Integration**: SDQ + specific bullying measures + functional assessment

### **B. Stepped-Care Monitoring Protocol**
- **Level 1**: Universal screening (school-wide SDQ)
- **Level 2**: Targeted assessment (FBS + peer nominations)
- **Level 3**: Intensive monitoring (observation + EMA)
- **Decision Rules**: Clear criteria for moving between levels

### **C. Multi-Informant Progress Tracking**
- **Sources**: 
  - Child self-report (ages 8+)
  - Parent/caregiver reports
  - Teacher reports
  - Peer reports (when appropriate)
- **Integration**: Look for convergence and divergence across sources
- **Frequency**: Monthly comprehensive reviews
