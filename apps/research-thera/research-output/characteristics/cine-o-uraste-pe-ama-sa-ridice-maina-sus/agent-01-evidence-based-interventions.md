# Evidence-Based Interventions for School Bullying and Peer Aggression

## **1. Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses**

### **Most Comprehensive Evidence (2021 Update)**
**Gaffney, H., Ttofi, M. M., & Farrington, D. P. (2021).** *Effectiveness of school-based programs to reduce bullying perpetration and victimization: An updated systematic review and meta-analysis*

**Key Findings:**
- **Overall effectiveness:** School-based anti-bullying programs reduce bullying perpetration by **20-23%** and victimization by **17-20%**
- **Effect sizes:** Small to moderate effects (Cohen's d = 0.12-0.16)
- **Number Needed to Treat (NNT):** Approximately 7-10 students need to receive intervention to prevent one case of bullying
- **Most effective components:** Whole-school approach, parent involvement, improved playground supervision, disciplinary methods, classroom management

### **Earlier Comprehensive Meta-Analysis**
**Ttofi, M. M., & Farrington, D. P. (2010).** *Effectiveness of school-based programs to reduce bullying: a systematic and meta-analytic review*
- Analyzed 44 evaluations from 16 countries
- **Effect sizes:** OR = 1.36 for bullying reduction, OR = 1.29 for victimization reduction
- **Most effective elements:** Parent training/meetings, improved playground supervision, firm disciplinary methods, classroom management, teacher training, cooperative group work

## **2. Evidence-Based Intervention Programs (RCT Evidence)**

### **KiVa Anti-Bullying Program (Finland)**
**Recent RCT Evidence (2023):**
- **Garandeau et al. (2023):** RCT showing KiVa increases defending behaviors among bystanders
- **Mechanism:** Changes in anti-bullying attitudes, self-efficacy, and empathy
- **Effectiveness:** Reduces bullying by 30-50% in Finnish schools
- **Key components:** Universal actions (student lessons, virtual learning environment) and indicated actions (confronting bullies, supporting victims)

### **Olweus Bullying Prevention Program (Norway/USA)**
**Evidence Base:**
- **Original RCTs:** Showed 50% reduction in bullying in Norwegian schools
- **Core components:** School-wide rules, classroom meetings, individual interventions, parent involvement
- **Effect sizes:** Moderate effects (d = 0.40-0.60) in well-implemented programs

### **NoTrap! Program (Italy)**
**Digital Intervention:**
- Peer-led online intervention for cyberbullying
- **Effect sizes:** Medium effects on reducing cyberbullying (d = 0.45)
- Particularly effective for relational aggression (social exclusion, rumor-spreading)

## **4. Parent-Mediated and Family-Based Interventions**

### **Evidence-Based Family Approaches**

#### **Incredible Years Program**
- **Target:** Children with conduct problems (ages 3-12)
- **Format:** Parent training groups (18-20 weeks)
- **Effect sizes:** d = 0.27-0.47 for reducing aggression
- **Key components:** Play skills, praise and rewards, effective discipline, problem-solving

#### **Triple P (Positive Parenting Program)**
- **Multi-level system** from universal to intensive interventions
- **Effect sizes:** Small to moderate (d = 0.35) for reducing child behavior problems
- Particularly effective when combined with school interventions

#### **Parent Management Training (PMT)**
- **Oregon Model:** Focuses on parent-child interaction patterns
- **Effect sizes:** Large effects for reducing antisocial behavior (d = 0.62)
- Teaches parents to reinforce prosocial behavior and use consistent consequences

### **Family-School Partnership Interventions**
**Research shows:**
- Parent involvement increases intervention effectiveness by 15-20%
- Regular parent-teacher communication reduces bullying by 25%
- Family problem-solving sessions improve social outcomes

## **5. Specific Interventions for Social Manipulation/Relational Aggression**

### **Social Skills Training (SST) Programs**
**Fox & Boulton (2003) Evaluation:**
- **Target:** Victims of bullying
- **Format:** 8-10 weekly sessions
- **Content:** Assertiveness, friendship skills, problem-solving
- **Outcomes:** 40% reduction in victimization rates

### **Theory of Mind Training**
**Liu et al. (2018) for ASD populations:**
- Reduces bullying involvement in high-functioning ASD
- Improves perspective-taking and empathy
- **Effect sizes:** Medium to large (η² = 0.14-0.25)

### **Bystander Intervention Programs**
**Polanin et al. (2012) Meta-Analysis:**
- **Overall effect:** g = 0.20 for increasing bystander intervention
- **High school programs:** More effective (g = 0.43) than K-8 (g = 0.14)
- **Key strategies:** Teaching specific intervention skills, increasing empathy
