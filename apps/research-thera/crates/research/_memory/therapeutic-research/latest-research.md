## Papers Reviewed

### [1] A Meta-Analysis on Effects of Parenting Programs on Bullying Prevention (2020, 70 citations)
- **Authors:** Qiqi Chen, Yuhong Zhu, W. Chui
- **Evidence Level:** meta-analysis
- **Relevance:** high
- **Population:** children, families
- **Key Finding:** Parenting programs show significant effects on bullying reduction (d = 0.64), with child- and parent-related factors (parenting style, children's empathy, parent-child interaction about bullying) significantly related to intervention effectiveness.
- **Therapeutic Techniques:** parenting style modification, parent-child communication about bullying, empathy development, parenting skills training, school-family partnerships
- **DOI:** Not available
- **Source:** https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/8411c4d674c5f3e7fb30989911719bbbf687f826

### [2] A Meta-Analytic Review of Preschool Social and Emotional Learning Interventions (2020, 187 citations)
- **Authors:** Dana Murano, J. Sawyer, A. Lipnevich
- **Evidence Level:** meta-analysis
- **Relevance:** high
- **Population:** preschool children
- **Key Finding:** Universal SEL interventions show small-medium effects on social-emotional skill development (g = 0.34) and problem behavior reduction (g = 0.32); targeted interventions show medium effects (g = 0.48).
- **Therapeutic Techniques:** universal SEL curricula, targeted interventions for at-risk children, social-emotional skill building, problem behavior reduction strategies
- **DOI:** Not available
- **Source:** https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/d6d87383394954d52f7b08e6804111ac7c98e62c

### [3] Short-Term Intervention Effects of the PATHS Curriculum in Young Low-Income Children: Capitalizing on Plasticity (2016, 63 citations)
- **Authors:** D. Fishbein, C. Domitrovich, Jason Williams, Stephanie Gitukui, Charles Guthrie, D. Shapiro, M. Greenberg
- **Evidence Level:** RCT
- **Relevance:** high
- **Population:** young children (low-income)
- **Key Finding:** Children receiving PATHS showed significantly greater improvements in social competence and behavioral problems than controls, with improvements in motor inhibition.
- **Therapeutic Techniques:** emotion recognition training, self-control strategies, social problem-solving, friendship skills development, executive function training
- **DOI:** 10.1007/s10935-016-0442-7
- **Source:** https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/9c512101a3fe83b4dd23b47d44c5750805cf336b

### [4] School bullying and group violence: How to occupy a place in the group by exclusion (2022, 6 citations)
- **Authors:** S. Tordjman
- **Evidence Level:** systematic_review
- **Relevance:** high
- **Population:** children, adolescents
- **Key Finding:** Bullying involves group dynamics where exclusion serves to establish social position; interventions should target all actors (harasser, harassed, spectators) through perspective-taking and collective responsibility.
- **Therapeutic Techniques:** role-playing, simulation games, perspective-taking exercises, cognitive-to-emotional empathy transition training, group discussions, collective responsibility building
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.encep.2022.06.004
- **Source:** https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/857399a91479a8aa6f48b4eb53cab9529c16e4b2

### [5] Impact of a school-based social skills training program on parent–child relationships and parent attitudes toward school (2023, 7 citations)
- **Authors:** Rebecca Slotkin, K. Bierman, Linda N. Jacobson
- **Evidence Level:** RCT
- **Relevance:** medium
- **Population:** children (rejected by peers), families
- **Key Finding:** Social skills training improved parent-child closeness and (for older children) parent attitudes toward school, mediated by increased teacher support and peer liking.
- **Therapeutic Techniques:** social skills instruction, peer interaction practice, teacher support enhancement, peer liking strategies, parent-child relationship building
- **DOI:** 10.1177/01650254231198031
- **Source:** https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/159ca12ed74c5a9dec9f9a80312a1d466bc96957

### [6] Examining the Effects of Negotiation and Peer Mediation on Students’ Conflict Resolution and Problem-Solving Skills (2019, 21 citations)
- **Authors:** S. Ay, H. Keskin, M. Akilli
- **Evidence Level:** cohort
- **Relevance:** high
- **Population:** children (grades 4-6)
- **Key Finding:** Negotiation and peer mediation training reduced obliging and avoiding behaviors in conflict resolution, though not overall problem-solving skills.
- **Therapeutic Techniques:** negotiation skills training, peer mediation techniques, conflict resolution strategies, reducing avoiding/obliging behaviors, peaceful conflict resolution
- **DOI:** 10.29333/iji.2019.12343a
- **Source:** https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/92078e16eecda1e3fc55e6535d0a608bdfcbc1cf

### [7] Social and Emotional Learning Associated With Universal Curriculum-Based Interventions in Early Childhood Education and Care Centers (2018, 139 citations)
- **Authors:** Claire Blewitt, M. Fuller‑Tyszkiewicz, A. Nolan, Heidi J Bergmeier, D. Vicary, T. Huang, P. McCabe, Tracey McKay, H. Skouteris
- **Evidence Level:** meta-analysis
- **Relevance:** high
- **Population:** young children
- **Key Finding:** Universal SEL interventions significantly improve social competence, emotional competence, behavioral self-regulation, emotional/behavioral problems, and early learning outcomes.
- **Therapeutic Techniques:** curriculum-based SEL, emotional competence training, behavioral self-regulation strategies, social competence development, emotional literacy
- **DOI:** 10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2018.4726
- **Source:** https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/a7c05b87297f981c73766c9255366b287f158423

### [8] Effectiveness of universal school-based social-emotional learning programs in promoting social-emotional skills, attitudes towards self and others, positive social behaviors, and improving emotional and conduct problems among Japanese children: a meta-analytic review (2023, 14 citations)
- **Authors:** Yuka Takizawa, M. Bambling, Yuki Matsumoto, Y. Ishimoto, S. Edirippulige
- **Evidence Level:** meta-analysis
- **Relevance:** high
- **Population:** children, adolescents (5-18 years)
- **Key Finding:** Universal school-based SEL programs have small overall effects (ES = 0.26) across social-emotional skills, attitudes, positive behaviors, and conduct/emotional problems.
- **Therapeutic Techniques:** social-emotional skills training, attitude towards self/others development, positive social behavior promotion, emotional problem reduction, conduct problem management
- **DOI:** 10.3389/feduc.2023.1228269
- **Source:** https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/81f01ced2881562c7cd7a012597917a670071af0

### [9] The effect of an aggressive behavior prevention program on kindergarten students (2018, 11 citations)
- **Authors:** Arzu Akcan, A. Ergun
- **Evidence Level:** RCT
- **Relevance:** high
- **Population:** kindergarten children
- **Key Finding:** The Aggressive Behavior Prevention Program significantly decreased aggression levels in kindergarten students based on Social Cognitive Theory.
- **Therapeutic Techniques:** Social Cognitive Theory-based intervention, anger management, alternative behavior training, family involvement, overt and relational aggression reduction
- **DOI:** Not available
- **Source:** https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/cef48c3bce6b172dbb3a9c50d390241b8889c934

### [10] Peer Aggression and Conflictual Teacher-Student Relationships: A Meta-Analysis (2022, 23 citations)
- **Authors:** Amanda Krause, J. D. Smith
- **Evidence Level:** meta-analysis
- **Relevance:** medium
- **Population:** children, adolescents
- **Key Finding:** Conflictual teacher-student relationships are significantly associated with peer aggression, suggesting the importance of teacher-student relationship quality in aggression prevention.
- **Therapeutic Techniques:** teacher-student relationship improvement, classroom management strategies, conflict resolution training, teacher mediation skills
- **DOI:** Not available
- **Source:** https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/d64506735476a25618a862fa480a3d298dfd5abe
