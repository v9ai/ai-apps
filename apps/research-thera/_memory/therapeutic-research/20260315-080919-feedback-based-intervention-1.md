## Papers Reviewed

### [1] Improving parenting, child attachment, and externalizing behaviors: Meta-analysis of the first 25 randomized controlled trials on the effects of Video-feedback Intervention to promote Positive Parenting and Sensitive Discipline (2022, 89 citations)
- **Authors:** Marinus H. van IJzendoorn, Carlo Schuengel, Qiang Wang, Marian J. Bakermans-Kranenburg
- **Evidence Level:** meta-analysis
- **Relevance:** high
- **Population:** parents and children (1‑6 years)
- **Key Finding:** VIPP‑SD improves parenting behavior (r = .18), attitudes (r = .16), and child attachment security (r = .23), but not child externalizing behavior (r = .07).
- **Therapeutic Techniques:** video feedback, coaching in sensitive limit‑setting, support of parental sensitive responsiveness
- **DOI:** 10.1017/S0954579421001462
- **Source:** https://doi.org/10.1017/S0954579421001462

### [2] Effects of Classroom Management Intervention Based on Teacher Training and Performance Feedback on Outcomes of Teacher-Student Dyads in Inclusive Classrooms (2015, 55 citations)
- **Authors:** Selma Akalın, Bülbin Sucuoğlu
- **Evidence Level:** single‑subject design (multiple‑probe)
- **Relevance:** high
- **Population:** teachers and students with special needs in inclusive classrooms
- **Key Finding:** Daily performance feedback increased teachers’ use of target classroom‑management skills, raised academic engagement and positive behaviors, and reduced negative behaviors.
- **Therapeutic Techniques:** teacher training in classroom management, daily performance feedback, individualization, transitions management, reinforcement
- **DOI:** 10.12738/estp.2015.3.2543
- **Source:** https://doi.org/10.12738/estp.2015.3.2543

### [3] Teacher coaching supported by formative assessment for improving classroom practices. (2018, 99 citations)
- **Authors:** Gregory A. Fabiano, Linda A. Reddy, Christopher M. Dudek
- **Evidence Level:** RCT
- **Relevance:** high
- **Population:** teachers
- **Key Finding:** Coaching combined with formative assessment significantly improved teachers’ classroom‑management practices.
- **Therapeutic Techniques:** teacher coaching, formative assessment, performance feedback, goal setting
- **DOI:** 10.1037/spq0000223
- **Source:** https://doi.org/10.1037/spq0000223

### [4] Effects of video‑feedback intervention on harmonious parent–child interaction and sensitive discipline of parents with intellectual disabilities: A randomized controlled trial (2017, 25 citations)
- **Authors:** M.W. Hodes, Marieke Meppelder, Marleen H. M. de Moor, et al.
- **Evidence Level:** RCT
- **Relevance:** high
- **Population:** parents with intellectual disabilities and their children
- **Key Finding:** Video‑feedback intervention improved harmonious parent–child interaction and sensitive discipline.
- **Therapeutic Techniques:** video feedback, modeling positive interactions, sensitive‑discipline strategies
- **DOI:** 10.1111/cch.12506
- **Source:** https://doi.org/10.1111/cch.12506

### [5] Attachment‑based intervention for enhancing sensitive discipline in mothers of 1‑ to 3‑year‑old children at risk for externalizing behavior problems: A randomized controlled trial. (2006, 345 citations)
- **Authors:** J. van Zeijl, Judi Mesman, Marinus H. van IJzendoorn, et al.
- **Evidence Level:** RCT
- **Relevance:** high
- **Population:** mothers of young children at risk for externalizing behavior
- **Key Finding:** VIPP‑SD improved maternal sensitive discipline and reduced child externalizing behavior.
- **Therapeutic Techniques:** video feedback, attachment‑based strategies, sensitive‑discipline techniques
- **DOI:** 10.1037/0022‑006X.74.6.994
- **Source:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0022‑006X.74.6.994

### [6] Improving parent–child interactions in maltreating families with the Attachment Video‑feedback Intervention: Parental childhood trauma as a moderator of treatment effects (2020, 20 citations)
- **Authors:** Sabine van der Asdonk, Chantal Cyr, Lenneke R. A. Alink
- **Evidence Level:** RCT
- **Relevance:** high
- **Population:** maltreating families
- **Key Finding:** Attachment video‑feedback improved parent–child interactions, with treatment effects moderated by parental trauma history.
- **Therapeutic Techniques:** attachment video feedback, trauma‑informed approaches, positive parenting
- **DOI:** 10.1080/14616734.2020.1799047
- **Source:** https://doi.org/10.1080/14616734.2020.1799047

### [7] Using Coaching to Support Teacher Implementation of Classroom‑based Interventions (2013, 190 citations)
- **Authors:** Wendy M. Reinke, Melissa Stormont, Keith C. Herman
- **Evidence Level:** systematic review
- **Relevance:** medium
- **Population:** teachers
- **Key Finding:** Coaching improves teacher implementation of classroom‑based interventions.
- **Therapeutic Techniques:** coaching, performance feedback, modeling, problem‑solving
- **DOI:** 10.1007/s10864‑013‑9186‑0
- **Source:** https://doi.org/10.1007/s10864‑013‑9186‑0

### [8] Near‑infrared spectroscopy (NIRS) neurofeedback as a treatment for children with attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD)—a pilot study (2015, 94 citations)
- **Authors:** Anna‑Maria Marx, Ann‑Christine Ehlis, Adrian Furdea, et al.
- **Evidence Level:** pilot study
- **Relevance:** medium
- **Population:** children with ADHD
- **Key Finding:** NIRS neurofeedback is feasible and may reduce ADHD symptoms.
- **Therapeutic Techniques:** neurofeedback, brain‑activity monitoring, reward feedback
- **DOI:** 10.3389/fnhum.2014.01038
- **Source:** https://doi.org/10.3389/fnhum.2014.01038

### [9] Video‑feedback Intervention to promote Positive Parenting adapted to Autism (VIPP‑AUTI): A randomized controlled trial (2014, 133 citations)
- **Authors:** Irina E. Poslawsky, Fabiënne B. A. Naber, Marian J. Bakermans‑Kranenburg, et al.
- **Evidence Level:** RCT
- **Relevance:** medium
- **Population:** children with autism and their parents
- **Key Finding:** VIPP‑AUTI improved parent–child interaction and reduced autism‑related symptoms.
- **Therapeutic Techniques:** video feedback, autism‑specific adaptations, positive parenting
- **DOI:** 10.1177/1362361314537124
- **Source:** https://doi.org/10.1177/1362361314537124

### [10] Further Evidence on Online Video‑Feedback Intervention: Enhancing Parent–Child Interactions and Reducing Disordered Eating in Children (2024, 3 citations)
- **Authors:** Silvia Cimino, Luca Cerniglia
- **Evidence Level:** RCT
- **Relevance:** medium
- **Population:** parents and children with disordered eating
- **Key Finding:** Online video‑feedback improved parent–child interactions and reduced disordered‑eating symptoms.
- **Therapeutic Techniques:** online video feedback, parent training, behavioral strategies
- **DOI:** 10.3390/ijerph21091260
- **Source:** https://doi.org/10.3390/ijerph21091260
