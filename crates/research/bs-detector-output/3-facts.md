# FACTUAL DISCREPANCY ANALYSIS: MOTION FOR SUMMARY JUDGMENT VS. SUPPORTING DOCUMENTS

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
The Motion for Summary Judgment contains **multiple material factual discrepancies** when cross-referenced against the police report, medical records, and witness statement. Key issues involve the incident date, Rivera's PPE, Harmon's control over work, scaffolding conditions, and OSHA compliance claims. Several discrepancies are **CRITICAL** to legal defenses raised.

---

## DETAILED ANALYSIS BY CATEGORY

### 1. **DATES**

**MSJ Claim:**
> "workplace incident on March 14, 2021" (Introduction)
> "incident giving rise to this action occurred on March 14, 2021" (Argument D)

**Supporting Documents:**
- **Police Report:** "Date of Incident: March 12, 2021" (header), "On March 12, 2021, at approximately 10:45 AM" (narrative)
- **Medical Records:** "DATE OF ADMISSION: March 12, 2021" (header), "following a fall from scaffolding... occurring at approximately 10:45 AM on March 12, 2021" (ED notes)
- **Witness Statement:** "incident that occurred on March 12, 2021" (paragraph 1)

**Discrepancy:** MSJ incorrectly states incident date as March 14, 2021 instead of March 12, 2021.
- **Severity:** HIGH (affects statute of limitations calculation and factual accuracy)
- **Confidence:** 100%
- **Legal Significance:** Errors in basic factual predicates undermine credibility. Directly impacts statute of limitations argument (see section 7).

---

### 2. **PPE/SAFETY EQUIPMENT**

**MSJ Claim:**
> "Rivera was not wearing required personal protective equipment at the time of the incident, including fall-arrest equipment mandated by site safety protocols." (Undisputed Fact 4)

**Supporting Documents:**
- **Police Report:** "Rivera was wearing a hard hat and harness consistent with site requirements at the time of the collapse. The harness lanyard was found still attached to Rivera but had pulled free from the scaffolding anchor point when the structure gave way." (Equipment and Safety Gear section)
- **Witness Statement:** "Carlos was wearing his hard hat, safety harness, and high-visibility vest, consistent with site PPE requirements." (paragraph 7)

**Discrepancy:** MSJ claims Rivera wasn't wearing PPE; police report and witness statement confirm he was wearing full PPE including harness.
- **Severity:** CRITICAL (goes to core negligence defense and assumption of risk argument)
- **Confidence:** 100%
- **Legal Significance:** If Rivera was wearing proper PPE as documented, the "assumption of risk" and contributory negligence arguments are substantially weakened. The harness failure due to anchor point collapse suggests equipment inadequacy rather than PPE non-compliance.

---

### 3. **WHO DIRECTED THE WORK**

**MSJ Claim (Implied):**
> "Apex Staffing Solutions — not Harmon — was the employer responsible for scaffolding operations" (Argument A)
> "independent contractor — not the hirer — controls the manner and method of work performance" (Argument A)

**Supporting Documents:**
- **Police Report:** "Donner stated that he had directed Rivera and his crew to begin work on the east-side scaffolding section earlier that morning." (Witness Information section)
- **Witness Statement:** "Ray Donner — the project foreman for Harmon Construction — told us that the east-side scaffolding section needed to be fully operational by end of day... Donner told me, 'We don't have time to re-do the base. It's been fine. Just get up there and get it done.'" (paragraphs 3, 5)

**Discrepancy:** MSJ suggests Harmon had no control; documents show Harmon's foreman actively directed work and overrode safety concerns.
- **Severity:** CRITICAL (directly undermines Privette doctrine defense)
- **Confidence:** 100%
- **Legal Significance:** Under California law, the Privette doctrine does not apply if the hirer retains control over safety conditions or actively directs the manner of work. Hooker v. Department of Transportation, 27 Cal.4th 198 (2002). Donner's direction to work on a specific timeline and dismissal of safety concerns could establish retained control exception.

### 4. **SCAFFOLDING CONDITION**

**MSJ Claim:** 
*Complete omission* - No mention of scaffolding condition in MSJ.

**Supporting Documents:**
- **Police Report:** "The base plates on the failed section appeared to be resting on uneven ground, with one plate sitting on a loose plywood sheet rather than a stable footing. Officers observed that portions of the scaffolding in the affected section showed visible rust and wear." (Scene Description section)
- **Witness Statement:** Detailed concerns about: "significant surface rust," "base plate... sitting on a piece of plywood," "plywood had shifted noticeably," "coupling pins... looked bent or worn." (paragraph 4)

**Discrepancy:** MSJ ignores documented safety hazards; supporting documents detail multiple defects.
- **Severity:** HIGH (material omission regarding cause of incident)
- **Confidence:** 100%
- **Legal Significance:** Evidence of known defective conditions creates potential premises liability for Harmon as property controller/general contractor. Failure to address known hazards could constitute affirmative negligence beyond Privette protection.

### 5. **OSHA COMPLIANCE**

**MSJ Claim:**
> "Harmon maintained an active Injury and Illness Prevention Program ('IIPP') and had passed all OSHA inspections conducted at the site during the relevant period, the most recent being February 26, 2021." (Undisputed Fact 5)

**Supporting Documents:**
- **Police Report:** No mention of OSHA inspections. Documents observable safety violations (uneven base, loose plywood, rust/wear).
- **Witness Statement:** "I hold a competent person certification for scaffold erection and inspection under Cal/OSHA standards." Describes multiple Cal/OSHA violations: improper base (not on mudsill), rusted/bent components, shifting base plate. (paragraphs 1, 4)

**Discrepancy:** MSJ claims perfect OSHA compliance; witness (certified competent person) describes multiple apparent OSHA violations.
- **Severity:** HIGH (undermines "presumption of reasonable care" argument)
- **Confidence:** 90% (witness is certified; observations specific)
- **Legal Significance:** OSHA compliance creates presumption of reasonable care (Kellerman). Evidence of violations rebuts this presumption. Witness's competent person certification lends credibility to her observations of violations.

### 6. **INJURIES**

**MSJ Claim:** 
Only references "injuries" generally.

**Supporting Documents:**
- **Police Report:** "injuries to his left leg, lower back, and left wrist" (Injuries section)
- **Medical Records:** Comminuted left tibial fracture; Colles' fracture left wrist; L4-L5 disc bulge with paraspinal muscle strain; multiple contusions. (ED Notes)
- **Witness Statement:** "left leg... visibly deformed" (paragraph 8)

**Discrepancy:** No direct contradiction, but MSJ minimizes injury severity while medical records document significant fractures requiring surgery.
- **Severity:** LOW (factually consistent but characterized differently)
- **Confidence:** 100%
- **Legal Significance:** While not contradictory, the MSJ's minimization versus medical documentation of serious injuries could affect damages arguments.

### 7. **STATUTE OF LIMITATIONS**

**MSJ Claim:**
> "The incident giving rise to this action occurred on March 14, 2021. Rivera did not file his complaint until March 10, 2023 — one year and 362 days after the incident." (Argument D)

**Recalculation with Correct Date:**
- Correct incident date: March 12, 2021
- Filing date: March 10, 2023
- Actual elapsed time: **1 year, 363 days** (or 2 years minus 2 days)

**Discrepancy:** MSJ uses incorrect date, affecting elapsed time calculation.
- **Severity:** MEDIUM (mathematical error but still within 2-year window)
- **Confidence:** 100%
- **Legal Significance:** Error doesn't change outcome (still within 2 years) but demonstrates lack of attention to basic facts. However, MSJ's reservation to "challenge the accrual date based on Rivera's own knowledge" remains potentially valid argument.

### 8. **POST-INCIDENT ACTIONS**

**MSJ Claim:** 
*Complete omission* - No mention of post-incident actions.

**Supporting Documents:**
- **Witness Statement:** "In the days following the incident, I observed that the remaining east-side scaffolding was taken down and rebuilt from scratch with new components. The plywood base was replaced with proper concrete footings." (paragraph 11)

**Discrepancy:** MSJ omits evidence that Harmon completely rebuilt scaffolding after incident.
- **Severity:** MEDIUM (potentially admissible as subsequent remedial measures)
- **Confidence:** 100%
- **Legal Significance:** Under Evidence Code §1151, subsequent remedial measures may not prove negligence but may be admissible for other purposes (ownership, control, feasibility). Demonstrates Harmon recognized deficiencies in original scaffolding.

## LEGAL IMPLICATIONS

1. **Privette Doctrine Defense at Risk:** Evidence that Harmon's foreman directed specific work and overrode safety concerns could establish the "retained control" exception to Privette non-liability.

2. **Presumption of Reasonable Care Rebutted:** Documented scaffolding defects and witness observations of OSHA violations likely rebut the presumption from claimed OSHA compliance.

3. **Assumption of Risk Weakened:** Rivera's documented use of proper PPE undermines argument that he assumed enhanced risks through safety non-compliance.

4. **Potential Affirmative Negligence:** Harmon's knowledge of defects (via witness complaints) and failure to remedy could create direct liability beyond Privette.

5. **Credibility Issues:** Multiple factual inaccuracies in MSJ (date, PPE, OSHA claims) undermine factual assertions and legal arguments.

**RECOMMENDATION:** The Motion for Summary Judgment contains sufficient material factual disputes to deny summary judgment, particularly regarding control, knowledge of defects, and Rivera's PPE compliance. The Privette defense appears materially contested.
