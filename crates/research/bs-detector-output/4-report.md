## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
The Motion for Summary Judgment (MSJ) contains **critical and sanctionable misrepresentations of law** and **material factual discrepancies**. The most severe finding is a fabricated, absolute quote inserted into the landmark *Privette* opinion, which materially alters the rebuttable presumption standard. Factually, the MSJ incorrectly claims the plaintiff was not wearing PPE and omits evidence that Harmon's foreman actively directed work and overrode safety concerns regarding defective scaffolding. These issues directly undermine the motion's core legal defenses and create multiple triable issues of material fact.

## 5. JUDICIAL MEMO
The Motion for Summary Judgment rests on a fundamental misstatement of California law and material factual disputes. Counsel has fabricated an absolute rule from *Privette v. Superior Court* by inserting the word "never," contradicting its established rebuttable presumption standard. Factually, the motion claims the plaintiff was not wearing PPE, directly contradicted by the police report confirming his harness failed when the anchor point collapsed. Furthermore, the moving party omits evidence that its own foreman directed the work and dismissed safety complaints about defective scaffolding, creating a triable issue on the "retained control" exception to *Privette*. These issues are dispositive to the motion's core arguments.
