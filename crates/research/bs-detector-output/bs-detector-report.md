### 2. **PPE/SAFETY EQUIPMENT**

**MSJ Claim:**
> "Rivera was not wearing required personal protective equipment at the time of the incident, including fall-arrest equipment mandated by site safety protocols." (Undisputed Fact 4)

**Supporting Documents:**
- **Police Report:** "Rivera was wearing a hard hat and harness consistent with site requirements at the time of the collapse. - **Witness Statement:** "Carlos was wearing his hard hat, safety harness, and high-visibility vest, consistent with site PPE requirements." (paragraph 7)

**Discrepancy:** MSJ claims Rivera wasn't wearing PPE; police report and witness statement confirm he was wearing full PPE including harness.
- **Severity:** CRITICAL (goes to core negligence defense and assumption of risk argument)
- **Confidence:** 100%
- **Legal Significance:** If Rivera was wearing proper PPE as documented, the "assumption of risk" and contributory negligence arguments are substantially weakened. The harness failure due to anchor point collapse suggests equipment inadequacy rather than PPE non-compliance.

### 3. **WHO DIRECTED THE WORK**

**MSJ Claim (Implied):**
> "Apex Staffing Solutions — not Harmon — was the employer responsible for scaffolding operations" (Argument A)
> "independent contractor — not the hirer — controls the manner and method of work performance" (Argument A)

**Supporting Documents:**
- **Police Report:** "Donner stated that he had directed Rivera and his crew to begin work on the east-side scaffolding section earlier that morning." (Witness Information section)
- **Witness Statement:** "Ray Donner — the project foreman for Harmon Construction — told us that the east-side scaffolding section needed to be fully operational by end of day... Donner told me, 'We don't have time to re-do the base. It's been fine. Just get up there and get it done.'" (paragraphs 3, 5)

**Discrepancy:** MSJ suggests Harmon had no control; documents show Harmon's foreman actively directed work and overrode safety concerns.
- **Severity:** CRITICAL (directly undermines Privette doctrine defense)
- **Confidence:** 100%
- **Legal Significance:** Under California law, the Privette doctrine does not apply if the hirer retains control over safety conditions or actively directs the manner of work. Hooker v. Department of Transportation, 27 Cal.4th 198 (2002). Donner's direction to work on a specific timeline and dismissal of safety concerns could establish retained control exception.
