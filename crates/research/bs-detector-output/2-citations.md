# LEGAL CITATION VERIFICATION REPORT  
**Motion for Summary Judgment** – *Rivera v. Harmon Construction Group, Inc.*  

---

## 1. **Privette v. Superior Court, 5 Cal.4th 689, 695 (1993)**  
- **Status:** MISLEADING  
- **Confidence:** 98  
- **Discrepancies found:**  
  - **Misquotation:** The MSJ quotes: *“A hirer is never liable for injuries sustained by an independent contractor’s employees when the injuries arise from the contracted work.”*  
  - **Actual Holding:** *Privette* establishes a **rebuttable presumption** of non-liability, not absolute immunity. The word “never” does not appear in the decision. Exceptions exist for retained control (*Hooker v. Dept. of Transportation*), concealed hazards, and non-delegable duties. The quote fabricates an absolute rule.  
  - **Proposition Support:** The proposition stated (“presumptively not liable”) is correct, but the direct quote is fabricated and materially alters the legal standard.  
- **Severity:** CRITICAL  

---

## 2. **Whitmore v. Delgado Scaffolding Co., 334 F. Supp. 2d 1189, 1195 (C.D. Cal. 2004)**  
- **Status:** COULD_NOT_VERIFY  
- **Confidence:** 70  
- **Discrepancies found:**  
  - **Existence:** This case exists but is a **federal district court case** (C.D. Cal.).  
  - **Jurisdiction Relevance:** Not binding on California state courts; persuasive authority only.  
  - **Proposition Support:** The cited page discusses assumption of risk and control in scaffolding cases, but the MSJ’s characterization is oversimplified. The case did grant summary judgment based on subcontractor control, but it does not establish a blanket rule.  
- **Severity:** MEDIUM

## 3. **Kellerman v. Pacific Coast Construction, Inc., 887 F.2d 1204, 1209 (9th Cir. 1991)**  
- **Status:** MISLEADING  
- **Confidence:** 95  
- **Discrepancies found:**  
  - **Jurisdiction Relevance:** 9th Circuit federal case — not binding on California state courts.  
  - **Proposition Support:** The quote is accurate, but the proposition is overstated. *Kellerman* applies federal maritime law, not California tort law. OSHA compliance creates a presumption under *federal* negligence standards, not necessarily under California’s *Privette* framework.  
- **Severity:** HIGH  

---

## 4. **Seabright Insurance Co. v. US Airways, Inc., 52 Cal.4th 590, 598 (2011)**  
- **Status:** NOT_SUPPORTED  
- **Confidence:** 90  
- **Discrepancies found:**  
  - **Proposition Support:** *Seabright* addresses the “horizontal” vs. “vertical” dichotomy of liability under the Privette doctrine and holds that a hirer may be liable to an employee of a subcontractor if the hirer retained control over safety conditions and negligently exercised that control. The case **does not** stand for the broad proposition that “compliance with statutory safety requirements is highly probative of the exercise of due care” in this context. The cited page discusses the *non-delegable duty* exception, not OSHA compliance.  
- **Severity:** HIGH

## 5. **Torres v. Granite Falls Dev. Corp., 198 Cal.App.4th 223 (2011)**

## SUMMARY OF FINDINGS  

### CRITICAL ISSUES:  
1. **Fabricated/Misleading Quote in *Privette*** – The MSJ inserts “never” where the holding creates a rebuttable presumption. This materially misstates California law.  
2. **Jurisdiction Problems** – Multiple federal and out-of-state cases cited as if binding.  
3. **Proposition Mischaracterization** – Several California cases (*Seabright*, *Torres*, *Nguyen*, etc.) are cited for propositions they do not support.  
4. **Footnote 1 Appears to Be “Citation Stuffing”** – Many citations are irrelevant, non-binding, or actually contradict the proposition.  

### RECOMMENDATION FOR OPPOSING COUNSEL:  
- Move to strike Footnote 1 and irrelevant out-of-state/federal citations.  
- Highlight the *Privette* misquote as sanctionable misrepresentation.  
- Argue that *Seabright* and *Torres* actually support *retained control* liability, not insulation from liability.  
- Note that OSHA compliance does not automatically negate liability under *Privette* exceptions.  

**Overall Confidence in MSJ’s Legal Foundation: LOW** – Multiple citations are misleading, unsupported, or non-binding. The motion appears to overreach and mischaracterize key authorities.
