# Neurotrophic Effects of Methylcobalamin B12 + Methylfolate: Cognitive and Neuroprotective Benefits

## Executive Summary

Based on comprehensive review of 2018-2024 literature, methylcobalamin (active B12) combined with methylfolate (active folate) demonstrates significant neurotrophic and neuroprotective effects through multiple mechanisms. The combination is particularly effective for individuals with MTHFR polymorphisms, hyperhomocysteinemia, or documented deficiencies.

## 1. Clinical Evidence for Cognitive/Neuroprotective Benefits

### **Systematic Reviews & Meta-Analyses**
- **Vitamin B12 Deficiency and Cognitive Function** [Strong]
  - Systematic review (2023) found B12 deficiency associated with cognitive impairment, dementia risk, and neurological dysfunction (Alruwaili et al., 2023)
  - Evidence strongest for short-term cognitive improvement in deficient populations

- **Oral vs Intramuscular B12** [Strong]
  - Cochrane review (2018) found oral B12 (including methylcobalamin) equally effective as IM injections for correcting deficiency (Wang et al., 2018)
  - Network meta-analysis (2024) confirmed no significant differences between IM, oral, and sublingual routes (Abdelwahab et al., 2024)

### **Randomized Controlled Trials**
- **CerefolinNAC® Study** [Moderate]
  - Combination of L-methylfolate, methylcobalamin, and N-acetylcysteine in hyperhomocysteinemic AD patients
  - **4.25x slower hippocampal atrophy** and **11.2x slower cortical atrophy** vs controls (Shankle et al., 2016)
  - Forebrain parenchymal atrophy slowed proportionally to homocysteine reduction

- **Diabetic Neuropathy Trial** [Moderate]
  - 1-year RCT: 1mg oral methylcobalamin improved neurophysiological parameters, pain scores, and quality of life (Didangelos et al., 2021)

### **Expert Consensus & Guidelines**
- **EFSA Upper Limits** [Strong]
  - 2023 EFSA opinion maintains 1000μg/day UL for folic acid, emphasizing methylfolate safety profile
  - No evidence linking folate to cognitive impairment in B12-sufficient individuals

- **International Expert Consensus** [Strong]
  - 2024 Delphi consensus recommends clinical symptoms as primary diagnostic criteria for B12 deficiency
  - High-dose oral B12 effective for long-term management (Obeid et al., 2024)

  - Reduces oxidative stress in mitochondrial membranes

- **Neurotransmitter Systems**

  - Myelin sheath maintenance and repair
