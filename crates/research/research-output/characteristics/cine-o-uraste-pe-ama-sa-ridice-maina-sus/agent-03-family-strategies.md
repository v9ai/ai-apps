# **Family-Based Strategies for Addressing School-Based Social Conflicts and Relational Aggression**

## **Understanding the Context**
Based on the description, Bogdan is engaging in **relational aggression** - attempting to turn classmates against Ama through negative comments and social manipulation. This behavior requires a multi-systemic approach involving family, school, and individual interventions.

## **1. Parent Management Training (PMT) and PCIT Evidence**

### **Evidence-Based Parent Training Programs:**

**A. Incredible Years Program (IY)**
- **Effectiveness**: Strong evidence for reducing conduct problems and improving social skills
- **Core Components**:
  1. **Child-directed play** to build positive parent-child relationships
  2. **Praise and rewards** for prosocial behavior
  3. **Effective limit-setting** with consistent consequences
  4. **Problem-solving skills** for conflict resolution

**B. Parent-Child Interaction Therapy (PCIT)**
- **Two-phase approach**:
  1. **Child-Directed Interaction (CDI)**: Parents learn PRIDE skills:
     - **P**raise appropriate behavior
     - **R**eflect appropriate speech
     - **I**mitate appropriate play
     - **D**escribe appropriate behavior
     - **E**nthusiasm
  2. **Parent-Directed Interaction (PDI)**: Parents learn effective commands and consistent consequences

**C. Triple P (Positive Parenting Program)**
- **Five levels** of intervention intensity
- Focuses on **promoting competence**, **preventing problems**, and **building relationships**

### **Practical Implementation Steps:**

1. **Weekly 1-hour parent coaching sessions** for 8-12 weeks
2. **In-session practice** with therapist coaching
3. **Home practice assignments** with specific goals
4. **Progress monitoring** using standardized measures

## **4. Communication Strategies and De-escalation Techniques**

### **Effective Communication Skills:**

**A. Active Listening Techniques:**
1. **Reflective listening**: "It sounds like you're feeling frustrated"
2. **Validating emotions**: "I can understand why that would upset you"
3. **Open-ended questions**: "What happened next?" instead of "Why did you do that?"

**B. "I-Statements" Formula:**
- "I feel ______ when ______ because ______"
- Example: "I feel concerned when I hear about excluding others because everyone deserves to feel included"

**C. Problem-Solving Conversations:**
1. **Define the problem** objectively
2. **Brainstorm solutions** together
3. **Evaluate options** (pros and cons)
4. **Choose and implement** a solution
5. **Review and adjust** as needed

### **De-escalation Strategies:**

**When Emotions Are High:**
1. **Pause and breathe**: Teach 4-7-8 breathing (inhale 4, hold 7, exhale 8)
2. **Take space**: "Let's both take 5 minutes to calm down"
3. **Use calming tools**: Stress balls, weighted blankets, calming music
4. **Physical movement**: Walk, stretch, or do jumping jacks

**Specific Scripts for Social Conflicts:**
- **When hearing negative comments**: "I notice you're saying unkind things about Ama. Let's talk about what's really bothering you."
- **When excluding others**: "I see you're leaving someone out. How would you feel if that happened to you?"
- **When seeking allies**: "Trying to get others to dislike someone isn't solving the problem. Let's find a better way."
