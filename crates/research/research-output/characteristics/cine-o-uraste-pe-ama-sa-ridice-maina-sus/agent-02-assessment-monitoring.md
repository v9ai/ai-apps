# Validated Assessment Tools for Peer Conflict and Social-Emotional Functioning

## 1. Standardized Instruments with Strong Psychometric Properties

### **Strengths and Difficulties Questionnaire (SDQ)**
- **Purpose**: Screens for emotional symptoms, conduct problems, hyperactivity/inattention, peer relationship problems, and prosocial behavior
- **Versions**: Parent, teacher, and self-report (for ages 11+)
- **Psychometric Properties**:
  - **Reliability**: Internal consistency α = .73-.82 across subscales; test-retest reliability r = .62-.85
  - **Validity**: Good convergent validity with CBCL (r = .60-.80), discriminant validity established
  - **Norms**: Available for multiple countries and age groups (4-17 years)
- **Administration**: 5-10 minutes
- **Use**: Ideal for baseline assessment and tracking changes over time

### **Child Behavior Checklist (CBCL) and Teacher Report Form (TRF)**
- **Purpose**: Comprehensive assessment of behavioral and emotional problems
- **Psychometric Properties**:
  - **Reliability**: Internal consistency α = .78-.97; test-retest reliability r = .82-.95
  - **Validity**: Extensive validation across clinical and community samples
- **Includes**: Aggressive behavior, social problems, attention problems scales
- **Administration**: 15-20 minutes

### **Social Skills Improvement System (SSIS) Rating Scales**
- **Purpose**: Assesses social skills, problem behaviors, and academic competence
- **Psychometric Properties**:
  - **Reliability**: Internal consistency α = .80-.95; test-retest reliability r = .70-.90
  - **Validity**: Strong criterion-related validity with other social-emotional measures
- **Includes**: Specific scales for bullying, relational aggression

## 2. Brief Screening Tools for Repeated Caregiver Measurement

### **Pediatric Symptom Checklist (PSC)**
- **Purpose**: Brief psychosocial screen for children and adolescents
- **Psychometric Properties**:
  - **Reliability**: Internal consistency α = .89-.91; test-retest reliability r = .84-.86
  - **Validity**: Sensitivity 80-95%, specificity 68-100%
- **Administration**: 5 minutes
- **Use**: Weekly/monthly monitoring

### **Behavior and Emotional Screening System (BESS)**
- **Purpose**: Universal screening for behavioral and emotional risk
- **Psychometric Properties**:
  - **Reliability**: Internal consistency α = .90-.95
  - **Validity**: Strong predictive validity for later problems
- **Administration**: 5 minutes
- **Versions**: Teacher, parent, student

### **Daily Behavior Report Cards (DBRCs)**
- **Purpose**: Individualized rating of specific target behaviors
- **Features**: Customizable to specific concerns (e.g., "used kind words," "included others")
- **Use**: Daily tracking by teachers/parents
- **Psychometric Support**: Strong evidence for treatment monitoring

## 3. Behavioral Coding Systems and Observation Protocols

### **Systematic Observation of Social Behavior**
- **Direct Observation Coding Systems**:
  1. **Playground Observation of Peer Aggression (POPA)**: Codes physical, verbal, relational aggression
  2. **Peer Interaction Coding System (PICS)**: Observes social initiations, responses, conflict resolution
  3. **Classroom Observation of Social Behavior**: Structured observation of peer interactions

### **Structured Peer Assessment Methods**
- **Peer Nomination Procedures**: Identify social status, acceptance, rejection
- **Sociometric Ratings**: Measure peer liking, disliking, perceived popularity
- **Psychometric Considerations**: Require adequate participation rates, protect confidentiality

### **Video-Based Coding Systems**
- **Observation of Human-Animal Interaction for Research (OHAIRE)**: Adaptable for peer interactions
- **Reliability**: Inter-rater reliability κ = .70-.85 for trained coders
- **Use**: Can capture subtle relational aggression behaviors

## 5. Measurement-Based Care Frameworks for Tracking Treatment Response

### **Routine Outcome Monitoring (ROM) Framework**
- **Core Components**:
  1. **Regular assessment** (weekly/biweekly)
  2. **Feedback to clinicians** and families
  3. **Clinical decision support** based on progress
  4. **Adjustment of interventions** when not progressing

### **Progress Monitoring Protocols**
1. **Baseline Assessment**: Comprehensive evaluation using multiple informants
2. **Goal Setting**: Specific, measurable goals (e.g., "reduce exclusionary comments by 50%")
3. **Frequent Measurement**: Weekly ratings of target behaviors
4. **Data-Based Decision Making**: Modify interventions based on progress data

### **Implementation Strategies for Schools**
- **Multi-Tiered Systems of Support (MTSS)**: Universal screening, targeted interventions
- **Data Teams**: Regular review of progress monitoring data
- **Family Engagement**: Sharing data with families, collaborative goal setting
