# **Neuropharmacology Analysis: EPA/DHA/DPA (800mg daily) for Cognitive and Neuroprotective Effects**

## **Executive Summary**

EPA/DHA/DPA at 800mg daily demonstrates **moderate evidence** for cognitive and neuroprotective benefits through multiple anti-inflammatory mechanisms. The evidence is strongest for mood regulation and neuroprotection in at-risk populations, with emerging support for cognitive enhancement in healthy aging.

## **1. Clinical Evidence for Cognitive/Neuroprotective Benefits**

### **Meta-Analyses & Systematic Reviews (2018-2026)**

**[Strong] Evidence Strength**
- **2019 Meta-Analysis** (Liao et al., 2019): Analysis of 26 RCTs found omega-3 PUFAs significantly improved depression symptoms (SMD = -0.28, 95% CI: -0.47 to -0.09), with EPA showing greater efficacy than DHA alone
- **2018 Cochrane Review** (Abdelhamid et al., 2018): Most extensive systematic assessment showing omega-3 reduces coronary heart disease mortality and events, with implications for vascular cognitive impairment
- **2024 Systematic Review** (Serefko et al., 2024): EPA and DHA demonstrate antidepressant effects through modulation of neuroinflammation, neurotransmitter function, and neuroplasticity

**[Moderate] Evidence Strength**
- **2019 Review** (Simonetto et al., 2019): Omega-3 PUFAs exert anti-inflammatory properties that may prevent vascular cognitive impairment and dementia
- **2021 Review** (Sambra et al., 2021): DHA and arachidonic acid have relevant roles in neuroprotection against neurodegenerative pathologies throughout the life cycle

**[Limited] Evidence Strength**
- **2012 Cochrane Review** (Sydenham et al., 2012): No benefit of omega-3 supplementation on cognitive function in cognitively healthy older people, but noted need for longer-duration studies
- **2020 Review** (Madore et al., 2020): Maternal omega-3 deficiency impairs microglial phagocytosis of synaptic elements in developing mouse brains

## **2. Mechanisms of Action Relevant to Brain Health**

### **Primary Mechanism: Anti-Inflammatory Effects**

**Neuroinflammation Modulation:**
- **Microglial Regulation**: Omega-3 PUFAs reduce pro-inflammatory cytokine production (TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-6) and promote anti-inflammatory mediators
- **Resolvin Formation**: EPA and DHA serve as precursors for specialized pro-resolving mediators (SPMs) including resolvins, protectins, and maresins that actively resolve inflammation
- **Membrane Fluidity**: Incorporation into neuronal membranes alters lipid raft composition, affecting receptor signaling and neurotransmitter release

**Oxidative Stress Reduction:**
- **Antioxidant Properties**: DHA protects against lipid peroxidation in neuronal membranes
- **Mitochondrial Function**: Improves mitochondrial efficiency and reduces reactive oxygen species production
- **Nrf2 Pathway Activation**: Enhances endogenous antioxidant defense systems

**Synaptic Plasticity & Neurotransmitter Systems:**
- **BDNF Enhancement**: Increases brain-derived neurotrophic factor expression
- **Neurotransmitter Modulation**: Affects serotonin, dopamine, and glutamate systems
- **Synaptic Pruning**: Regulates microglial-mediated synaptic refinement during development and aging

**Blood-Brain Barrier Integrity:**
- **Endothelial Function**: Improves cerebral blood flow and reduces neurovascular inflammation
- **APOE Modulation**: Genotype-dependent effects on oxylipin response to supplementation
