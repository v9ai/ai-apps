- **Expected Outcome**: Enhanced energy metabolism, reduced oxidative stress, improved cellular repair

**EPA/DHA/DPA (800mg) + EVOO (45mL) + Plasmalogens (1mL) + NAC (1800mg)**

- **Evidence**: Moderate clinical evidence for individual components; strong mechanistic synergy
- **Expected Outcome**: Reduced neuroinflammation, enhanced membrane integrity, improved redox balance

**Lithium Orotate + Lutein/Zeaxanthin + High Polyphenol Cocoa**
- **Mechanism**: Lithium enhances BDNF and GSK-3β inhibition → Carotenoids protect neuronal structure → Cocoa improves cerebral blood flow → **Multi-pathway neuroplasticity**
- **Evidence**: Emerging combination evidence; strong individual mechanistic support
- **Expected Outcome**: Enhanced synaptic plasticity, structural brain preservation, improved neurovascular coupling

## **3. INTERACTION RISKS**

### **High-Risk Interactions**

#### **1. NAC + Metformin/Acarbose**
- **Risk**: NAC may enhance hypoglycemic effects
- **Mechanism**: NAC improves insulin sensitivity + glucose-lowering drugs
- **Management**: Monitor blood glucose, consider dose adjustment

#### **2. Lithium Orotate + NSAIDs/Diuretics**
- **Risk**: Reduced lithium clearance, potential toxicity
- **Mechanism**: NSAIDs reduce renal lithium excretion
- **Management**: Avoid concurrent NSAID use, monitor renal function

#### **3. Multiple Blood-Thinning Effects**
- **Agents**: Omega-3s, EVOO, Cocoa, NAC
- **Risk**: Additive antiplatelet effects
- **Management**: Monitor bleeding risk, especially with anticoagulants

#### **4. Gastrointestinal Load**
- **Agents**: Acarbose, Metformin, NAC, Omega-3s
- **Risk**: Cumulative GI distress
- **Management**: Stagger initiation, take with food, consider digestive enzymes

### **Moderate-Risk Interactions**

#### **1. Vitamin B12 Depletion**
- **Agents**: Metformin (primary), NAC (secondary)
- **Risk**: Long-term B12 deficiency
- **Management**: Supplement B12, regular monitoring

#### **2. Iron Absorption Interference**
- **Agents**: Cocoa polyphenols, NAC
- **Risk**: Reduced non-heme iron absorption
- **Management**: Space from iron-rich meals/supplements
