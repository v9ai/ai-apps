## **3. Interaction Risks**

### **Critical Interactions Requiring Management**

1. **Zinc-Copper-Iron Triad**
   - **Risk:** Competitive absorption leading to deficiencies
   - **Mechanism:** High zinc (>50 mg/day) induces metallothionein, reducing copper absorption
   - **Iron and zinc** compete for DMT1 transporter
   - **Management:** 
     - Maintain 10-15:1 zinc:copper ratio
     - Separate iron and zinc by 3-4 hours
     - Monitor serum levels of all three minerals

2. **Calcium-Magnesium-Zinc Competition**
   - **Risk:** Reduced absorption of all minerals
   - **Management:** Separate calcium supplements from zinc/magnesium by 2-3 hours

3. **Thyroid Medication Interactions**
   - **Risk:** Reduced absorption of thyroid medications
   - **Components:** Iron, calcium, magnesium, selenium
   - **Management:** Take thyroid medications 4+ hours apart from all supplements

4. **Finasteride Neurosteroid Effects**
   - **Risk:** Reduced allopregnanolone → depression/anxiety/cognitive effects
   - **Management:** Topical route reduces but doesn't eliminate risk; monitor mood/cognition

5. **Berberine Drug Interactions**
   - **Risk:** CYP3A4 substrate interactions with statins, immunosuppressants
   - **Management:** Monitor drug levels if on concurrent medications

### **Beneficial Interactions to Leverage**
1. **Vitamin C + Iron**: Enhanced iron absorption
2. **Fat-soluble vitamins + dietary fat**: Improved absorption of D3, K2, omega-3
3. **B vitamins + methylation support**: Enhanced homocysteine metabolism
