# Master Author Landscape Report
## March 19–26, 2026

### Executive Summary
This week’s AI research landscape is characterized by **democratized innovation**, with independent researchers producing nearly a third of all papers, and a notable rise in **formal AI co-authorship**. Academic institutions—particularly in Asia—lead in applied domains like vision and robotics, while North American and European labs focus on safety and multimodal systems. Collaboration patterns show strong cross-disciplinary ties, especially between AI and chemistry, cybersecurity, and social sciences. A significant geographic shift is evident, with Asian institutions dominating output in applied AI research.

## Synthesis & Strategic Insights

1. **Democratization of AI Research** – Independent and young researchers are driving LLM innovation and safety discourse, often publishing via open repositories (Zenodo, arXiv).

2. **AI as Co-Author** – A paradigm shift is underway: AI systems (Claude-358 Opus 4.6, GitHub Copilot) are listed as formal co-authors, raising questions about authorship norms and intellectual contribution.

3. **Security & Safety Ascendancy** – Nearly 25% of papers address agent security, alignment auditing, or mechanistic interpretability, signaling a maturation toward deployment-ready, robust AI.

4. **Cross-Disciplinary Fusion** – Strong integration with chemistry (molecular AI), cybersecurity (GNNs for attack detection), and social sciences (political propaganda analysis) indicates AI’s expanding role as a tool for domain science.

5. **Geographic Rebalancing** – Asian institutions now lead in applied AI output, while North America retains strength in foundational safety. Europe holds a strong position in robotics and multimodal systems.

6. **Industry Lag** – The near-absence of major industry labs (Google, OpenAI, Meta) suggests either a publication-cycle lag or a strategic pivot toward internal, product-focused R&D.

### Recommended Monitoring Priorities:
- **Independent researcher output** on Zenodo and arXiv for early signals of disruptive ideas.
- **Asian academic preprints** in vision, robotics, and NLP for applied innovation.
- **Safety and security proceedings** (NeurIPS, ICLR, security conferences) for governance and robustness trends.
- **Cross-disciplinary journals** (e.g., Nature AI, Science Robotics) for fusion research.
