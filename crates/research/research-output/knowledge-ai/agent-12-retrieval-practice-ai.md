# Retrieval Practice Enhanced by AI: A Comprehensive Landscape Review (2018-2026)

## Executive Summary

This review synthesizes research on retrieval practice and the testing effect enhanced by artificial intelligence, covering cognitive mechanisms, AI-generated practice questions, adaptive quizzing systems, feedback timing, and domain differences. The integration of AI with retrieval practice represents a transformative opportunity for personalized learning, with applications spanning STEM and humanities education.

## 1. Testing Effect: Mechanisms and Boundary Conditions

### 1.1 Cognitive Mechanisms
The testing effect (retrieval practice) operates through multiple cognitive mechanisms:

**1.1.1 Elaborative Processing Theory**
- Retrieval practice promotes deeper semantic processing and elaboration of memory traces
- **Li et al. (2024)** demonstrated through EEG studies that making judgments of learning (JOLs) reactively enhances memory by increasing learning engagement and inducing elaborative processing

**1.1.2 Transfer-Appropriate Processing**
- Practice tests improve performance on final tests when they require similar cognitive processes
- **Giannakopoulos et al. (2024)** found that lower constraint testing enhances the testing effect for some contextual details but not others

**1.1.3 Consolidation and Reconsolidation**
- Retrieval strengthens memory traces and makes them more resistant to interference
- **Roelle and Richter (2025)** reviewed how retrieval practice, distributed practice, and successive relearning promote sustainable learning through consolidation mechanisms

### 1.2 Boundary Conditions
Recent research has identified important boundary conditions:

**1.2.1 Feedback Timing**
- **Mera et al. (2025)** explored the impact of feedback and final test timing on the pretesting effect, finding optimal timing depends on retention intervals
- Immediate feedback may be beneficial for short-term retention, while delayed feedback can enhance long-term retention

**1.2.2 Question Format Effects**
- **van Wijk et al. (2024)** compared retrieval practice using very short answer questions and multiple choice questions, finding format-dependent effects on learning outcomes
- Higher constraint questions (short answer) may produce stronger testing effects than lower constraint questions (multiple choice)

**1.2.3 Metacognitive Factors**
- **Akbulut (2024)** found that graded practice tests outperformed ungraded practice, suggesting motivational and metacognitive factors influence the testing effect
- Students' awareness of test importance and grading criteria affects engagement with retrieval practice

## 2. AI-Generated Practice Questions: Quality and Difficulty Calibration

### 2.1 Quality Assessment of AI-Generated Questions
**2.1.1 Content Accuracy and Relevance**
- **O'Malley et al. (2024)** investigated AI-generated exam questions in medical education, finding that while AI can generate plausible questions, expert validation remains essential
- **Zakareya et al. (2024)** compared discrimination indices of AI-generated vs. human-generated multiple-choice questions, finding comparable quality when properly prompted

**2.1.2 Cognitive Complexity Alignment**

### 2.2 Difficulty Calibration Methods

- **Edelsbrunner et al. (2025)** conducted a meta-analysis on domain-specific knowledge tests, highlighting challenges in maintaining appropriate difficulty across learning stages

**2.2.2 Learner-Model Based Calibration**
- AI systems that adapt question difficulty based on individual learner performance and knowledge state
- **Ningsih et al. (2026)** developed an AI-powered adaptive quizzing system that adjusts question difficulty in real-time based on student performance

### 2.3 Quality Control Frameworks

- **Arries (2024)** evaluated student satisfaction with AI-generated multiple choice questions in medical education, finding positive reception when combined with expert oversight

**2.3.2 Automated Quality Metrics**
- Development of automated metrics for assessing question quality, including clarity, discrimination, and cognitive alignment
- **Juričić and Obrvan (2024)** studied students' perception of AI-generated exam questions, identifying key quality indicators from learner perspectives
