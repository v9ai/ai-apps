# AI-Supported Collaborative Learning: Research Landscape (2018-2026)

## Executive Summary

This research landscape analysis examines AI-supported collaborative learning across five key domains: (1) intelligent group formation algorithms, (2) AI moderation of group discussions, (3) collaborative knowledge construction, (4) peer tutoring orchestration, and (5) CSCL with AI agents. The analysis synthesizes findings from 2018-2026, building on foundational self-regulated learning research while exploring emerging AI applications in collaborative learning contexts.

## 1. Intelligent Group Formation Algorithms

### 1.1 Algorithmic Approaches to Optimal Team Composition

**Key Methods and Algorithms:**
- **Knowledge Map-based Genetic Algorithms**: Flanagan et al. (2021) proposed explainable group formation using knowledge maps and genetic algorithms to optimize team composition based on learning analytics data
- **Learning Analytics-driven Grouping**: Lechuga & Doroudi (2022) introduced three algorithms leveraging adaptive intelligent tutoring system data for classroom group formation
- **Multi-criteria Optimization**: Algorithms considering learning styles, knowledge levels, personality traits, and social compatibility
- **Explainable AI Approaches**: Increasing focus on transparent group formation decisions to build trust and understanding

**Optimization Criteria:**
- **Heterogeneous vs. Homogeneous Grouping**: Balancing diversity with shared learning objectives
- **Complementary Skill Sets**: Matching students with different strengths and weaknesses
- **Social Compatibility**: Considering interpersonal dynamics and communication styles
- **Learning Goal Alignment**: Aligning group composition with specific learning objectives

**Key Reference**: **Flanagan et al. (2021)** [Towards Explainable Group Formation by Knowledge Map based Genetic Algorithm](https://doi.org/10.1109/icalt52272.2021.00119)

### 1.2 Data Sources for Group Formation

- **Learning Management System (LMS) Data**: Activity logs, assessment results, participation patterns
- **Intelligent Tutoring System Data**: Problem-solving strategies, help-seeking behaviors
- **Social Network Analysis**: Communication patterns, collaboration histories
- **Multimodal Learning Analytics**: Combining behavioral, cognitive, and affective data
