# AI for Open Educational Resources (OER): A Comprehensive Research Landscape (2018-2026)

## Executive Summary

This research survey examines AI applications specifically focused on Open Educational Resources (OER) across five key domains: (1) automatic OER search, curation, and quality assessment, (2) content alignment to learning standards and competencies, (3) remix and adaptation of OER with AI assistance, (4) accessibility checking and improvement of OER, and (5) copyright and licensing—AI-assisted license compatibility checking. The analysis builds upon prior foundational research in LLMs in education and human-AI collaborative learning, providing a specialized focus on OER-specific AI applications.

## 1. Automatic OER Search, Curation, and Quality Assessment

### 1.1 Semantic Web and AI-Powered Discovery Systems

**Ilkou et al. (2021)** [EduCOR: An Educational and Career-Oriented Recommendation Ontology](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-030-88361-4_32). This foundational work introduces an educational, career-oriented ontology that provides a foundation for representing online learning resources for personalized learning systems. The ontology enables learning material repositories to offer learning resources that are annotated with metadata about required and taught skills, knowledge areas, and learning outcomes.

**Evenstein Sigalov & Nachmias (2023)** [Investigating the potential of the semantic web for education: Exploring Wikidata as a learning platform](https://doi.org/10.1007/s10639-023-11664-1). This research explores how semantic web technologies, particularly Wikidata, can serve as platforms for educational resource discovery and curation, demonstrating AI applications in structured educational data.

**Kerres & Buntins (2020)** [Recommender in AI-enhanced Learning: An Assessment from the Perspective of Instructional Design](https://doi.org/10.1515/edu-2020-0119). Analyzes how AI-enhanced recommender systems support learners in finding materials and sequencing learning paths, with specific applications to OER discovery.

### 1.2 Quality Assessment Frameworks

**Nurbekova et al. (2023)** [A Decision-Making Platform for Educational Content Assessment Within a Stakeholder-Driven Digital Educational Ecosystem](https://doi.org/10.3991/ijep.v13i7.41689). Proposes a digital decision-making platform for evaluating educational content quality through a three-stage quality evaluation process considering content, metadata, and pedagogical alignment.

**Stracke & Trisolini (2021)** [A Systematic Literature Review on the Quality of MOOCs](https://doi.org/10.3390/su13115817). While focused on MOOCs, this systematic review provides frameworks and methodologies that can be adapted for OER quality assessment, including AI-based approaches.

### 1.3 AI-Enhanced Metadata and Tagging

**Vuorikari (2009)** [Tags and self-organisation: a metadata ecology for learning resources in a multilingual context](https://doi.org/10.3991/ijep.v13i7.41689). Foundational work on social tagging and metadata ecology that informs current AI approaches to automated metadata generation and organization for OER.

## 2. Content Alignment to Learning Standards and Competencies

### 2.1 Standards Mapping and Competency Alignment

**Darvishi et al. (2022)** [Incorporating Training, Self-monitoring and AI-Assistance to Improve Peer Feedback Quality](https://doi.org/10.1145/3491140.3528265). While focused on peer feedback, this research demonstrates AI approaches to aligning educational content with quality standards and assessment criteria.

**Parker et al. (2021)** [Uses, Revisions, and the Future of Validated Assessments in Computing Education: A Case Study of the FCS1 and SCS1](https://doi.org/10.1145/3446871.3469744). Provides methodologies for aligning educational resources with validated assessments, offering frameworks that can be adapted for AI-driven standards alignment.

### 2.2 Curriculum Mapping and Learning Path Design

**Bucchiarone et al. (2024)** [Designing and Generating Lesson Plans combining Open Educational Content and Generative AI](https://doi.org/10.1145/3652620.3687773). Demonstrates AI approaches to aligning OER with specific learning objectives and curriculum requirements through automated lesson plan generation.

## 3. Remix and Adaptation of OER with AI Assistance

### 3.1 Generative AI for OER Adaptation

**Bozkurt (2023)** [Generative AI, Synthetic Contents, Open Educational Resources (OER), and Open Educational Practices (OEP): A New Front in the Openness Landscape](https://doi.org/10.55982/openpraxis.15.3.579). This seminal paper examines the transformation of the educational landscape through the integration of generative AI with OER, exploring the shift from organic (human-created) to synthetic (AI-created) content.

**Balaji et al. (2025)** [Teacher in the Loop AI (TiL-AI): A Strategy for Empowering Educators in Developing Countries through OER Adaptation](https://doi.org/10.56059/jl4d.v12i2.1934). Presents a practical framework for using generative AI to adapt OER to fit national and local curricula, ensuring relevance and cultural resonance while maintaining teacher oversight.

**Tila & Levy (2023)** [Curating OER Content through AI and ChatGPT](http://dx.doi.org/10.4236/jss.2023.1112035). Investigates the potential for generative AI to curate and adapt OER content, providing case studies and implementation frameworks.

### 3.2 Automated Content Remixing and Personalization

**Bucchiarone et al. (2024)** [Unlocking Education Together: A Meta-model Emphasizing Usability and Reuse of Open Educational Resources](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-031-60221-4_45). Proposes a meta-model for OER reuse and remixing that incorporates AI capabilities for content adaptation and personalization.

## 4. Accessibility Checking and Improvement of OER

### 4.1 AI-Powered Accessibility Assessment

**Rampelt et al. (2025)** [How Do AI Educators Use Open Educational Resources? A Cross-Sectoral Case Study on OER for AI Education](https://doi.org/10.55982/openpraxis.17.1.766). While focused on AI education, this study provides insights into how AI tools can assess and improve the accessibility of educational resources.

**Tucker & Charette (2025)** [Elements for Excellence in the Design of OER Textbooks: User Research, Expert Peer Reviews, and Accessibility Testing](https://doi.org/10.55982/openpraxis.17.4.930). Discusses rigorous stages of OER development including accessibility testing, with implications for AI-assisted accessibility assessment.

### 4.2 Universal Design for Learning (UDL) Integration

**Henshaw (2025)** [Multimedia Learning and UDL in UK Higher Education](https://doi.org/10.56397/jare.2025.03.03). Examines the integration of multimedia learning and Universal Design for Learning principles, with applications for AI-driven accessibility improvements in OER.

**Heggart & Fatayer (2024)** [Designing Learning Experiences for Inclusivity and Diversity](https://doi.org/10.5130/aal). Provides frameworks for creating inclusive learning experiences that can inform AI approaches to accessibility checking and improvement.

## 5. Copyright and Licensing: AI-Assisted License Compatibility Checking

### 5.1 Intellectual Property Management for OER

**Funk et al. (2025)** [Ten quick tips for navigating intellectual property in FAIR educational resources](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pcbi.1013208). Provides practical guidance for creating FAIR-compliant OER while respecting intellectual property rights, with implications for AI-assisted license checking.

**Nascimbeni & Burgos (2019)** [Unveiling the Relationship between the Use of Open Educational Resources and the Adoption of Open Teaching Practices in Higher Education](https://doi.org/10.3390/su11205637). Examines the relationship between OER use and open teaching practices, including considerations of licensing and intellectual property.

### 5.2 License Compatibility and Compliance

**Bozkurt (2024)** [GenAI et al.: Cocreation, Authorship, Ownership, Academic Ethics and Integrity in a Time of Generative AI](https://doi.org/10.55982/openpraxis.16.1.654). Critically examines issues of authorship, ownership, and academic ethics in the context of generative AI and OER, including licensing considerations.

## 6. Emerging Trends and Future Directions

### 6.1 Integration of Generative AI with OER Ecosystems

**Panke (2024)** [Open Educational Resources and Artificial Intelligence for Future Open Education](https://doi.org/10.25159/2663-659x/15106). Explores the intersection of OER and AI with an emphasis on open pedagogy applications, providing a forward-looking perspective on AI-OER integration.

**McNulty et al. (2025)** [Open Educational Resources and Generative AI: A Practical Approach to OER Development](https://stars.library.ucf.edu/teachwithai/2025/friday/3). Presents practical approaches to integrating generative AI into OER development processes using the ADDIE model as a framework.

### 6.2 Ethical Considerations and Equity

**Gabriel (2024)** [Generative AI and Educational (In)Equity](https://doi.org/10.34190/icair.4.1.3153). Examines the complex relationship between generative AI and educational equity, analyzing both opportunities and challenges in OER contexts.

**Bulathwela et al. (2024)** [Artificial Intelligence Alone Will Not Democratise Education: On Educational Inequality, Techno-Solutionism and Inclusive Tools](https://doi.org/10.3390/su16020781). Provides critical perspectives on the limitations of AI in democratizing education, with important implications for OER development and deployment.

### 6.3 Teacher-AI Collaboration Models

**van den Berg & Du Plessis (2023)** [ChatGPT and Generative AI: Possibilities for Its Contribution to Lesson Planning, Critical Thinking and Openness in Teacher Education](https://doi.org/10.3390/educsci13100998). Examines how generative AI tools can contribute to lesson planning and openness in education, providing models for teacher-AI collaboration in OER development.

## 7. Methodological Approaches and Technical Frameworks

### 7.1 Technical Architectures

- **Semantic Web Technologies**: Ontologies, linked data, and knowledge graphs for OER discovery and alignment
- **Natural Language Processing**: Text analysis for content quality assessment and standards alignment
- **Computer Vision**: Image and multimedia analysis for accessibility checking
- **Machine Learning Models**: Recommendation systems, classification algorithms, and predictive analytics

### 7.2 Evaluation Metrics
- **Quality Metrics**: Accuracy, completeness, pedagogical soundness, alignment with standards
- **Accessibility Metrics**: Compliance with WCAG guidelines, usability for diverse learners
- **Licensing Metrics**: License compatibility, attribution accuracy, compliance with open licenses
- **Adaptation Metrics**: Cultural relevance, language appropriateness, contextual alignment

## 8. Key Challenges and Research Gaps

### 8.1 Technical Challenges
- **Metadata Quality**: Inconsistent or incomplete metadata in existing OER repositories
- **Scalability**: Handling large volumes of diverse OER across multiple languages and formats
- **Interoperability**: Integration with existing educational systems and standards
- **Accuracy**: Ensuring reliable AI assessments of quality, accessibility, and licensing

### 8.2 Pedagogical Challenges
- **Contextual Adaptation**: Maintaining pedagogical effectiveness while adapting OER for different contexts
- **Cultural Relevance**: Ensuring AI adaptations respect cultural differences and local educational practices
- **Teacher Agency**: Balancing automation with teacher control and professional judgment

### 8.3 Ethical and Legal Challenges
- **Intellectual Property**: Navigating complex copyright and licensing landscapes
- **Bias and Fairness**: Addressing algorithmic bias in OER recommendations and adaptations
- **Transparency**: Making AI processes and decisions understandable to educators and learners
- **Data Privacy**: Protecting user data in OER discovery and adaptation systems

## 9. Future Research Directions

1. **Longitudinal Studies**: Extended investigations of AI impact on OER adoption and effectiveness
2. **Cross-Cultural Applications**: Adaptation of AI-OER systems across diverse educational and cultural contexts
3. **Integration Frameworks**: Development of standardized approaches for AI-OER integration
4. **Teacher Professional Development**: Research on effective training for AI literacy in OER contexts
5. **Policy Development**: Studies informing educational AI policy specific to OER development and use

## 10. Conclusion

The integration of AI with Open Educational Resources represents a transformative opportunity to enhance the discovery, adaptation, and effectiveness of openly licensed educational materials. While significant progress has been made in areas such as semantic web technologies for OER discovery, generative AI for content adaptation, and AI-assisted accessibility checking, substantial challenges remain regarding metadata quality, pedagogical effectiveness, and ethical considerations. The successful implementation of AI-OER systems requires careful attention to complementary strengths of human educators and AI systems, development of appropriate technical standards and frameworks, and ongoing research into effective integration models that respect educational values and promote equitable access to quality learning resources.
