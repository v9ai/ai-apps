## **4. Build vs Buy Analysis for Key Capabilities**

### **4.1 Decision Framework**

**Build Considerations:**
- **Core Competency**: AI/ML capabilities central to competitive advantage
- **Customization Needs**: Highly specific pedagogical requirements
- **Data Control**: Sensitive student data requiring on-premise solutions
- **Long-term Cost**: Lower TCO over 5+ year horizon with sufficient scale
- **Integration Complexity**: Deep integration with existing systems

**Buy Considerations:**
- **Time to Market**: Need for rapid deployment (3-6 months)
- **Expertise Gap**: Lack of in-house AI/ML talent
- **Maintenance Burden**: Ongoing model updates and infrastructure management
- **Regulatory Compliance**: Pre-certified solutions for data privacy
- **Best Practices**: Access to proven pedagogical approaches

### **4.2 Capability-Specific Recommendations**

**Knowledge Tracing & Assessment:**
- **Build If**: Large-scale platform (>1M users), proprietary assessment methodology
- **Buy If**: Standardized testing needs, rapid deployment required
- **Recommended Vendors**: ALEKS (McGraw-Hill), Knewton Alta, Edmentum

**Personalized Content Delivery:**
- **Build If**: Unique content library, specific adaptive algorithms needed
- **Buy If**: Content-agnostic delivery platform sufficient
- **Recommended Vendors**: Area9 Lyceum, Smart Sparrow, Fishtree

**Learning Analytics & Reporting:**
- **Build If**: Institutional research capabilities, custom metrics required
- **Buy If**: Standard analytics needs, integration with existing SIS/LMS
- **Recommended Vendors**: Civitas Learning, BrightBytes, Hoonuit

**AI-Powered Tutoring:**
- **Build If**: Domain-specific expertise, large user base for training data
- **Buy If**: General tutoring needs, limited AI development resources
- **Recommended Vendors**: Carnegie Learning, Squirrel AI, Third Space Learning

### **4.3 Hybrid Approach: Build Core, Buy Edge**

**Recommended Strategy for Mid-Sized Institutions:**
1. **Build Core Platform**: Custom learning experience layer and data infrastructure
2. **Buy Specialized Components**: Assessment engines, content libraries, analytics tools
3. **Integrate via APIs**: Microservices architecture with vendor-agnostic interfaces
4. **Maintain Data Sovereignty**: Central data lake with controlled vendor access

**Technology Stack Considerations:**
- **Cloud Infrastructure**: AWS Educate, Google Cloud for Education, Azure for Education
- **ML Operations**: MLflow, Kubeflow, Seldon Core for model management
- **Data Privacy**: Differential privacy, federated learning, homomorphic encryption
- **Integration Standards**: LTI Advantage, Caliper Analytics, xAPI
