# AI Tutoring for Mathematics and Science: A Comprehensive Research Landscape (2018-2026)

This research survey examines AI tutoring applications specifically for mathematics and science education, focusing on five key areas: (1) math problem solving with step-by-step reasoning and symbolic computation, (2) science simulation and inquiry-based learning, (3) misconception detection and remediation, (4) visual and diagrammatic reasoning, and (5) production systems including Photomath, Wolfram Alpha, Khanmigo, and ALEKS. ## 1. Mathematics Problem Solving: Step-by-Step Reasoning and Symbolic Computation

### 1.1 Cognitive Foundations and Expert Models

**Kenneth R. Koedinger et al. ### 1.2 Modern AI Approaches to Mathematical Reasoning

**Shayan Doroudi (2022)** [The Intertwined Histories of Artificial Intelligence and Education](https://doi.org/10.1007/s40593-022-00313-2). This historical analysis (105 citations) traces the development of AI in mathematics education, highlighting the evolution from rule-based systems to modern machine learning approaches.

### 1.3 Symbolic Computation and Automated Reasoning

## 11. Conclusion

The landscape of AI tutoring for mathematics and science has evolved significantly from 2018-2026, building upon foundational ITS research and accelerated by advances in machine learning and large language models. Key developments include:

1. **Advanced Mathematical Reasoning**: Integration of symbolic computation with neural approaches for step-by-step problem solving
2. **Immersive Science Simulations**: Development of virtual and augmented reality environments for inquiry-based learning
3. **Sophisticated Misconception Detection**: Machine learning approaches for identifying and remediating student errors
