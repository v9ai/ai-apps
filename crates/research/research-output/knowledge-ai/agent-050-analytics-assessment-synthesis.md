# Analytics & Assessment Intelligence: Comprehensive Synthesis Report (2018-2026)

## 1. Predictive Analytics: What Works and Fairness Challenges

**Deep Learning Advancements**: **Sukrit Leelaluk et al. (2025)** [Knowledge Distillation in RNN-Attention Models for Early Prediction of Student Performance](https://doi.org/10.1145/3672608.3707805) applies knowledge distillation techniques to RNN-Attention models, enabling more efficient and accurate early prediction while maintaining model interpretability.

**Feature Engineering Innovations**: **Khalid Benabbes et al. (2022)** [New Automatic Hybrid Approach for Tracking Learner Comprehension Progress in the LMS](https://doi.org/10.3991/ijim.v16i19.33733) introduces unsupervised clustering approaches to automatically identify learning styles based on LMS interaction data, analyzing traces from 920 learners across three agronomy courses.

### 1.2 Fairness and Bias Mitigation

**Comprehensive Fairness Frameworks**: **Godfrey Perfectson Oise (2025)** [Fairness and Bias Mitigation in Student Success Prediction Models](https://doi.org/10.21203/rs.3.rs-7899983/v1) proposes a fairness-aware predictive framework integrating bias detection, mitigation, and interpretability, evaluating five ML algorithms on 5,000 student records.

**Policy Gradient Methods**: **Ingrid Solheim and Marco De Santis (2026)** [Fairness-Aware Policy Gradient Methods for Mitigating Demographic Bias in AI-Driven Student Performance Prediction](https://doi.org/10.71465/ajeet3552) introduces a novel fairness-aware policy gradient framework integrating demographic parity constraints into reinforcement learning algorithms.

## 2. Automated Scoring: Essay, Code, Competency Assessment State-of-the-Art

### 2.1 Transformer-Based Essay Scoring

**BERT-Based Approaches**: **Yongjie Wang et al. **Dual-Scale BERT Models**: **Minsoo Cho et al. **LLM-Based Scoring**: **Xiaoyi Tang et al. ### 2.2 Trait-Based and Rubric-Aligned Scoring

**Multi-Trait Assessment**: **Rahul Kumar et al. (2022)** [Many Hands Make Light Work: Using Essay Traits to Automatically Score Essays](https://doi.org/10.18653/v1/2022.naacl-main.106) demonstrates how essay traits can be leveraged for automated scoring, providing granular feedback.

**Rubric-Based Approaches**: **Haneul Yoo et al. (2024)** [DREsS: Dataset for Rubric-based Essay Scoring on EFL Writing](https://doi.org/10.18653/v1/2025.acl-long.659) releases a large-scale dataset (48.9K samples) for rubric-based automated essay scoring.

**Discourse Coherence Assessment**: **Ben Naismith et al. (2023)** [Automated evaluation of written discourse coherence using GPT-4](https://doi.org/10.18653/v1/2023.bea-1.32) explores how LLMs can assess discourse coherence, a writing quality characteristic difficult to identify automatically.

### 2.3 Cross-Prompt Generalization

**Domain Generalization**: **Zhiwei Jiang et al. **Contrastive Learning**: **Yuan Chen and Xia Li (2023)** [PMAES: Prompt-mapping Contrastive Learning for Cross-prompt Automated Essay Scoring](https://doi.org/10.18653/v1/2023.acl-long.83) introduces prompt-mapping contrastive learning to address discrepancies between different prompts.

### 2.4 Code and Competency Assessment

**Programming Assessment**: **Zhenyu Xu & Victor S. Sheng (2024)** [Detecting AI-Generated Code Assignments Using Perplexity of Large Language Models](https://doi.org/10.1609/aaai.v38i21.30361) develops specialized detectors for AI-generated code in programming education.

## 3. Item Generation: Quality and Viability for Production Use

**Programming Assessment Applications**: **Halim Teguh Saputro et al. ### 3.3 Distractor Generation and Difficulty Estimation

**Systematic Distractor Generation**: **Mark J. Gierl, Hollis Lai, and Vasily Tanygin (2021)** [Distractor Generation](https://doi.org/10.4324/9781003025634-6) provides comprehensive methods for generating plausible distractors maintaining cognitive alignment.

**Item Difficulty Modeling**: **Esther Ulitzsch et al. (2025)** [Using Item Parameter Predictions for Reducing Calibration Sample Requirements—A Case Study Based on a High‑Stakes Admission Test](https://doi.org/10.1111/jedm.12426) presents a two-step approach blending IDM with Bayesian estimation techniques.

### 3.4 Production Viability and Quality Assurance

**Expert Evaluation**: The ReQUESTA framework shows superior distractor quality through hybrid orchestration, with expert evaluations indicating stronger alignment with central concepts and superior linguistic consistency.

**Psychometric Validation**: Generated items from advanced frameworks show consistent psychometric properties, with items being more challenging, more discriminative, and more strongly aligned with overall performance compared to baseline approaches.
