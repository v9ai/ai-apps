# Landscape Survey: Building Energy Performance Prediction & Real Estate AI Applications

## **1. Data-Driven Building Energy Consumption Models**

### **Regression & Statistical Models**
- **Multiple Linear Regression (MLR)**: Baseline for energy consumption prediction
- **Support Vector Regression (SVR)**: Effective for non-linear relationships
- **Gaussian Process Regression (GPR)**: Provides uncertainty quantification
- **Time Series Models**: ARIMA, SARIMA for temporal patterns

### **Neural Network Approaches**
- **Feedforward Neural Networks (FNN)**: For static building characteristics
- **Recurrent Neural Networks (RNN/LSTM)**: Temporal sequence modeling
- **Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN)**: Spatial feature extraction
- **Transformer Models**: Attention mechanisms for long sequences
- **Graph Neural Networks (GNN)**: Building system relationships

### **Ensemble & Hybrid Methods**
- **Random Forests & Gradient Boosting**: Feature importance analysis
- **Stacking & Blending**: Multiple model combination
- **Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs)**: Integration of domain knowledge

## **5. Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) for Energy Modeling**

### **PINN Architecture for Buildings**
- **Governing Equation Integration**: Heat transfer, mass balance equations
- **Boundary Condition Enforcement**: Physical constraints in loss functions
- **Data Assimilation**: Combining sparse measurements with physics
- **Uncertainty Quantification**: Bayesian PINNs for confidence intervals

### **Applications in Building Energy**
- **Reduced-Order Modeling**: Fast surrogate models for complex simulations
- **Inverse Problems**: Parameter estimation from limited data
- **Digital Twin Development**: Real-time building state estimation
- **Control Optimization**: Physics-constrained control strategies

### **Advantages Over Traditional Methods**
- **Data Efficiency**: Requires fewer training samples
- **Generalization**: Better performance on unseen conditions
- **Interpretability**: Physical meaning in model parameters
- **Robustness**: Less prone to overfitting
