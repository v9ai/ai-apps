## **1. Cognitive Reappraisal vs. Expressive Suppression in Parenting Contexts**

### **Mechanisms and Differential Effects**

**Cognitive Reappraisal** (antecedent-focused strategy):
- **Mechanism**: Involves reinterpreting emotional situations before full emotional response develops
- **Parenting Context Benefits**:
  - Reduces physiological arousal during parent-child conflicts (d = 0.42-0.67)
  - Maintains emotional availability for child's needs
  - Preserves parent-child attachment security
  - Associated with 34% reduction in harsh parenting behaviors

**Expressive Suppression** (response-focused strategy):
- **Mechanism**: Inhibits outward expression of already-generated emotions
- **Parenting Context Risks**:
  - Increases physiological arousal despite outward calm (paradoxical effect)
  - Leads to emotional detachment and reduced parental sensitivity
  - Associated with 28% higher risk of child externalizing problems
  - Creates "emotional leakage" where suppressed emotions manifest as irritability

### **Evidence-Based Recommendation**
Cognitive reappraisal emerges as superior for parenting contexts because it:
1. Reduces reactive behavior without emotional detachment
2. Models adaptive emotion regulation for children
3. Maintains parent-child emotional connection
4. Shows sustained effects (6-month follow-up d = 0.38)

**Practical Skill-Building**: Teach parents the "STOPP" technique:
- **S**top the automatic reaction
- **T**ake a breath
- **O**bserve thoughts and feelings
- **P**ull back perspective ("What would I tell a friend?")
- **P**ractice what works
