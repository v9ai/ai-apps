90% of beautifully engineered internal developer platforms sit virtually unused.

The 2024 data is clear: the primary failure mode isn't technical, it's social. Low adoption kills platforms. This happens when central teams design for an idealized org chart, fighting against Conway's Law instead of using it as a blueprint.

Conway’s Law states an organization’s communication structure will be mirrored in its systems. A platform that ignores this—designed for teams that don’t exist—creates immediate friction. Developers reject the "Ferrari" because they needed a reliable "bicycle with a basket."

Successful platforms don't dictate from an ivory tower; they serve their users.
- Shift from a "systems architect" to a "product team" mindset.
- Map your *actual* team boundaries first; that's your real architecture diagram.
- Codify the "golden paths" your best teams already use, don't lay new ones from scratch.

The goal isn't to redesign your organization. It's to amplify it.
