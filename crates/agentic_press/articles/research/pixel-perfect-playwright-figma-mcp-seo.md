## Search Intent

The dominant intent is **informational with a practical edge**. Searchers are senior frontend engineers who have heard the hype around Figma MCP and AI-assisted design-to-code workflows, and want an honest, experience-based assessment of what actually works. They are past the "what is MCP" stage and want to know: does this produce production-quality UI? What breaks? What workarounds exist? The secondary audience is developers evaluating whether to adopt Playwright-based visual testing as a design fidelity gate in CI/CD pipelines.

## Differentiation Strategy

1. **Honest, opinionated angle**: Most Figma MCP content is vendor-promoted or optimistic tutorials. This piece leads with "what breaks" and earns trust before explaining what works. Senior engineers respond to honesty over hype.

2. **Concrete measurement layer**: No competing article pairs Figma MCP with headless Playwright as a quantitative design fidelity gate. This is a novel angle -- using Playwright not just for testing but as a measurement instrument for design accuracy.

3. **Specific stack (Radix Themes)**: Targeting a real design system (Radix Themes + its Figma kit) instead of generic Tailwind examples. This serves a specific audience and captures long-tail searches.

4. **TypeScript code examples**: Full, runnable TypeScript snippets for Playwright measurement scripts, not pseudocode. This makes the article a reference piece that gets bookmarked and linked.

5. **MCP protocol-level critique**: Going deeper than "it's cool" into actual protocol limitations (session-less, no discovery, token walls). This positions the author as technically sophisticated and attracts backlinks from the MCP developer community.
