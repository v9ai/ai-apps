# SEO Strategy: Verification Gate

## Target Keywords
| Keyword | Monthly Volume | Difficulty | Intent | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| AI code verification | est. medium | medium | informational | P1 |
| autonomous testing AI agent | est. medium | medium | informational | P1 |
| AI change validation | est. low | low | informational | P2 |
| AI quality gate autonomous | est. low | low | informational | P2 |
| why AI needs verification | est. low | low | informational | P3 |

## Search Intent
Informational — engineers worried about AI-generated code quality want to know how to verify agent output. The contrast angle ("says no") is memorable because most AI content focuses on generation, not rejection.

## Competitive Landscape
| Rank | Title | Domain | Format | Word Count | Gap |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | DORA Report: 91% increase in review time | google.com | report | ~3000 | Problem statement, no solution |
| 2 | Strategies for Testing AI Systems | techwell.com | training | ~1000 | Human-centered, not agent-to-agent |
| 3 | Agents At Work 2026 Playbook | promptengineering.org | guide | ~4000 | General, no verification-specific depth |

## Recommended Structure
- **Format**: Contrast/tension piece — generation vs verification
- **Word count**: 1500-2000
- **Title tag**: "The Agent That Says No: Why Verification Beats Generation"
- **Meta description**: "An autonomous improvement system without verification is autonomous damage. Meet the agent that gates every change with confidence scores, counterfactual analysis, and build checks."
- **H1**: The Agent That Says No: Why Verification Beats Generation
- **H2s**:
  1. Generation Is Easy. Verification Is Hard. — why AI output quality depends on rejection
  2. Five Papers on Calibrated Self-Awareness — Agentic Uncertainty, TrajAD, Confidence Calibration, LUMINA, Determinism-Faithfulness
  3. The Five Verification Checks — coherence, cross-skill, convention, regression, build
  4. Counterfactual Analysis — what if this change were not made?
  5. The Four Verdicts — ACCEPT, WARN, REJECT, PARTIAL

## Internal Linking Opportunities
- Code Improver article → the Verification Gate validates its output
- Skill Evolver article → also validated by the Verification Gate
- Meta-Optimizer article → consumes verification results

## Differentiation Strategy
The "agent that says no" framing is unique. Every competitor talks about AI generation; almost none talk about AI verification. Google's DORA data (91% increase in code review time) validates the problem. Our solution (confidence-scored, counterfactual-aware) is specific and implementable.

## Distribution Notes
- HackerNews: "verification beats generation" is a contrarian take worth debating
- Twitter/X: the DORA stat + our approach = strong thread
- Dev.to: the four verdicts system is concretely useful
