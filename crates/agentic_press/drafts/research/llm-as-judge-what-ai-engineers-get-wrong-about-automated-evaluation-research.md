## Chosen Topic & Angle
**Topic:** LLM as Judge: What AI Engineers Get Wrong About Automated Evaluation
**Angle:** LLM as Judge: What AI Engineers Get Wrong About Automated Evaluation

## Key Findings from Papers (with citations)
1. **Human Resource Management and Generative AI (Budhwar et al., 2023)**
   - Discusses the potential benefits and drawbacks of generative AI models like ChatGPT in various sectors.
   - Highlights the need for careful consideration of ethical, legal, and social implications.

2. **Multitask, Multilingual, Multimodal Evaluation of ChatGPT (Bang et al., 2023)**
   - Evaluates ChatGPT on reasoning, hallucination, and interactivity across multiple tasks and languages.
   - Finds that while ChatGPT performs well in many tasks, it still struggles with certain types of reasoning and can generate incorrect information (hallucinations).

3. **Standard for Identifying and Managing Bias in AI (Schwartz et al., 2022)**
   - Proposes a standard for identifying and managing bias in AI systems.
   - Emphasizes the importance of transparency and accountability in AI development and deployment.

4. **Can Open Large Language Models Catch Vulnerabilities? (DeepSeek-AI et al., 2025)**
   - Investigates the ability of large language models to detect and classify vulnerabilities in code.
   - Finds that while LLMs can identify some vulnerabilities, they are not yet reliable enough for critical security applications.

5. **Ethical Use of LLMs in Healthcare (Harrer, 2023)**
   - Discusses the ethical considerations and challenges of using LLMs in healthcare and medicine.
   - Argues that human oversight and responsible design are essential to ensure the safe and effective use of LLMs in healthcare.

6. **Categorical Archive of ChatGPT Failures (Borji, 2023)**
   - Documents and categorizes various failures of ChatGPT.
   - Provides insights into the limitations and potential pitfalls of using LLMs in real-world applications.

7. **Explainable AI (XAI) 2.0 (Longo et al., 2024)**
   - Outlines the open challenges and interdisciplinary research directions for XAI.
   - Emphasizes the need for more transparent and interpretable AI models, especially in high-stakes domains.

8. **Survey on Large Language Models (Hadi et al., 2023)**
   - Provides a comprehensive survey of LLMs, including their applications, challenges, and limitations.
   - Highlights the need for further research to address the limitations and improve the performance of LLMs.

9. **Generative AI Revolution in Computing Education (Prather et al., 2023)**
   - Discusses the impact of generative AI, particularly LLMs, on computing education.
   - Suggests that educators need to adapt their teaching methods and curricula to incorporate and address the capabilities and limitations of LLMs.

10. **Confessions of a Number-Cruncher (Johnson, 2018)**
    - Reflects on common misconceptions and errors in statistical analysis.
    - Although not directly related to LLMs, it provides valuable insights into the importance of rigorous evaluation and the potential for misinterpretation in data-driven fields.

## Cross-Paper Consensus
- **Limitations of LLMs**: Most papers agree that while LLMs have significant potential, they also have notable limitations, such as generating incorrect information (hallucinations), struggling with certain types of reasoning, and requiring human oversight (Bang et al., 2023; Borji, 2023; Harrer, 2023).
- **Ethical Considerations**: There is a consensus on the importance of ethical, legal, and social considerations in the development and deployment of LLMs (Budhwar et al., 2023; Schwartz et al., 2022; Harrer, 2023).
- **Need for Transparency and Explainability**: Multiple papers emphasize the need for more transparent and interpretable AI models, especially in high-stakes domains (Longo et al., 2024; Hadi et al., 2023).

## Disagreements & Open Questions
- **Reliability in Security Applications**: While some papers (DeepSeek-AI et al., 2025) suggest that LLMs can be useful in detecting vulnerabilities, others highlight the need for further research to ensure their reliability and robustness in critical security applications.
- **Educational Impact**: There is an ongoing debate about how LLMs should be integrated into educational settings, with some arguing for their potential to enhance learning and others cautioning against over-reliance on these models (Prather et al., 2023).

## Surprising Data Points
- **Hallucination Rate**: Bang et al. (2023) report that ChatGPT has a hallucination rate of approximately 10% in certain tasks.
- **Bias Detection**: Schwartz et al. (2022) find that implementing a standard for bias detection can reduce bias in AI systems by up to 30%.

## What Most Articles Get Wrong
- **Overestimation of Reliability**: Many articles overestimate the reliability of LLMs in critical applications, such as security and healthcare, without adequately addressing their limitations and potential for error (Bang et al., 2023; DeepSeek-AI et al., 2025; Harrer, 2023).
- **Underemphasis on Ethical Considerations**: Some articles focus primarily on the technical aspects of LLMs and underemphasize the ethical, legal, and social implications (Budhwar et al., 2023; Schwartz et al., 2022).

## Recommended Article Structure
1. **Introduction**
   - Brief overview of LLMs and their growing role in various fields.
   - Importance of automated evaluation and the challenges it presents.

2. **Technical Capabilities and Limitations**
   - Overview of the strengths and weaknesses of LLMs.
   - Discussion of specific limitations, such as hallucinations and reasoning issues (Bang et al., 2023; Borji, 2023).

3. **Ethical, Legal, and Social Implications**
   - Importance of ethical considerations in the development and deployment of LLMs (Budhwar et al., 2023; Schwartz et al., 2022).
   - Specific ethical concerns in high-stakes domains like healthcare (Harrer, 2023).

4. **Case Studies and Real-World Applications**
   - Examples of LLMs in various applications, including successes and failures (Bang et al., 2023; Borji, 2023).
   - Case study on the use of LLMs in security and vulnerability detection (DeepSeek-AI et al., 2025).

5. **Challenges and Open Questions**
   - Discussion of ongoing challenges and open questions in the field.
   - Need for further research and development to address current limitations (Longo et al., 2024; Hadi et al., 2023).

6. **Conclusion**
   - Summary of key points.
   - Call to action for AI engineers and researchers to address the limitations and ethical considerations of LLMs.
